## Summary

Hanahan's 2022 perspective in *Cancer Discovery* proposes four new additions to the Hallmarks of Cancer framework, building on the 2000 and 2011 formulations (Hanahan & Weinberg). The paper argues that:

1. **Unlocking phenotypic plasticity** should be elevated to a full hallmark capability (the ninth), as the ability of cancer cells to disrupt normal differentiation programs — via dedifferentiation, blocked differentiation, or transdifferentiation — is broadly operative across cancer types and mechanistically separable from the eight established core hallmarks.
2. **Nonmutational epigenetic reprogramming** should be recognized as a second enabling characteristic alongside genome instability and mutation, reflecting that purely epigenetic (non-DNA-sequence) changes can reprogram hallmark acquisition.
3. **Polymorphic microbiomes** (variable microbial communities in barrier tissues and within tumors) constitute a second additional enabling characteristic, capable of both promoting and antagonizing hallmark capabilities.
4. **Senescent cells** of diverse origins within the TME should be added to the roster of functionally important non-cancer cell types, alongside immune cells, fibroblasts, and endothelial cells, because their SASP can convey hallmark capabilities to neighboring cancer cells.

## Key Contributions

### 1. Unlocking Phenotypic Plasticity (New Hallmark Capability)

Cancer cells acquire the ability to disrupt the normal differentiation programs of their cells of origin, manifested in three overlapping modes:

- **Dedifferentiation**: Mature or partially differentiated cells revert to progenitor-like states. Examples include colon cancer (loss of HOXA5 and SMAD4 differentiation suppressors), melanoma (MITF downregulation with reactivation of neural crest progenitor genes), and pancreatic islet cell neoplasias.
- **Blocked differentiation**: Developing progenitor cells are prevented from completing terminal differentiation, locking them in a proliferative progenitor state. The canonical example is acute promyelocytic leukemia (APL), where PML-RARα fusion blocks granulocyte differentiation; retinoic acid therapy reverses this, providing proof-of-concept for differentiation therapy.
- **Transdifferentiation**: Cancer cells switch to an entirely different cell lineage identity. Examples include pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (acinar-to-ductal transdifferentiation driven by SOX9 upregulation and PTF1a/MIST1 loss), Barrett's esophagus (squamous-to-columnar epithelium), and lung cancer lineage plasticity (SCLC/NSCLC interconversion).

Metabolic modulators of differentiation state are also highlighted: alpha-ketoglutarate (αKG) produced downstream of p53 maintains differentiated cell states, while IDH1/2 mutations producing the oncometabolite D-2-hydroxyglutarate (D-2HG) inhibit αKG-dependent dioxygenases and freeze cells in progenitor states via epigenetic lock-in.

Phenotypic plasticity is argued to be a **discrete hallmark** because: (i) it has regulatory mechanisms separable from core oncogenic drivers; (ii) it is operative in multiple cancer types; (iii) it generates transcriptomic and histologic heterogeneity that facilitates malignant progression and therapy resistance.

### 2. Nonmutational Epigenetic Reprogramming (New Enabling Characteristic)

The tumor microenvironment's physical and chemical properties (hypoxia, ECM stiffness, paracrine signals) can reprogram cancer cell epigenomes in ways that enable hallmark capabilities, independent of DNA mutations:

- **Hypoxia** reduces TET demethylase activity, causing hypermethylation. In posterior fossa ependymoma — a pediatric tumor characterized by a near-total absence of recurrent driver mutations — a hypoxia-induced epigenetic program appears to drive tumorigenesis.
- **EMT circuitry**: ZEB1 induces the histone methyltransferase SETD1B in a positive feedback loop, maintaining the invasive EMT state epigenetically. SNAIL1 upregulation similarly remodels the chromatin landscape to maintain mesenchymal phenotypes.
- **Epigenetic heterogeneity**: Linker histone H1.0 is dynamically and heterogeneously repressed in cancer cell subpopulations, creating epigenetically distinct stem-like cells with altered accessibility to megabase-sized chromatin domains.
- **Stromal cell reprogramming**: Accessory cells in the TME (CAFs, innate immune cells, endothelial cells, pericytes) are epigenetically reprogrammed upon recruitment by soluble and physical TME factors. In a lung metastasis model, combined epigenetic therapy (5-azacytidine + HDAC inhibitor) switched infiltrating myeloid cells from immature tumor-promoting to mature tumor-antagonizing macrophage states.

### 3. Polymorphic Microbiomes (New Enabling Characteristic)

The variability of microbial communities colonizing barrier tissues among individuals can both promote and antagonize cancer hallmarks through two general mechanisms:

- **Mutagenesis of epithelial cells**: E.g., E. coli carrying the PKS locus mutagenizes the human genome.
- **Modulation of innate and adaptive immunity**: Microbiome-derived factors (inosine, SagA mucopeptides, cytokines) systemically modulate immune responses.

Key evidence:
- **Gut microbiome and colorectal cancer**: Fecal transplants from tumor-bearing mice into predisposed recipients establish cancer-promoting and cancer-protective microbiomes; butyrate-producing bacteria induce colonic epithelial senescence, promoting tumorigenesis.
- **Microbiome and immunotherapy**: Fecal microbiota transplants from immunotherapy-responsive melanoma patients restored anti-PD-1 efficacy in previously resistant patients (two independent clinical studies, 2021). Enterococcus SagA and microbiome-derived inosine modulate checkpoint inhibitor response.
- **Intratumoral microbiome**: A pan-cancer survey of 1,526 tumors across 7 cancer types found tumor type-specific intracellular bacteria within cancer and immune cells. Pancreatic cancer intratumoral microbiome promotes oncogenesis via immune suppression; germ-free or antibiotic-treated mice show impaired tumorigenesis.
- **Extragut microbiomes**: Gut dysbiosis promotes cholangiocarcinoma by enabling bacterial entry into the liver via the portal vein, triggering TLR4/CXCL1 signaling that recruits gMDSCs to suppress NK cells. Skin, lung, oral, vaginal/cervical microbiomes also implicated in their associated cancers.

### 4. Senescent Cells (New TME Cell Type)

Cellular senescence — typically irreversible proliferative arrest with activation of a SASP — is a double-edged phenomenon in cancer:

- **Tumor-suppressive role** (established): Oncogene-induced senescence limits aberrant proliferation; therapy-induced senescence limits cancer cell division.
- **Tumor-promoting role** (increasingly documented): Senescent cancer cells can escape the nonproliferative state and resume proliferation (transitory/reversible senescence). Senescent CAFs, endothelial cells, and immune cells in the TME can convey hallmark capabilities via paracrine SASP (proliferative signaling, angiogenesis induction, invasion, immune suppression).

Key evidence:
- Pharmacologic depletion of p16-INK4a-expressing senescent cells in aging mice reduced spontaneous tumorigenesis and cancer-associated death.
- Senescent CAFs in tumors are demonstrably tumor-promoting.
- Senescent fibroblasts in aging skin recruit immunosuppressive innate immune cells and suppress antitumoral CD8 T cells via SASP.
- Therapy-induced senescent endothelial cells secrete CXCL11 (increasing breast cancer aggressiveness) and facilitate metastatic spreading via sunitinib-induced senescence.

Therapeutic implications: senolytics (pharmacologic or CAR-T-based elimination of senescent cells) and SASP-reprogramming strategies are being explored.

## Methodology and Architecture

This is a **perspective/review article** with no original experimental data. Hanahan applies the following evaluative framework to justify new hallmark candidates:

1. **Independence criterion**: Is the proposed parameter discernibly, if not completely, independent from the established hallmarks and enabling characteristics?
2. **Breadth criterion**: Is there evidence of broad applicability across the spectrum of human cancers (not just one tumor type)?
3. **Mechanistic plausibility**: Are there identified molecular mechanisms by which the parameter enables hallmark acquisition?

For each of the four proposed dimensions, the paper presents illustrative case studies (2–5 cancer type examples) drawing on published experimental evidence, including mouse models, patient data, single-cell genomics, and clinical trials. The paper explicitly acknowledges that the examples are illustrative, not comprehensive, and that the proposed dimensions are "trial balloons" intended to stimulate debate and further investigation.

## Results

The paper's primary contribution is conceptual: an updated hallmarks framework. The empirical findings assembled as supporting evidence are summarized per dimension in Key Contributions above. Key quantitative highlights from cited studies include:

- 1,526-tumor pan-cancer microbiome survey revealing tumor type-specific intracellular bacteria (Nejman et al. 2020).
- p-EMT hi/lo cells interconverting to mixed populations within 4 days in culture, across 198 cell lines representing 22 cancer types (confirming epigenetic rather than genetic basis).
- Depletion of p16-INK4a+ senescent cells in aging mice: reduced spontaneous tumor incidence and cancer-associated death.
- Two independent 2021 clinical studies demonstrating fecal microbiota transplant restoring anti-PD-1 response in melanoma patients who had progressed on prior checkpoint blockade.
