## Summary

Science Review proposing a paradigm shift from animal-model-centric drug development to a human-centric framework built on three pillars: human-derived cells (iPSC, primary), microphysiological systems (organoids, organ-on-chip), and AI (generative design, foundation models, agentic AI). Highlights PDTO biobanks for cancer drug sensitivity, MCLA-158 bispecific antibody discovered in organoids, and Virtual Lab agentic AI for autonomous protein design. Covers regulatory milestones including FDA Modernization Act 2.0/3.0 and ISTAND Liver-Chip qualification.

## Key Contributions

- Diagnoses >90% preclinical failure rate as structural, not incidental
- NAMs triad: human cells + MPS + AI
- 4-step roadmap: lab-in-a-loop → organs-on-chips → clinical trial-in-a-dish → digital twins
- Cancer-specific examples: PDTO chemo-sensitivity, MCLA-158, esophageal on-chip, CAR-T on-chip evaluation

## Methodology and Architecture

Review paper synthesizing evidence from PDTO biobanks, organ-on-chip platforms, AI drug discovery (Virtual Lab, CLY-124 DrugReflector, TITAN/BiomedCLIP), and regulatory frameworks (FDA ISTAND, CiPA, OECD GIVIMP).

## Results

| Platform | Key Result |
|----------|-----------|
| PDTO biobank | In vitro chemo-sensitivity correlates with clinical outcome |
| MCLA-158 | CRC organoid-discovered EGFR x LGR5 bispecific Ab in clinical trial |
| Virtual Lab | 92 nanobodies designed → 2 validated |
| CLY-124 | 17x hit rate vs random, Phase 1 |
