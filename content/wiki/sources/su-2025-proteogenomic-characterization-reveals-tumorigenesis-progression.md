# LUAD Subsolid Nodule Proteogenomics (Su 2025)

## Summary

Nature Communications 2025 study characterizing the molecular progression of early-stage lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD) across 66 subsolid nodules using combined proteogenomics and glycoproteomics. Profiles stage-wise protein and glycoprotein changes across the AIS (adenocarcinoma in situ) to MIA (minimally invasive adenocarcinoma) to IAC (invasive adenocarcinoma) continuum, identifying candidate early detection biomarkers and documenting accompanying immune microenvironment remodeling.

## Key points

- 66 LUAD subsolid nodules profiled with WES/RNA-seq + TMT proteomics + glycoproteomics
- Stage-wise proteomic changes mapped across AIS to MIA to IAC progression
- Glycoprotein alterations are associated with the transition to invasive disease
- Early detection biomarker candidates proposed from pre-invasive stages
- Immune microenvironment remodeling accompanies tumor progression

## Project relevance

- Glycoproteomics application in early lung cancer as a methodological reference
- Stage-resolved molecular atlas useful for early-stage biomarker discovery
- Proteomic characterization of subsolid nodules complements solid tumor LUAD studies (e.g., CPTAC LUAD)

## Caveats

- Moderate cohort size (n=66); larger validation needed
- Limited to subsolid nodules; findings may not generalize to solid lung tumors
- Retrospective study design; prospective validation required
