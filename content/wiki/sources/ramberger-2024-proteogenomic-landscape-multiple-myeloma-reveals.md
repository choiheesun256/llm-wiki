# Multiple Myeloma Proteogenomics

## Summary

First comprehensive proteogenomic landscape of multiple myeloma analyzing 138 plasma cell malignancies (NDMM, PCL, MGUS, healthy) using TMT-based proteomics/phosphoproteomics, RNA-seq, and nanopore DNA sequencing. Reveals highly deregulated phosphoproteome, chromosomal alteration-defined protein subgroups, a prognostic protein signature independent of R-ISS, and genetic subgroup-specific therapeutic targets.

## Key points

- 138 plasma cell malignancies with deep proteogenomics
- Phosphoproteome highly deregulated in MM
- Chromosomal alterations drive protein-level subgroups
- Prognostic protein signature independent of R-ISS
- Genetic subgroup-specific drug targets identified

## Project relevance

- First large-scale MM proteogenomics (Nature Cancer)
- Hematologic malignancy multi-omics methodology
- Prognostic biomarker discovery from proteomics

## Caveats

- Treatment-naive only; no response data
- CD138+ sorting with variable immune contamination
- Single-center; external validation needed
