# Purine Metabolism Pan-Cancer Metabolic Dysregulation

## Summary
Molecular Cancer 2025 pan-cancer metabolomics study profiling plasma from 2,561 patients across 20 cancer types. Identifies hypoxanthine, cysteine, and pyruvic acid as universal cancer biomarkers. 33 core purine metabolism genes discovered in TCGA pan-cancer tissues. Proposes therapeutic compounds targeting purine metabolism via pharmaco-transcriptomics and pharmaco-proteomics analysis.

## Key points
- Pan-cancer plasma metabolomics (2,561 patients, 20 cancer types)
- Hypoxanthine as universal cancer biomarker
- 33 purine metabolism genes across TCGA
- Drug candidates via pharmaco-multi-omics

## Project relevance
- Cancer metabolism-immunity link
- Pan-cancer biomarker discovery methodology
- Drug repurposing via multi-omics

## Caveats
- Chinese cohorts
- Metabolomics + bioinformatics (limited functional validation)
- Biomarker validation needed in independent cohorts
