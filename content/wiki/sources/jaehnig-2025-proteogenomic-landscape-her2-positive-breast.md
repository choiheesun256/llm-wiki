# Proteogenomic Landscape of HER2+ Breast Cancer (CALGB 40601)

## Summary

Proteogenomic analysis of CALGB 40601 trial (HER2+ breast cancer, neoadjuvant trastuzumab ± lapatinib). Proteome-based subtypes outperform PAM50 in predicting pCR. GPRC5A and TPBG identified as resistance biomarkers. Phosphoproteomics reveals bypass kinase activation in non-responders. Immune-enriched subtype achieves highest pCR rate.

## Key points

- Proteome subtypes predict pCR better than PAM50 transcriptomic classification
- GPRC5A and TPBG high expression associated with treatment resistance
- Phosphoproteome identifies non-HER2 kinase bypass pathways in resistant tumors
- Immune-enriched proteome subtype has highest pCR rate

## Project relevance

- Resistance biomarkers (GPRC5A/TPBG) for HER2-targeted therapy patient stratification
- Multi-omics integration demonstrating clinical superiority over single-omic approaches
- Bypass kinase pathways inform rational combination therapy design

## Caveats

- Single trial (CALGB 40601); external validation needed
- Pre-treatment biopsies only; no longitudinal sampling
- GPRC5A/TPBG functional validation limited (correlation, not causation)
