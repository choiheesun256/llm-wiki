# Time-Resolved Multi-Omic Atlas of Mouse Stomach

## Summary

Comprehensive temporal proteome and transcriptome atlas of mouse stomach development across 15 timepoints (E12.5 to W8), quantifying 12,108 gene products. Identifies three developmental phases and connects developmentally regulated proteins to diffuse-type gastric cancer. Validates novel splicing junctions at peptide level.

## Key points

- 15-timepoint proteome + transcriptome atlas of mouse stomach
- 12,108 gene products; 3 developmental phases identified
- Development-regulated proteins overexpressed in diffuse gastric cancer
- Novel splicing junctions validated by MS

## Project relevance

- Temporal multi-omics methodology
- Development-cancer molecular connection
- RNA-protein discordance analysis

## Caveats

- Mouse model; human developmental differences expected
- Bulk tissue without cell-type resolution
- Gastric cancer link is correlative
