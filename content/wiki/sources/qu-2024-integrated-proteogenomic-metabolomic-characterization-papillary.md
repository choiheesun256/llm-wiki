# PTC Proteogenomic-Metabolomic Characterization

## Summary

Integrated proteogenomic and metabolomic characterization of 102 papillary thyroid cancer patients stratified by recurrence risk. Combines WES, RNA-seq, proteomics, phosphoproteomics, and metabolomics (503 metabolites) to define 4 molecular subtypes (CS1-CS4) with distinct clinical features. BRAF V600E (47%), MUC16 (36%), and TERT promoter mutations (14%) are prevalent, with RET/NTRK fusions enriched in high recurrence risk.

## Key points

- 102-patient PTC with recurrence risk stratification
- 4 molecular subtypes (CS1-CS4) defined
- Metabolomics integration enhances subtyping
- RET/NTRK fusions enriched in high recurrence risk
- Subtype-specific druggable targets identified

## Project relevance

- Proteogenomics + metabolomics integration
- Recurrence risk prediction methodology
- Thyroid cancer molecular subtyping

## Caveats

- Chinese cohort; low RAS mutation frequency vs TCGA
- Retrospective design
- PTC only; excludes aggressive subtypes (ATC/PDTC)
