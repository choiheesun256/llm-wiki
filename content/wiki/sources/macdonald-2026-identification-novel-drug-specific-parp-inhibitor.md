# Drug-Specific PARPi Resistance via BCRP/ABCG2

## Summary

Identifies drug-specific PARP inhibitor resistance mechanisms: niraparib resistance driven by BCRP/ABCG2 upregulation, while olaparib resistance involves P-gp/ABCB1. Paclitaxel pre-treatment induces ABCB1 only, suggesting first-line chemotherapy can compromise subsequent PARPi maintenance. ABCG2 knockdown restores niraparib sensitivity.

## Key points

- ABCG2 drives niraparib-specific resistance; ABCB1 drives olaparib resistance
- Paclitaxel pre-treatment induces ABCB1, compromising PARPi
- Resistance from transcriptional regulation of efflux transporters
- Clinical implications for treatment sequencing

## Project relevance

- Drug-specific resistance mechanisms
- Treatment sequence optimization in ovarian cancer
- Efflux transporter biology in drug resistance

## Caveats

- A2780-based; limited HGSOC representativeness
- In vitro only; no in vivo validation
- Ascites-based patient validation; tissue data lacking
