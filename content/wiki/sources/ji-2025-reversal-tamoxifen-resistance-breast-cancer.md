# N6AMT1-NEDD4L-p110α Pathway in Tamoxifen Resistance

## Summary

N6AMT1 downregulation drives tamoxifen resistance through NEDD4L loss → p110α protein stabilization → PI3K/AKT hyperactivation. FOXA1 is identified as the transcriptional activator of N6AMT1. Restoring N6AMT1 or inhibiting PI3K reverses tamoxifen resistance in vitro and in vivo. Low N6AMT1 expression correlates with poor outcomes in ER+ breast cancer patients.

## Key points

- FOXA1 → N6AMT1 → NEDD4L → p110α ubiquitination/degradation axis
- N6AMT1 loss causes p110α accumulation and PI3K/AKT hyperactivation
- N6AMT1 restoration or PI3K inhibition reverses tamoxifen resistance
- Low N6AMT1 correlates with poor clinical outcome in ER+ breast cancer

## Project relevance

- Novel upstream regulator of PI3K pathway in endocrine resistance
- Molecular rationale for tamoxifen + PI3K inhibitor combination
- N6AMT1 as a potential biomarker for endocrine therapy response

## Caveats

- Primarily MCF-7-based; generalization across ER+ cell lines limited
- Immunodeficient xenograft models; immune-related resistance factors not assessed
- Clinical validation is retrospective correlation only
