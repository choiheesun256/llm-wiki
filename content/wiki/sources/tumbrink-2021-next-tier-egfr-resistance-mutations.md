# EGFR Resistance Mutations Review (Tumbrink 2021)

## Summary

Tumbrink et al. review the structural and functional landscape of on-target resistance mutations that emerge following third-generation EGFR TKI (osimertinib) therapy in NSCLC. While C797S is the most frequent on-target resistance mutation, the review catalogs a spectrum of additional mutations including L718Q, G724S, S768I, and L792H. It highlights differences in resistance mutation spectra between first-line and second-line osimertinib use, and discusses EGFR kinase domain structural analysis (PDB: 3IKA) as a basis for next-generation TKI design.

## Key points

- C797S is the most common on-target resistance mutation; cis vs trans relationship with T790M is clinically critical.
- L718Q/V mutations emerge predominantly after third-line treatment.
- G724S is associated with EGFR exon 19 deletions.
- First-line osimertinib: on-target resistance mutations account for only ~10% of resistance mechanisms; off-target bypass dominates.
- Compound EGFR mutations are increasing in clinical prevalence.
- Data sources: FLAURA, AURA, and TREM trial data; published cfDNA resistance cohorts.

## Project relevance

- Reference for understanding TKI resistance mechanisms relevant to the drug repurposing project where EGFR-mutant NSCLC is a target cancer type.
- Structural analysis of resistance mutations provides a rationale for network-based identification of combination or bypass targets.
- cfDNA-based resistance profiling approach is relevant for liquid biopsy data integration in multi-omic analyses.

## Caveats

- Review paper; no original experimental data.
- Published in 2021; additional resistance mutations and newer-generation TKIs (e.g., fourth-generation allosteric inhibitors) have been reported since.
- cfDNA-based resistance data may carry ascertainment bias toward later-line, heavily treated patients.
