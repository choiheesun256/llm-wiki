# UBD/FAT10 Pan-Cancer Landscape (Frontiers in Oncology 2025)

## Summary
Integrative pan-cancer analysis (TCGA/GTEx/CPTAC, 33 cancer types) of UBD (FAT10), a cytokine-inducible ubiquitin-like modifier, characterizing its prognostic significance, immune infiltration correlations, and molecular pathways. UBD is upregulated in 14 cancers with diagnostic AUC >0.8 in gastrointestinal tumors. In esophageal carcinoma (ESCA), UBD promotes proliferation and migration via the TP53 pathway, experimentally validated by lentiviral overexpression and functional assays. Imatinib and TTNPB are proposed as candidate drugs via molecular docking.

## Key points
- Pan-cancer: TCGA/GTEx bulk RNA-seq + CPTAC proteomics; 7 immune deconvolution methods used
- UBD upregulated in 14 cancers; downregulated in THCA and KICH
- GI tumor diagnostic AUC >0.8; context-dependent prognostic role (protective in SKCM/SARC; poor in UVM/PAAD)
- UBD positively correlates with IFN-gamma dominant C2 immune subtype (CD8+ T cells, M1 macrophages)
- Imatinib (docking score -8.9 kcal/mol) and TTNPB identified as candidate drugs via molecular docking + MD simulation
- ESCA: lentiviral UBD overexpression → TP53 pathway activation → enhanced proliferation and migration
- Transcriptomic + western blot (p53, p21) validation in ESCA cell lines

## Project relevance
- Pan-cancer multi-omics framework (TCGA+GTEx+CPTAC) is a standard reference pipeline
- Molecular docking + MD simulation for drug candidate identification methodology
- Context-dependent prognostic roles of the same gene across cancer types highlights importance of cancer-specific analysis
- UBD's immune C2 subtype correlation provides immunotherapy response prediction clues

## Caveats
- Pan-cancer associations are correlative; cancer-specific causal mechanisms not fully established
- Functional experiments limited to ESCA cell lines; no in vivo validation
- UBD role is highly context-dependent; single mechanistic framing may oversimplify
- Molecular docking predictions require preclinical validation
