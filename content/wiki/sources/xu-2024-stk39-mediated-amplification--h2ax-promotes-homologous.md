# STK39-mediated γH2AX Amplification and PARPi Resistance

## Summary
NAR 2024 study identifying STK39 as a novel enhancer of homologous recombination repair via γH2AX amplification. After DNA damage, STK39 is phosphorylated by ATM and recruited to damage sites via the MRN complex, amplifying γH2AX production. STK39 overexpression in PAAD correlates with PARPi resistance; combining STK39 inhibition with PARPi shows synergistic efficacy.

## Key points
- STK39 amplifies γH2AX via ATM phosphorylation and MRN complex recruitment
- Promotes HR repair independent of BRCA status
- STK39 overexpressed in PAAD correlates with PARPi resistance
- STK39i + PARPi synergy demonstrated in preclinical models

## Project relevance
- Novel PARPi resistance mechanism beyond BRCA mutations
- Expands PARPi applicability beyond BRCA-mutant tumors
- Combination therapy rationale for pancreatic cancer

## Caveats
- Primarily PAAD models (generalizability unclear)
- Preclinical stage only
- No clinical STK39 inhibitor currently available
