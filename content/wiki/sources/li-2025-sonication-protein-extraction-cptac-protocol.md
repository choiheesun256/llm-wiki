# CPTAC Sonication Protocol for Proteomics

## Summary

Updated CPTAC proteomics protocol adding sonication to urea-based tissue lysis, improving detection of membrane-bound and DNA-binding proteins. Identifies >12,000 proteins and >25,000 phosphopeptides in TMT experiments from PDX tumor tissues. Provides detailed optimized conditions for sonication parameters.

## Key points

- Sonication step improves membrane and DNA-binding protein detection
- >12,000 proteins and >25,000 phosphopeptides per TMT set
- CPTAC standard protocol update
- Applicable to various biological specimens

## Project relevance

- Proteomics sample preparation optimization
- CPTAC standard protocol for large-scale studies
- Improved drug target protein coverage (membrane proteins)

## Caveats

- Protocol paper; no novel biological findings
- PDX tissue; primary tissue differences possible
- Sonication conditions may vary by equipment
- CPTAC-specific workflow
