# TNBC Proteogenomic Subtype Therapeutic Vulnerabilities

## Summary

Integrated proteogenomic analysis of TCGA TNBC (113 samples, 4 Lehmann subtypes: BL1, BL2, LAR, M) using RPPA, WES, and RNA-seq. Only 4% of protein changes explained by mutations vs. 21% by CNAs, establishing CNA as the dominant driver. Identifies subtype-specific actionable vulnerabilities: CDK (BL1), PI3K (LAR), TKI (BL2), mTOR (M). Validated with CPTAC MS-based proteomics and GDSC drug sensitivity data.

## Key points

- CNA is the dominant driver of protein changes in TNBC (21% vs. 4% mutations)
- Subtype-specific drug targets: CDK (BL1), PI3K (LAR), TKI (BL2), mTOR (M)
- Reverse proteogenomic approach: protein → RNA → DNA to identify drivers
- CPTAC MS and GDSC drug data validation

## Project relevance

- CNA-driven proteome changes as therapeutic targets in TNBC
- Subtype-specific precision therapy approach
- Proteogenomic integration methodology for actionable target identification

## Caveats

- RPPA covers only ~171 proteins (not whole proteome)
- TCGA retrospective data; no treatment response information
- In vitro validation only (GDSC cell lines)
- Lehmann subtype classification may not capture full heterogeneity
