# Crk/Abi1/Abl Kinase Signaling in GBM Invasion

## Summary

Demonstrates reciprocal regulation of Abl kinase by Crk adaptor (via Y251 phosphorylation) and Abi1 in controlling glioblastoma cell invasion. Crk Y251 phosphorylation creates negative feedback on Abl, while Abi1 positively regulates Abl activity. This Crk-Abi1-Abl axis is essential for GBM invasive phenotype and connects to WAVE complex-mediated migration.

## Key points

- Crk Y251 phosphorylation creates Abl negative feedback loop
- Abi1 positively regulates Abl kinase activity
- Reciprocal regulation controls GBM invasion and migration
- Abl inhibition (imatinib) reduces invasive phenotype

## Project relevance

- Kinase signaling mechanism in cancer invasion
- Potential therapeutic target (Abl) in glioblastoma
- Phosphorylation-based signaling regulation paradigm

## Caveats

- In vitro only (GBM cell lines); no in vivo validation
- Published 2015; imatinib has shown limited GBM clinical efficacy
- Focused mechanism; broader signaling context not addressed
- Limited translational impact demonstrated
