# ESR1 LBD Mutations in Hormone-Resistant Breast Cancer (Toy 2013)

## Summary

This Nature Genetics paper is the first systematic characterization of ESR1 ligand-binding domain (LBD) mutations in metastatic ER+ breast cancer. Targeted sequencing (230-gene panel, MSKCC cohort + BOLERO-2 trial) of 80 metastatic tumors identified ESR1 LBD mutations in 14 cases (18%), compared with <1% in TCGA primary tumors. Y537S and D538G are the most frequent hotspot mutations. Functional studies — luciferase reporter assays, MCF7 stable cell lines, and molecular dynamics simulations — demonstrate that these mutations lock ER in a ligand-independent agonist conformation, enabling hormone-independent transcription, HSP90-independent stability, and tumor growth in the absence of estrogen.

## Key points

- ESR1 LBD mutations are highly enriched in metastatic (18%) vs primary (<1%) ER+ breast cancer, consistent with endocrine therapy-driven clonal selection
- Y537S and D538G are the most common activating mutations; both adopt an agonist ER conformation without ligand
- Mutations confer resistance to aromatase inhibitors and tamoxifen but retain some sensitivity to fulvestrant
- HSP90-independent protein stability indicates the mutant ER is intrinsically conformationally stable
- Established the molecular rationale for next-generation SERDs (e.g., elacestrant) designed to degrade constitutively active ESR1 mutants

## Project relevance

- Foundational paper for ESR1-mutant breast cancer biology; essential reference for endocrine therapy resistance mechanisms in the drug repurposing project
- Demonstrates that metastatic tumor sequencing is essential to capture resistance-driving mutations absent in primary tumors
- ESR1 LBD mutations define a key patient subgroup for evaluating SERD/SERM combinations

## Caveats

- 2013 discovery paper; extensive follow-up literature now exists, including larger clinical cohorts and prospective trial data
- SERD and SERM efficacy against specific ESR1 mutants was not fully evaluated at the time of publication
- Mutations observed only at metastatic timepoints in this cohort; longitudinal clonal dynamics not tracked
