# HCC Proteogenomic Characterization

## Summary

Integrative proteogenomic analysis of hepatocellular carcinoma across multiple etiologies (HBV, HCV, metabolic) and stages. Identifies proteomics-driven molecular subtypes, etiology-specific molecular features, metabolic reprogramming patterns, and tumor microenvironment heterogeneity. Provides actionable therapeutic targets through multi-omics integration.

## Key points

- Proteomics-based molecular subtyping of HCC
- Etiology-specific molecular signatures (HBV vs HCV vs metabolic)
- Metabolic reprogramming differences across subtypes
- Immune microenvironment heterogeneity characterized
- Multi-omics integration reveals therapeutic targets

## Project relevance

- Proteogenomics pipeline for solid tumors
- Cancer subtype classification methodology
- TME characterization approach

## Caveats

- Single-center cohort; generalizability needs confirmation
- Bulk analysis; lacks single-cell resolution
- Limited functional validation of targets
