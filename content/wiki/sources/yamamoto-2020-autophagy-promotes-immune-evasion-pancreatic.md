# Autophagy-mediated MHC-I Degradation in PDAC

## Summary
Nature 2020 landmark study showing PDAC cells selectively degrade MHC-I via autophagy cargo receptor NBR1, reducing surface antigen presentation and enabling immune evasion. Autophagy inhibition (genetic or pharmacological with chloroquine) restores MHC-I surface expression, enhances CD8+ T cell responses, and synergizes with dual ICB (anti-PD1 + anti-CTLA4).

## Key points
- Autophagy selectively degrades MHC-I via NBR1 cargo receptor
- Chloroquine restores antigen presentation on tumor cell surface
- Synergy with dual ICB (anti-PD1 + anti-CTLA4) in PDAC mouse models
- Explains PDAC resistance to single-agent immunotherapy

## Project relevance
- Mechanistic basis for PDAC immunotherapy resistance
- Autophagy-immunity axis as druggable pathway
- Drug combination rationale (chloroquine + ICB) for repurposing

## Caveats
- Mouse models (clinical translation uncertain)
- Chloroquine clinical efficacy remains unproven
- PDAC-specific mechanism (not confirmed in other cancers)
