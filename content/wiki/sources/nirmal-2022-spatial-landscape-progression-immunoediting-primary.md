# Melanoma Spatial Progression and Immunoediting

## Summary

Maps the spatial landscape of primary melanoma progression and immunoediting at single-cell resolution using CyCIF multiplexed imaging and spatial transcriptomics. Reveals spatially organized immunoediting patterns, stage-specific immune evasion mechanisms, and cell-cell interaction networks that change during tumor progression.

## Key points

- CyCIF + spatial transcriptomics integration for melanoma
- Spatially organized immunoediting patterns discovered
- Stage-specific immune evasion mechanisms identified
- Cell-cell interaction networks differ by tumor region
- Spatial biomarkers of progression

## Project relevance

- Spatial analysis methodology for tumor-immune interactions
- Multiplexed imaging integration approach
- TME spatial organization framework

## Caveats

- Melanoma-specific; generalization to other cancers needed
- Cross-sectional design; no longitudinal validation
- Limited cohort size
