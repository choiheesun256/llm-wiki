# Chlorambucil for BRCA1/2-Deficient Tumours (Tacconi 2019)

## Summary

A screen of 1,280 FDA-approved drugs (Prestwick library) identified chlorambucil as selectively toxic to BRCA2-deficient cells. This bifunctional alkylating agent, already used in CLL treatment, retains efficacy against olaparib-resistant and cisplatin-resistant BRCA1/2-deficient tumors in PDX models. Chlorambucil induces replication-associated DSBs, and shows a superior therapeutic index over cisplatin due to lower toxicity in normal cells.

## Key points

- Drug repurposing via 1,280-compound FDA-approved library viability screen (Prestwick) identified chlorambucil as a selective killer of BRCA1/2-deficient cells
- Effective against both olaparib-resistant and cisplatin-resistant BRCA1/2-deficient cells and PDTXs
- Mechanism: induction of replication-associated DSBs; ATR, FANCD2, and SNM1A are key sensitivity determinants
- Higher therapeutic index than cisplatin — lower toxicity in normal (BRCA-proficient) cells

## Project relevance

- Direct example of phenotypic drug repurposing from an FDA-approved library screen
- Demonstrates that PARPi resistance does not preclude all DNA-damage-based strategies — relevant to combination and rescue approaches
- BRCA1/2-deficiency as a synthetic lethality context is a core framework for the drug repurposing project

## Caveats

- Evidence is primarily in vitro and PDX; no clinical trial data reported
- Subtle response differences between BRCA1 vs BRCA2 deficiency are not fully characterized
- Chlorambucil is an older alkylating agent with known off-target effects
