# Multi-omic Profiling of ccRCC Metabolic Reprogramming

## Summary

Integrative multi-omic profiling (WES, RNA-seq, proteomics, metabolomics, spatial transcriptomics, spatial metabolomics, snRNA-seq/snATAC-seq) of 100 ccRCC patients identifies four immune subtypes (IM1–IM4) and a novel DCCD (De-clear cell differentiated) subtype characterized by loss of lipid droplets, reduced metabolic activity, enhanced nutrient uptake, and poor prognosis even at Stage I.

## Key points

- Four immune subtypes with distinct prognosis: IM4 (worst) has highest stromal/TAM infiltration
- DCCD represents a common mode of ccRCC progression with metabolic dedifferentiation
- Stage I DCCD tumors have higher recurrence — nephrectomy alone may be insufficient
- Aristolochic acid mutational signature present in 49% of tumors (East Asian-specific)

## Project relevance

- Comprehensive multi-omics + spatial + single-cell integration workflow as a reference pipeline
- Immune subtype classification informs immunotherapy/targeted therapy selection
- Metabolic reprogramming in DCCD may reveal druggable metabolic vulnerabilities

## Caveats

- Single-institution Chinese cohort; generalizability to other populations unknown
- Spatial and single-cell data limited to 10 samples
- No prospective validation of subtype-specific treatment strategies
