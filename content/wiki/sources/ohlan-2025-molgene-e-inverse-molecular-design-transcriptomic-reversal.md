# MolGene-E: Inverse Molecular Design

## Summary

Deep generative framework for inverse molecular design from single-cell transcriptomics. Combines a cross-modal domain adaptation model (harmonizing bulk and single-cell chemical transcriptomics) with a contrastive learning-based generative model for phenotype-to-molecule generation. Achieves SOTA zero-shot performance on L1000toRNAseq and Sciplex-3 datasets.

## Key points

- First framework for molecule design from single-cell transcriptomics
- Cross-modal domain adaptation for bulk ↔ single-cell
- Contrastive learning aligns phenotypic and chemical spaces
- Zero-shot SOTA on unseen perturbation profiles

## Project relevance

- Phenotype-based drug design (target-free)
- Drug repurposing via transcriptomic reversal
- Generative AI for drug discovery

## Caveats

- Preprint (bioRxiv 2025); not peer-reviewed
- In silico only; no wet-lab validation
- Training data chemical diversity limitations
