# Lapatinib Resistance in HER2+ Breast Cancer (Steggall 2025)

## Summary

Multi-omics study dissecting lapatinib resistance mechanisms in HER2+ breast cancer by integrating ATAC-seq, RNA-seq, and mass spectrometry-based proteomics across parental and lapatinib-resistant cell line pairs. Identifies chromatin remodeling, transcription factor activity changes, and bypass signaling pathway activation at the protein level as co-occurring resistance determinants.

## Key points

- Chromatin accessibility changes (ATAC-seq) contribute to acquired lapatinib resistance
- Specific transcription factor activity alterations identified in resistant cells
- Bypass signaling pathways activated at the proteome level
- Integrative multi-omics pipeline reveals resistance determinants not visible from single-layer analysis

## Project relevance

- Multi-omics resistance analysis pipeline integrating epigenomic, transcriptomic, and proteomic layers
- Identifies combinatorial treatment targets for overcoming HER2 TKI resistance
- Methodological template for epigenetic + proteomic integration in drug resistance studies

## Caveats

- Cell line-based (in vitro) study; lacks clinical tissue validation
- Findings specific to lapatinib; applicability to other HER2 TKIs (neratinib, tucatinib) not confirmed
- In vivo and patient-derived model validation absent
