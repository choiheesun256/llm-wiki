# Large-Scale PTM Analysis by Mass Spectrometry

## Summary

Perspective article reviewing the status of large-scale post-translational modification analysis by mass spectrometry. Covers phosphorylation, ubiquitination, acetylation, and glycosylation enrichment strategies, MS acquisition methods, and data analysis pipelines. Discusses achievements (>50K phosphosites quantified) and future directions including deeper coverage, PTM dynamics, and crosstalk analysis.

## Key points

- Phosphoproteomics routinely quantifies >50,000 sites
- DiGly enrichment enables proteome-wide ubiquitination analysis
- Lysine acetylome mapping established
- Multi-PTM integration emerging
- Quantitative PTM dynamics achievable

## Project relevance

- PTM analysis methodology reference
- Phosphoproteomics enrichment strategies
- Foundation for understanding current PTM technologies

## Caveats

- Published 2013; does not cover recent advances (DIA, TMT, etc.)
- Review article; no original data
- Rapidly evolving field with significant advances since publication
