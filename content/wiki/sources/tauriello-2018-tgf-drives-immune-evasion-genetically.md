# TGFβ-Driven Immune Evasion in CRC Metastasis (Tauriello 2018)

## Summary

Using a quadruple-mutant (Lgr5-creERT2; Apc/Kras/Tgfbr2/Trp53, LAKTP) mouse model and orthotopic transplantation of mouse tumor organoids, this Nature paper establishes TGFβ as the central driver of immune evasion in metastatic CRC. TGFβ promotes T cell exclusion and blocks TH1 effector differentiation. Anti-PD-1 monotherapy has limited effect in this model, but the TGFBR1 inhibitor galunisertib prevents metastasis and converts anti-PD-1-refractory tumors into responders. The TGFβ-high, immune-excluded phenotype maps to human CMS4 (mesenchymal) CRC.

## Key points

- TGFβ is the primary driver of T cell exclusion and TH1 suppression in metastatic CRC — not PD-L1
- Anti-PD-1 alone is ineffective; galunisertib (TGFBR1 inhibitor) restores anti-tumor immunity and prevents metastasis
- TGFβ inhibition combined with anti-PD-1 is synergistic, converting ICB-resistant tumors to responders
- CMS4 (mesenchymal) subtype in human CRC corresponds to the high-TGFβ, immune-excluded phenotype
- Genetically reconstituted organoid transplant model faithfully recapitulates human metastatic CRC progression

## Project relevance

- Defines TGFβ as a key resistance node for immunotherapy in MSS CRC — directly relevant to the drug repurposing project
- TGFβ pathway inhibition is an actionable combination strategy for immune-cold tumors
- CMS4 classification provides a patient selection framework for TGFβ-targeting strategies

## Caveats

- Mouse model; direct translation to human clinical outcomes has not yet been demonstrated
- Clinical trials of galunisertib have shown limited single-agent activity in CRC
- Findings are specific to MSS CRC; MSI-H CRC is already ICB-responsive via distinct mechanisms
