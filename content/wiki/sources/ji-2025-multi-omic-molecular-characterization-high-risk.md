# High-Risk Early Breast Cancer Multi-Omic Subtyping

## Summary

Multi-omic analysis of 142 high-risk early breast cancer patients identifies three molecular subtypes: IA (immune-active), VT (VEGF/TME), and KA (kinase-active). Each subtype corresponds to distinct optimal neoadjuvant strategies. A 7-gene RNA classifier predicts KA subtype with AUC ~90%, enabling clinically practical patient stratification for kinase-targeted therapy addition.

## Key points

- Three subtypes (IA/VT/KA) with distinct biology and treatment sensitivity
- IA benefits from immunotherapy, VT from anti-angiogenics, KA from CDK4/6 inhibitors
- 7-gene classifier achieves AUC ~90% for KA subtype identification
- Subtype-guided treatment improves pCR rates

## Project relevance

- Multi-omic subtyping directly informing neoadjuvant treatment selection
- Clinically practical 7-gene classifier (RT-qPCR level)
- Molecular rationale for adding targeted agents to standard chemotherapy

## Caveats

- Single-institution cohort (142 patients); prospective randomized validation needed
- Retrospective design; not a subtype-guided randomized trial
- Asian cohort; generalization to other populations requires confirmation
