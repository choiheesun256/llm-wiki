# Pan-Cancer Proteogenomics of Tumor Immunity

## Summary

Comprehensive pan-cancer immune landscape analysis of 1,056 tumors across 10 cancer types using CPTAC proteogenomic data. Identifies 7 distinct immune subtypes based on integrative learning of cell type compositions and pathway activities. DNA alterations associate with immune subtypes. Phosphoproteomics-based kinase activity analysis reveals subtype-specific therapeutic targets for precision immunotherapy.

## Key points

- 1,056 tumors across 10 cancer types (CPTAC)
- 7 pan-cancer immune subtypes defined
- DNA alterations associated with immune subtypes
- Kinase activity reveals subtype-specific therapeutic targets
- Digital pathology validates immune cell compositions

## Project relevance

- Pan-cancer immune subtyping from proteogenomics
- Kinase activity-based immunotherapy target discovery
- CPTAC large-scale resource

## Caveats

- Bulk analysis; lacks single-cell resolution
- Treatment-naive only; no immunotherapy response data
- Retrospective; prospective validation needed
