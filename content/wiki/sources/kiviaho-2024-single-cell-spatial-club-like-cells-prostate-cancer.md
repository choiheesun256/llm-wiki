# Club-like Cells and MDSC Interaction in Prostate Cancer

## Summary

Combined scRNA-seq and Visium spatial transcriptomics of 120 prostate cancer patients identifies tumor-expanded club-like cells (luminal progenitor features) that spatially colocalize with immunosuppressive myeloid cells (MDSC). Ligand-receptor analysis supports immunosuppressive niche formation. Androgen deprivation therapy alters club-like cell proportions, suggesting treatment-responsive TME remodeling.

## Key points

- Club-like cells expanded in tumor, colocalize with MDSC spatially
- Ligand-receptor interactions support immunosuppressive niche
- ADT alters club-like cell proportions
- 120-patient cohort with scRNA-seq + Visium integration

## Project relevance

- Spatial transcriptomics for cell-cell interaction mapping
- Novel cell type identification in prostate cancer TME
- Treatment-induced TME remodeling characterization

## Caveats

- Visium spot resolution requires deconvolution
- Spatial correlation does not prove causation
- MDSC classification criteria debated in field
- ADT effects confounded by disease stage
