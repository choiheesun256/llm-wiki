# Non-Canonical IKKs in Cancer

## Summary

Review of TBK1 and IKKε non-canonical IKK kinases in cancer. IKKε is amplified in ~30% of breast cancers and activates NF-κB. TBK1 is essential for survival of KRAS-driven lung cancers through the RalB-Sec5-TBK1 axis. Both coordinate interferon responses, NF-κB activation, and cell survival signaling relevant to tumorigenesis.

## Key points

- IKKε amplified in ~30% breast cancers
- TBK1 essential for KRAS-driven NSCLC survival
- Non-canonical NF-κB activation mechanisms
- Interferon and inflammatory response coordination

## Project relevance

- TBK1 as therapeutic target in KRAS cancers
- NF-κB pathway in cancer biology
- Kinase signaling fundamentals

## Caveats

- Published 2011; TBK1 biology substantially advanced since
- Review; no new data
- Pre-immunotherapy era context
