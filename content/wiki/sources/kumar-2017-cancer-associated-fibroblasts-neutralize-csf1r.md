# CAF-Mediated PMN-MDSC Infiltration Limits CSF1R Blockade

## Summary

CSF1R inhibitors deplete tumor-associated macrophages but fail as monotherapy. Kumar et al. show that CAF upregulate granulocytic chemokines (CXCL1/2/5) upon CSF1R blockade via relief of HDAC2-mediated repression, recruiting PMN-MDSCs that compensate for TAM loss. Combining CSF1R inhibitor with CXCR2 antagonist blocks PMN-MDSC recruitment and reduces tumor growth, further enhanced by anti-PD-1.

## Key points

- CSF1R inhibition induces compensatory PMN-MDSC infiltration via CAF
- CSF1 represses CAF chemokine expression through HDAC2
- CSF1R + CXCR2 combination blocks compensatory immune suppression
- Triple therapy (CSF1R + CXCR2 + anti-PD-1) shows strongest anti-tumor effect

## Project relevance

- Drug resistance mechanism through TME cellular crosstalk
- Rationale for combination immunotherapy design
- CAF as therapeutic target in cancer treatment

## Caveats

- Mouse tumor models only; human TME may differ
- CAF subtype heterogeneity not addressed
- Clinical toxicity of triple combination unknown
- Single epigenetic mechanism (HDAC2) may oversimplify
