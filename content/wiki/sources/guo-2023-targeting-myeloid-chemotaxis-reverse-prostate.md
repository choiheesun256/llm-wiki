# Myeloid Chemotaxis and Prostate Cancer Therapy Resistance

## Summary

Senescence-associated myeloid inflammation drives ARSI resistance in metastatic CRPC. CXCR2 chemokines recruit PMN-MDSCs to tumors. A proof-of-concept clinical trial of CXCR2 inhibitor (AZD5069) + enzalutamide shows safety and early efficacy in ARSI-resistant mCRPC.

## Key points

- CXCR2-mediated myeloid recruitment drives therapy resistance, not just immune suppression
- Blood NLR serves as a simple biomarker for myeloid-mediated resistance
- Clinical PoC trial demonstrates tolerability and reduction of intratumor myeloid cells
- AZD5069 is a drug repurposing case (respiratory → oncology)

## Project relevance

- Senescence-inflammation-resistance axis applicable to other cancers and drug classes
- Drug repurposing paradigm: existing compound redirected to cancer indication
- Biomarker (NLR) for treatment stratification

## Caveats

- Small proof-of-concept trial; randomized data pending
- Prostate cancer-specific; generalizability needs validation
