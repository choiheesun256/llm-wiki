# Ovarian cancer mutational processes drive site-specific immune evasion

## Summary

A landmark Nature (2022) study performing integrative multi-omics analysis of 160 tumor sites from 42 treatment-naive high-grade serous ovarian cancer (HGSOC) patients (MSK SPECTRUM cohort). Using WGS, scRNA-seq, H&E histopathology, and multiplexed immunofluorescence, the study links genomic archetypes to distinct immune evasion modes. HRD-Dup (BRCA1-associated) and HRD-Del (BRCA2-associated) tumors display inflammatory signaling, immunoediting, and HLA loss, while foldback-inversion tumors are characterized by TGFβ-driven immunosuppression and immune exclusion. Critically, immune phenotypic states are anatomical site-specific, differing between adnexal and peritoneal metastatic sites.

## Key points

- Three HGSOC genomic archetypes defined by WGS: HRD-Dup (BRCA1 loss), HRD-Del (BRCA2 loss), and foldback-inversion — each with a distinct immune evasion mode
- HRD-Dup and HRD-Del: high inflammatory signaling + evidence of immunoediting + HLA allele loss → active but edited immune response
- Foldback-inversion: TGFβ pathway overactivation → immune exclusion (T cells present but excluded from tumor core)
- scRNA-seq reveals tumor cell phenotypic states that vary systematically by anatomical location (adnexal vs. peritoneal)
- Peritoneal metastatic sites harbor distinct immune microenvironments compared to adnexal primary sites
- Demonstrates that a single biopsy from one site may inadequately characterize tumor immune biology

## Project relevance

Highly relevant as a methodological and conceptual reference for multi-omics cancer studies. The genomic archetype-to-immune phenotype axis is directly applicable to drug repurposing work: HRD status, TGFβ pathway activity, and immune exclusion scores could serve as patient stratification features. The site-specific immune heterogeneity data cautions against single-site sampling assumptions. The WGS + scRNA-seq integration framework is a reference design for future multi-site tumor studies.

## Caveats

- Treatment-naive cohort only — dynamics of immune evolution under therapy and resistance mechanisms are not captured
- Cohort size (42 patients, 160 sites) limits statistical power for rare molecular subgroups
- scRNA-seq and WGS are not always from the exact same tumor tissue fragment, introducing spatial assignment uncertainty
- Spatial transcriptomics was not used, so direct cell-cell interaction inference from proximity is limited
- Findings are HGSOC-specific; generalization to other gynecological or solid tumors requires validation
