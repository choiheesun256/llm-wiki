# B7-H4 Palmitoylation and Immune Evasion

## Summary
Nat Comms 2025 study discovering that ZDHHC3-mediated palmitoylation of B7-H4 at Cys130 prevents its lysosomal degradation, sustaining immune suppression. Abemaciclib (CDK4/6 inhibitor) promotes lysosome activation and B7-H4 degradation independently of cell cycle effects, restoring T cell-mediated anti-tumor immunity. Demonstrates drug repurposing potential for B7-H4-expressing tumors.

## Key points
- B7-H4 palmitoylation by ZDHHC3 at Cys130 prevents lysosomal degradation
- Abemaciclib induces B7-H4 lysosomal degradation independent of cell cycle
- T cell activation restored in preclinical models
- Drug repurposing: CDK4/6 inhibitor for immune checkpoint modulation

## Project relevance
- PTM-mediated immune checkpoint regulation as novel axis
- Drug repurposing strategy (CDK4/6i beyond cell cycle)
- B7-H4 as emerging immune target

## Caveats
- Breast cancer models primarily
- Abemaciclib has pleiotropic effects (specificity concerns)
- Clinical validation needed
