# Proteogenomics of EGFR/ALK WT Never-Smoker LUAD

## Summary

Proteogenomic analysis of 99 Korean never-smoker lung adenocarcinoma patients (EGFR/ALK wild-type) using WGS, RNA-seq, and TMT-based proteomics/phosphoproteomics. Identifies 4 proteogenomic subtypes (proliferation, angiogenesis, immune, metabolism) with distinct therapeutic vulnerabilities: CDK inhibitors, anti-angiogenics, immunotherapy, and metabolic targeting respectively.

## Key points

- 99 EGFR/ALK WT never-smoker LUAD patients profiled by multi-omics
- 4 proteogenomic subtypes with distinct biology and drug targets
- Korean cohort addresses understudied never-smoker population
- TMT proteomics + phosphoproteomics + WGS integration

## Project relevance

- Proteogenomic subtyping for precision oncology
- Multi-omics integration methodology (WGS + RNA + protein)
- Therapeutic vulnerability identification in treatment-limited population

## Caveats

- Single Korean cohort (99 patients); needs external validation
- No treatment response data to validate subtype-drug matching
- EGFR/ALK WT subset only; not representative of all LUAD
- Population-specific findings may not generalize
