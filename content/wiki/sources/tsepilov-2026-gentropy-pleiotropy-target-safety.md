# Open Targets Gentropy pleiotropy map

## Summary

This preprint applies the Open Targets Gentropy framework to 100,526 GWAS and builds gene-level and variant-level pleiotropy maps. For target discovery, the key contribution is a safety-liability axis that is not captured by cell-line essentiality screens.

## Key points

- Prioritizes causal genes for 77.9% of protein-coding genes.
- Defines gene pleiotropy score and variant pleiotropy score.
- High pleiotropy is enriched in immune, inflammatory, oncogenic, and organism-level liability pathways.
- PAV support plus intermediate pleiotropy is presented as a strong translational target-evidence profile.
- High gene pleiotropy captures safety-stopped clinical programme enrichment while being depleted in DepMap/FUSIL cellular-lethal sets.

## Project relevance

- Adds an organism-level safety screen to target discovery.
- Useful for E3 ligase, proteasome substrate, PROTAC, and target-degradation programmes where in vitro viability may miss systemic risk.
- Practical target triage should check PAV support, gPS, therapeutic-area breadth, and safety-stopped family precedents.

## Caveats

- Pleiotropy can mix horizontal and vertical effects.
- Fine-mapping and ancestry representation affect confidence.
- Gene-level pleiotropy is a liability screen, not a mechanism proof.
