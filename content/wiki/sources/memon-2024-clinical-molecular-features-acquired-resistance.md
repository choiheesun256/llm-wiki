# Acquired Resistance to Immunotherapy in NSCLC

## Summary

Analysis of 1,201 NSCLC patients treated with PD-(L)1 blockade reveals acquired resistance occurs in >60% of initial responders. Relapsed tumors show upregulated IFNγ response genes, persistent IFN signaling, immune dysfunction, and antigen presentation mutations. The TME is persistently inflamed rather than excluded, suggesting reprogramming rather than exclusion strategies needed.

## Key points

- Acquired resistance in >60% of initial responders
- IFNγ response paradoxically upregulated in resistant tumors
- Antigen presentation mutations in relapsed tumors
- Persistently inflamed TME characterizes resistance

## Project relevance

- Molecular mechanisms of immunotherapy resistance
- IFN signaling in drug resistance
- TME characterization in treatment failure

## Caveats

- NSCLC-specific findings
- Retrospective study
- Heterogeneous treatment regimens
