# Golgi-Associated Macrophage TAZ

## Summary

Discovers a noncanonical role for TAZ in macrophages where it localizes to the Golgi apparatus (not nucleus) upon TLR activation. Golgi-associated TAZ enhances vesicle trafficking and proinflammatory cytokine secretion independently of the canonical Hippo-TEAD pathway. TAZ depletion in tumor-associated macrophages promotes tumor growth by suppressing lymphocyte infiltration, indicating that YAP/TAZ-TEAD therapies may be ineffective in macrophages.

## Key points

- TAZ localizes to Golgi in macrophages (noncanonical)
- Hippo-independent: no TEAD binding in macrophages
- Golgi-TAZ drives proinflammatory cytokine secretion
- TAZ depletion promotes tumor growth via immune suppression
- Implications for YAP/TAZ-TEAD targeted therapy limitations

## Project relevance

- Noncanonical signaling pathway in TME
- Macrophage biology in tumor immunity
- Therapeutic target considerations

## Caveats

- Macrophage-specific; other immune cells not tested
- Mouse models; human relevance needs confirmation
- Indirect tumor effect through immune modulation
