# EMP and Immune Evasion (Review)

## Summary

Review of epithelial-mesenchymal plasticity (EMP) in tumor immune evasion. Hybrid E/M states promote immune escape through increased checkpoint molecules, defective antigen presentation, and immunosuppressive TME remodeling (MDSC, TAM, Treg recruitment). EMP is now recognized as a plastic spectrum, not a binary switch, with unique immunomodulatory effects contributing to ICB resistance.

## Key points

- EMP as plastic spectrum with hybrid E/M states
- Hybrid E/M cells promote both immune evasion and TME suppression
- CTC clusters leverage EMP for immunosuppressive advantage
- EMP contributes to ICB resistance mechanisms

## Project relevance

- EMT-immune crosstalk in drug resistance
- Immunotherapy resistance mechanisms
- TME remodeling by cancer cell plasticity

## Caveats

- Review article; no original data
- Quantitative definition of hybrid E/M states not established
- EMP therapeutic targeting strategies unclear
