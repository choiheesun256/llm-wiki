# BRCA1 intronic Alu elements drive gene rearrangements and PARP inhibitor resistance

## Summary

A Nature Communications (2019) study from Fox Chase Cancer Center identifying a structural mechanism of PARP inhibitor (PARPi) resistance mediated by Alu element-driven rearrangements in BRCA1 intron 15. While BRCA1 BRCT domain mutations normally cause proteasomal degradation rendering cells PARPi-sensitive, Alu-mediated recombination in intron 15 produces a truncated protein (BRCA1-I15) that lacks the BRCT domain yet evades proteasomal degradation. This hypomorphic BRCA1 retains partial homologous recombination (HR) function, conferring PARPi resistance. The intron 15-encoded residues in BRCA1-I15 were confirmed by MS peptide analysis using the SNU-251-RR resistant cell line model.

## Key points

- BRCA1 BRCT domain mutations → proteasomal degradation of full-length BRCA1 → HR deficiency → PARPi sensitivity (expected)
- Alu element-mediated rearrangements within BRCA1 intron 15 generate a truncated isoform BRCA1-I15, bypassing the degradation signal
- BRCA1-I15 lacks the BRCT domain but retains partial HR activity sufficient to restore DNA double-strand break repair
- LC-MS/MS peptide analysis directly confirmed intron 15-encoded amino acid sequences in the expressed BRCA1-I15 protein
- SNU-251-RR (PARPi-resistant derivative of SNU-251 ovarian cancer cell line) was the primary experimental model
- Demonstrates that absence of detectable full-length BRCA1 protein is insufficient to predict PARPi sensitivity if hypomorphic isoforms exist

## Project relevance

Illustrates a structural variant-based resistance mechanism that is invisible to standard mutation calling — relevant to drug repurposing and resistance prediction pipelines. Highlights the importance of assessing BRCA1 protein isoforms (not just mutation status) when predicting PARPi response. The Alu recombination mechanism connects to broader themes of repeat element-driven genome instability. The MS-based protein isoform confirmation strategy is methodologically transferable to other truncation/splicing resistance mechanisms.

## Caveats

- Experimental evidence is based on a single cell line model (SNU-251-RR); prevalence of this Alu rearrangement in clinical PARPi-resistant patient samples remains to be established at scale
- The precise BRCT-independent domain(s) of BRCA1-I15 responsible for partial HR function are not fully characterized
- Alu rearrangements at other BRCA1 intronic positions and in BRCA2 have not been systematically explored in this study
- Detection of BRCA1-I15 may require protein-level or isoform-aware RNA-seq analysis that is not standard in clinical genomic testing
