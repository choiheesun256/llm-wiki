# Xuanwei Air-Pollution LUAD Proteogenomics (eLife 2024)

## Summary
Integrated multi-omics (WES, RNA-seq, proteomics, phosphoproteomics, CT radiomics) of 169 never-smoking female LUAD patients from Xuanwei, China (XWLC cohort), where coal smoke is the primary carcinogen. XWLC is defined as a molecularly distinct LUAD subtype driven by benzo[a]pyrene (BaP); EGFR-G719X is a BaP-induced hotspot in 20% of cases associated with worse outcomes. Multi-omics clustering identified four clinically relevant subtypes, and MAD1/TPRN were proposed as novel therapeutic targets.

## Key points
- 169 never-smoking women; WES (n=135), RNA-seq (n=136), proteomics/phosphoproteomics (n=102), radiomics (n=115)
- Compared against CNLC (non-smoking), TSLC (smoking), TNLC (non-smoking TCGA) cohorts
- BaP mutational signature confirmed; CYP1A1 protein upregulated in tumors
- EGFR-G719X hotspot in 20% XWLC → MAPK activation, worse prognosis than common EGFR mutations
- 4 multi-omics XWLC subtypes with distinct biology, metabolism, immune landscape, and radiomic features
- Radiomics integration enables non-invasive molecular subtyping potential
- MAD1 and TPRN identified as novel therapeutic targets

## Project relevance
- Radiomics integration into multi-omics pipeline is a unique methodological reference
- Population-specific (never-smoker, air-pollution-exposed) molecular characterization
- Novel target identification (MAD1, TPRN) workflow transferable to drug repurposing
- EGFR subtype-specific treatment implications relevant to precision oncology

## Caveats
- Single geographic cohort (Xuanwei); results may not generalize
- Female never-smokers only; applicability to male/smoking patients limited
- Radiomics-omics associations are correlative, not causal
- MAD1 and TPRN lack functional validation
