# Proteogenomics of Prostate Cancer Radioresistance

## Summary

Multi-omic (WGS, WES, RNA-seq, subcellular proteomics, miRNA) characterization of radioresistance in prostate cancer cell lines treated with conventional fractionation (CF) and hypofractionation (HF). Radioresistance involves massive genomic instability and DNA mismatch repair abrogation regardless of fractionation. POLQ (DNA Polymerase Theta) is identified as a candidate radiosensitizer target validated by genetic and pharmacologic inhibition.

## Key points

- CF-resistant cells show more aggressive molecular phenotypes than HF-resistant cells
- Subcellular fractionation proteomics reveals compartment-specific protein changes
- POLQ inhibition resensitizes radioresistant prostate cancer cells
- Integrates cell line models with primary patient molecular data

## Project relevance

- POLQ as an actionable target for drug repurposing in radioresistant cancers
- Subcellular proteomics workflow applicable to drug mechanism studies
- Fractionation-specific resistance supports treatment personalization

## Caveats

- Cell line-based study; limited in vivo or clinical validation
- Prostate cancer-specific; generalizability to other cancers needs testing
