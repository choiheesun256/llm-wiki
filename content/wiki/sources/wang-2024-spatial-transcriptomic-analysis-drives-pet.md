## Summary

Nature Communications 2024. Spatial transcriptomics (Visium) combined with CODEX and IHC proteomics across 40 human PDAC surgical specimens to systematically identify cancer cell surface markers suitable for PET imaging. Claudin-4 (CLDN4) emerged as the top candidate: ~16-fold upregulated versus normal pancreas, spatially co-localizing with cancer cells rather than stroma. A peptide-based PET radiotracer targeting CLDN4 achieves ~25% IA/cc uptake in metastatic lesions. S100P also showed strong spatial correlation with cancer cells. From Stanford.

## Key points

- Translational pipeline linking spatially resolved surface marker discovery directly to radiotracer development
- Spatial correlation scoring as a quantitative strategy for tumor-cell specificity within the surfaceome
- Multi-modal integration of Visium, CODEX, and IHC on the same 40-patient cohort
- CLDN4 expression is cancer-cell-specific, not stromal — important for PET signal-to-background
- S100P co-identified as a secondary biomarker candidate

## Project relevance

Demonstrates how spatial transcriptomics can drive therapeutic target selection beyond gene expression alone. The surfaceome scoring framework could be applied to other cancer types, including pancreatic precursor lesions (IPMN). The translational leap from ST discovery to in vivo radiotracer is a strong model for bench-to-bedside spatial omics workflows.

## Caveats

- Visium spatial resolution (~55 µm/spot) limits single-cell precision
- 40-patient cohort; multi-institutional validation pending
- No clinical Phase I data for the CLDN4-targeting tracer yet
- In vivo validation relies on xenograft (immunodeficient) models
