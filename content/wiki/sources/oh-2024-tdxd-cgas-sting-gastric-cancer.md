# T-DXd cGAS-STING Activation in Gastric Cancer

## Summary

Demonstrates that trastuzumab deruxtecan (T-DXd) activates the cGAS-STING innate immune pathway in HER2-positive gastric cancer through DNA damage-induced cytosolic DNA accumulation. This triggers type I IFN response and immune cell recruitment, enhancing antitumor immunity. Provides mechanistic rationale for T-DXd and immune checkpoint inhibitor combination therapy.

## Key points

- T-DXd induces DNA damage and cytosolic DNA accumulation
- cGAS-STING pathway activation triggers innate immunity
- Type I IFN response and immune cell recruitment
- Bystander immune activation effect
- Rationale for T-DXd + ICI combination

## Project relevance

- ADC mechanism of action beyond direct cytotoxicity
- DNA damage-innate immunity connection
- Combination therapy rationale (ADC + ICI)

## Caveats

- Preclinical models only; clinical validation needed
- HER2+ specific; uncertain in HER2-low setting
- Xenograft models lack full immune competence
