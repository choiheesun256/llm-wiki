# Proteogenomics of Urothelial Bladder Cancer

## Summary
Nat Comms 2023 comprehensive proteogenomic analysis of 448 samples from 190 urothelial cancer patients across all disease stages. Distinguishes molecular features of papillary urothelial cancer (PUC, HRAS/FGFR3) from carcinoma in situ (CIS, TP53/RB1). Identifies distinct origins of invasive tumors with different prognosis. RBPMS loss in CIS-derived tumors activates AP-1 and promotes metastasis.

## Key points
- 448 samples from 190 patients spanning all urothelial cancer stages
- PUC vs CIS have distinct molecular paths to invasion
- RBPMS loss drives CIS-derived metastasis via AP-1 activation
- Phosphoproteomics reveals stage-specific signaling networks

## Project relevance
- Comprehensive cancer proteogenomics methodology
- Stage-resolved molecular characterization approach
- Therapeutic target identification (RBPMS/AP-1 axis)

## Caveats
- Chinese cohort only (limited ancestry diversity)
- Complex multi-cohort integration challenges
- Some disease stages have small sample sizes
