# m6A/IGF2BP Pan-Cancer Proteogenomics

## Summary

Pan-cancer proteogenomic analysis of RNA m6A modification regulators using CPTAC data. Identifies IGF2BP1/2/3 m6A readers as overexpressed at the protein level across cancers, with significant mRNA-protein discordance. Reveals the proteome-level impact of the m6A epitranscriptomic pathway and its association with prognosis.

## Key points

- IGF2BP proteins overexpressed across cancers
- mRNA-protein discordance in m6A regulators
- m6A pathway broadly impacts the proteome
- IGF2BP associated with poor prognosis

## Project relevance

- Epitranscriptomics-proteomics integration
- Pan-cancer CPTAC data reanalysis methodology
- Post-transcriptional regulation in cancer

## Caveats

- Public CPTAC data reanalysis; no original data
- Correlative; no direct m6A-protein causality
- Limited functional validation
