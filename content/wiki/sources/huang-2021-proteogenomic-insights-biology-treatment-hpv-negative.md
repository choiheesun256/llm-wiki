# CPTAC Proteogenomics HPV-Negative HNSCC

## Summary

CPTAC proteogenomic study of 108 HPV-negative HNSCCs integrating WES, WGS, RNA-seq, methylation, miRNA-seq, proteomics, and phosphoproteomics. Identifies mutual exclusivity between FAT1 truncation and 11q13.3 amplification converging on actin dynamics. Phosphoproteomics distinguishes two modes of EGFR activation informing anti-EGFR stratification. Three molecular subtypes map to CDK inhibitors, anti-EGFR therapy, and immunotherapy, respectively.

## Key points

- 11,744 proteins and 56,959 phosphosites quantified; protein outperforms RNA for gene function prediction
- FAT1/11q13.3 mutual exclusivity converges on actin dysregulation
- EGFR ligand abundance-based stratification for anti-EGFR monoclonal antibody response
- Immune-cold tumors driven by widespread deletion of immune modulatory genes

## Project relevance

- Reference CPTAC proteogenomics pipeline (WES+RNA+protein+phospho)
- Three subtypes directly mapped to existing therapies (CDK4/6i, cetuximab, pembrolizumab)
- Phosphoproteomics essential for distinguishing EGFR activation modes

## Caveats

- HPV-negative only; HPV-positive HNSCC requires separate analysis
- No treatment-response data for prospective subtype validation
- 70% smokers — potential smoking-associated bias
