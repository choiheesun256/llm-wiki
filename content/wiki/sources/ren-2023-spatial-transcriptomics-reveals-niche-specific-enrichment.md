# Spatial Transcriptomics of Malignant Gliomas

## Summary

Spatial profiling of DMG and GBM combining short- and long-read Visium spatial transcriptomics from 10 tumors. Identifies four spatially segregated transcriptional modules and reveals that radial glial stem-like (RG-like) cells are enriched in the neuron-rich invasive niche. FAM20C mediates invasive growth of RG-like cells in this niche. Long-read ST captures isoform diversity across glioma niches.

## Key points

- 10 glioma samples with short + long-read Visium ST
- 4 spatially segregated transcriptional modules
- RG-like cells enriched in neuron-rich invasive niche
- FAM20C mediates invasive growth
- Long-read ST reveals isoform diversity

## Project relevance

- Combined short/long-read spatial transcriptomics
- Glioma niche-specific analysis
- Stem cell spatial distribution methodology

## Caveats

- Small cohort (10 samples)
- Visium spot-level, not single-cell resolution
- Mouse validation model
