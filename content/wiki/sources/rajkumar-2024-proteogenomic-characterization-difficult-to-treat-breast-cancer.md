# Difficult-to-Treat Breast Cancer Proteogenomics

## Summary

Proteogenomic characterization of 117 untreated primary breast tumors comparing difficult-to-treat breast cancer (DTBC) vs luminal A using laser microdissection (LMD) for tumor cell enrichment. Simultaneous DNA, RNA, and protein extraction from LMD samples enables integrated WGS, RNA-seq, proteomics, and phosphoproteomics. Phosphoproteomic clusters distinguish high vs low relapse-risk basal-like tumors.

## Key points

- 117 breast tumors with LMD tumor cell enrichment
- LMD minimizes stromal contamination vs bulk
- DTBC-specific mutations (TP53, PLXNB3) and CNVs (1q21)
- Phosphoproteomic clusters predict basal-like relapse risk
- Simultaneous multi-omics from same LMD sample

## Project relevance

- LMD-based tumor enrichment for proteogenomics
- Phosphoproteomics for relapse risk prediction
- Multi-omics simultaneous extraction methodology

## Caveats

- Retrospective design
- LMD is labor-intensive for large-scale studies
- Untreated samples only; no treatment response data
