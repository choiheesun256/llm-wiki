# ESR1 Activating Mutations in Endocrine-Resistant Breast Cancer

## Summary

First identification of ESR1 ligand-binding domain (LBD) mutations (Y537S, D538G) in metastatic ER+ breast cancer through whole-exome sequencing. These mutations cause ligand-independent ER activation, explaining resistance to aromatase inhibitors. Mutations are absent in primary tumors, indicating acquired resistance during metastatic progression.

## Key points

- ESR1 LBD mutations in ~20% metastatic ER+ breast cancer
- Constitutive ER activation without estrogen
- Absent in primary tumors; acquired during treatment
- Key mechanism of endocrine therapy resistance

## Project relevance

- Landmark drug resistance mechanism discovery
- ESR1 mutations as treatment decision biomarker
- Foundation for SERD and combination therapy development

## Caveats

- Small discovery cohort (11 patients)
- Published 2013; mutation frequency refined by later studies
- Limited functional validation
