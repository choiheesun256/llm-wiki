# LICOB: Liver Cancer Organoid Biobank Pharmaco-Proteogenomics

## Summary

LICOB (Liver Cancer Organoid Biobank) establishes 65 organoids from 57 HCC/ICC patients with matched multi-omics (WES, RNA-seq, proteomics) and 129-drug screening. Proteogenomic subtypes correlate with drug sensitivity profiles. Temsirolimus + lenvatinib synergy identified in mTOR-active subgroup. Organoid drug responses correlate with patient clinical outcomes.

## Key points

- 65 liver cancer organoids retaining original tumor molecular features
- 129-drug screening linked to proteogenomic subtypes
- Temsirolimus + lenvatinib synergy in mTOR-active subgroup
- Organoid-to-patient response correlation validated

## Project relevance

- Organoid pharmaco-proteogenomics as precision oncology platform
- Combination therapy discovery (temsirolimus + lenvatinib)
- Molecular subtype-guided patient stratification for drug selection

## Caveats

- Organoids lack TME components (stroma, immune cells)
- Patient-organoid clinical correlation limited to small subset
- Mixed HCC/ICC cohort complicates subtype interpretation
- Long-term passage drift potential
