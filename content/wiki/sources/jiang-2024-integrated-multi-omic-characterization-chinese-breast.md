# CBCGA: Chinese Breast Cancer Multi-Omic Atlas

## Summary

CBCGA integrates 6 omics layers (genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, metabolomic, radiomic, digital pathology) across 773 Chinese breast cancer patients. Identifies AKT1 E17K enrichment in Asian populations and ferroptosis vulnerability in basal-like subtype. Metabolomic subtypes provide independent prognostic value beyond standard classifications. Most comprehensive multi-omic breast cancer characterization in an Asian cohort.

## Key points

- 773 patients with 6-layer multi-omics (most comprehensive Asian breast cancer atlas)
- AKT1 E17K mutation enriched in Chinese vs. Western cohorts
- Basal-like subtype shows GPX4-dependent ferroptosis vulnerability
- Metabolomic subtypes add independent prognostic information

## Project relevance

- Population-specific genomic landscape informing targeted therapy selection
- Ferroptosis as therapeutic strategy for basal-like breast cancer
- Multi-omic integration methodology (6 layers) as reference framework

## Caveats

- Chinese cohort-specific; pan-Asian/global generalization needed
- Ferroptosis vulnerability validated at cell line level only
- 6-layer integration challenging to reproduce at other institutions
- Radiomic features depend on institution-specific imaging protocols
