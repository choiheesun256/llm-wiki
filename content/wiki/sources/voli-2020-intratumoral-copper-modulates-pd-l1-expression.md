# Intratumoral copper modulates PD-L1 expression and influences tumor immune evasion

## Summary

A Cancer Research (2020) study demonstrating that intratumoral copper (Cu) levels regulate PD-L1 expression and influence tumor immune evasion. Copper transporter CTR-1 (SLC31A1) expression correlates with PD-L1 across TCGA cancer types. Mechanistically, copper supplementation upregulates PD-L1 via STAT3 and EGFR phosphorylation, while copper chelators (disulfiram/DC and TEPA) suppress STAT3/EGFR activity and trigger ubiquitin-mediated PD-L1 degradation. In vivo, chelator treatment increases CD8+ T cell and NK cell tumor infiltration and slows tumor growth. Clinical validation in a 180-patient neuroblastoma/brain tumor IHC cohort.

## Key points

- CTR-1 (copper importer SLC31A1) expression positively correlates with PD-L1 (CD274) across TCGA in a pan-cancer analysis
- Copper supplementation activates STAT3 and EGFR phosphorylation, transcriptionally upregulating PD-L1
- Copper chelators (disulfiram+copper/DC and TEPA) inhibit pSTAT3 and pEGFR, driving ubiquitin-mediated proteasomal PD-L1 degradation
- Chelator treatment increases CD8+ T cell and NK cell infiltration into tumors and reduces tumor growth in mouse models
- IHC in 180 neuroblastoma/brain tumor patients confirms CTR-1 high expression associates with PD-L1 high and worse prognosis
- Positions copper chelation as a drug repurposing strategy for immune checkpoint modulation

## Project relevance

Directly relevant to the drug repurposing project: disulfiram (already FDA-approved for alcohol dependence) and TEPA represent drug repurposing candidates that modulate PD-L1 via the copper-STAT3/EGFR axis. The CTR-1:PD-L1 correlation in TCGA is a network-based association connecting metal ion transport to immune checkpoint biology. The STAT3/EGFR mechanistic link also connects to existing targeted therapy combinations. Provides a concrete example of a metabolic-to-immune signaling axis exploitable for repurposing.

## Caveats

- Clinical cohort is restricted to pediatric tumors (neuroblastoma, brain tumors) — direct extrapolation to adult epithelial cancers requires caution
- Systemic effects of copper chelation on normal immune function and physiology not fully characterized
- Whether PD-L1 regulation is entirely STAT3/EGFR-dependent or involves additional pathways is unclear
- Disulfiram has pleiotropic mechanisms beyond copper chelation (proteasome inhibition, aldehyde dehydrogenase inhibition) making mechanism attribution difficult
- PD-L1 protein levels measured post-chelation do not account for tumor cell-intrinsic vs. immune cell-derived PD-L1
