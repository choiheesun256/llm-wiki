# circPPAPDC1A and Osimertinib Resistance (Tang 2024)

## Summary

circ_PPAPDC1A is significantly upregulated in NSCLC tissue from patients who acquired osimertinib resistance, identified by circRNA microarray of matched pre/post-resistance tissue pairs (n=5). Acting as a ceRNA (competing endogenous RNA), it sponges miR-30a-3p to derepress IGF1R expression, thereby activating the PI3K/AKT/mTOR bypass pathway. Silencing circ_PPAPDC1A partially reverses resistance in PC9/OR and HCC827/OR cell lines and in xenograft models. Dual-luciferase reporter assays confirm direct miR-30a-3p binding.

## Key points

- circ_PPAPDC1A is upregulated in matched patient tissues from before vs after acquisition of osimertinib resistance
- ceRNA mechanism: circ_PPAPDC1A sponges miR-30a-3p → IGF1R upregulation → PI3K/AKT/mTOR activation
- IGF1R-driven bypass signaling operates independently of EGFR, enabling resistance despite third-generation TKI blockade
- Knockdown of circ_PPAPDC1A re-sensitizes resistant cells both in vitro and in vivo

## Project relevance

- circRNA-mediated ceRNA is a non-genomic, post-transcriptional resistance mechanism relevant to EGFR-mutant NSCLC
- IGF1R/PI3K bypass is a recurrent resistance node; supports combination strategies targeting IGF1R alongside osimertinib
- Demonstrates the value of matched pre/post-resistance tissue profiling for discovering resistance drivers

## Caveats

- Only 5 patient samples; cohort is underpowered for biomarker validation
- In vivo contribution of the ceRNA axis is modest; xenograft models may not fully reflect the clinical TME
- Focused on a single circRNA; broader circRNA network contributions to resistance are unexplored
