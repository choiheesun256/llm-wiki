# T cell exhaustion proteostasis axis and E3 ligase rescue

## Summary

This Cell paper identifies proteostasis collapse as a key axis in terminally exhausted T cells and nominates NEURL3, RNF149, and WSB1 as E3 ligases that preserve progenitor-exhausted or tissue-resident-like function.

## Key points

- A 121-gene tissue-residency/progenitor-exhaustion signature is enriched for protein folding and degradation genes.
- Terminally exhausted T cells accumulate K48-polyubiquitin and unfolded proteins despite functional proteasome activity.
- NEURL3, RNF149, or WSB1 overexpression improves T-cell therapy response in tumour models.
- Triple-ligase overexpression with anti-PD-1 shows synergy in a checkpoint-resistant melanoma model.
- Human melanoma datasets support RNF149/WSB1 association with checkpoint response.

## Project relevance

- Direct bridge between cancer immunotherapy, proteostasis, E3 ligase biology, and target discovery.
- Suggests substrate mapping, rare immune-cell proteomics, and T_EX-specific degrader concepts as follow-up directions.
- Useful for NRL degrader biology and cancer TME immune-state work.

## Caveats

- Direct substrates of NEURL3/RNF149/WSB1 in CD8 T cells remain to be mapped.
- Adoptive-transfer models need careful translation to human TIL biology.
