# Proteogenomics of Early Duodenal Cancer

## Summary

Comprehensive proteogenomic analysis of 438 samples from 156 duodenal cancer patients covering 7 subtypes and multiple carcinogenesis stages. WES + proteomics + phosphoproteomics reveals stage-specific molecular events: LYN amplification (8q) drives MAPK signaling in neoplasia-infiltration transition; AARS1 identified as drug-targetable enzyme in immune-enriched DC progression.

## Key points

- 438 samples across 7 DC subtypes and carcinogenesis stages
- LYN amplification drives neoplasia-infiltration transition
- AARS1 as drug-targetable enzyme in DC progression
- Subtype-specific carcinogenesis molecular tracks

## Project relevance

- Stage-resolved proteogenomics methodology
- Rare cancer comprehensive molecular characterization
- Drug target identification through multi-omics integration

## Caveats

- Rare cancer with small subtype sample sizes
- Chinese population-specific findings possible
- FFPE sample quality variation
- Limited functional validation of AARS1 target
