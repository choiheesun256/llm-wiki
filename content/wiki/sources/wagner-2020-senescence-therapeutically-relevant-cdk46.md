# Senescence as a therapeutically relevant response to CDK4/6 inhibitors

## Summary

An Oncogene (2020) review from Imperial College London systematically covering senescence as a therapeutically relevant cellular response to CDK4/6 inhibitors (palbociclib, abemaciclib, ribociclib). The review delineates the molecular determinants that push cells toward senescence, quiescence, or apoptosis upon CDK4/6 inhibition, with RB status as the central gating factor. Key topics include the SASP (senescence-associated secretory phenotype) and its dual role in tumor microenvironment remodeling, established resistance mechanisms (RB1 loss, AKT1, RAS, AURKA, CCNE2, FGFR2), and combination strategies with MEK/PI3K inhibitors and senolytics.

## Key points

- CDK4/6 inhibitors block RB phosphorylation → E2F suppression → G1 arrest; downstream cell fate (senescence, quiescence, or apoptosis) is context-dependent
- Senescence is the predominant durable response in RB-proficient cells; RB1 loss is the clearest mechanism of primary and acquired resistance
- SASP comprises pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, IL-8), growth factors, and proteases — can paradoxically restimulate surviving tumor cells or recruit immune effectors
- Additional resistance mechanisms: CCNE2 amplification, AURKA overexpression, AKT1/RAS pathway activation, FGFR2 upregulation
- abemaciclib shows higher apoptosis-inducing potential than palbociclib/ribociclib, partly attributed to CDK2 co-inhibition
- Senolytics (e.g., navitoclax/ABT-263) combined with CDK4/6 inhibitors can clear senescent cells and reduce relapse potential

## Project relevance

Provides a comprehensive resistance mechanism catalog for CDK4/6 inhibitors — directly applicable to drug repurposing strategies aimed at overcoming or exploiting senescence. The SASP component list is a resource for identifying secondary drug targets that modulate the TME after CDK4/6 inhibitor treatment. The senolytic combination rationale is relevant for sequencing-based drug combination design. RB1 status as a biomarker informs patient stratification for CDK4/6 inhibitor use.

## Caveats

- Review article: inherits methodological heterogeneity and potential selection bias from cited primary studies
- The boundary between senescence and quiescence is experimentally ambiguous in some contexts; marker-based classification has limitations
- SASP effects on the TME are highly context-dependent (cancer type, immune status) — broad generalizations should be made cautiously
- Senolytic combination strategies are largely based on preclinical evidence; clinical translation is still limited
- Rapidly evolving field; newer resistance mechanisms and clinical trial data may have emerged since 2020
