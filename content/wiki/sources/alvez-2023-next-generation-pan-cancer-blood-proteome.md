# Pan-Cancer Blood Proteome Profiling (PEA)

## Summary

Pan-cancer plasma proteome profiling of 1,477 patients across 12 cancer types using Olink Explore 1536 Proximity Extension Assay (PEA), quantifying 1,463 proteins. Establishes the open-access Disease Blood Atlas resource and identifies cancer-specific protein signatures with machine learning classification models.

## Key points

- 1,477 patients, 12 cancer types, 1,463 plasma proteins
- Disease Blood Atlas as open-access resource
- Cancer-specific and shared protein signatures identified
- ML-based classification models per cancer type

## Project relevance

- Blood-based cancer biomarker discovery
- Pan-cancer proteomics comparison
- PEA technology for high-throughput plasma profiling

## Caveats

- PEA vs MS-based proteomics cross-validation lacking
- Pre-treatment samples only
- Swedish cohort; ethnic diversity limited
