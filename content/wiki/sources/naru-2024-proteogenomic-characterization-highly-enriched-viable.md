# AML Viable Leukemic Blast Proteogenomics

## Summary

Proteogenomic analysis of FACS-enriched viable leukemic blasts (VLBs) from AML patients reveals significant protein expression differences from bulk mononuclear cells. Identifies mutation-associated protein biomarkers (N=27 SWOG cohort) and detects mutated protein products by MS as potential neoantigen candidates. mRNA-protein correlation improves for mutation-associated genes.

## Key points

- VLB enrichment provides cleaner proteomic signatures than bulk
- Mutation-associated protein biomarkers identified
- MS-based detection of mutated protein products
- mRNA-protein correlation enhanced for mutated genes

## Project relevance

- Cell-type enrichment strategy for proteogenomics
- AML protein biomarker discovery
- MS-based neoantigen detection approach

## Caveats

- Small cohorts (3 + 27 patients)
- Limited mutational diversity in AML
- Neoantigen functional validation lacking
