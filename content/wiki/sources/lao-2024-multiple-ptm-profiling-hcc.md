# Multiple PTM Landscape in HCC

## Summary

Comprehensive profiling of 9 PTM types (phosphorylation, acetylation, crotonylation, ubiquitination, lactylation, succinylation, malonylation, N-glycosylation, β-hydroxybutyrylation) in 18 HCC patients using 4D-label free proteomics. Reveals phosphosites prefer intrinsically disordered regions while acylation sites prefer folded regions. Identifies NCL phosphorylation-acylation cross-talk in RNA splicing regulation.

## Key points

- 9 PTM types profiled simultaneously in 18 HCC patients
- Phosphosites in IDRs; acylated sites in folded regions
- NCL phospho-acylation cross-talk regulates RNA splicing
- Most PTMs upregulated in tumor vs. normal tissue

## Project relevance

- Multi-PTM proteomics methodology and cross-talk analysis
- PTM structural context (IDR vs. folded region)
- HCC therapeutic target identification through PTM landscape

## Caveats

- Small cohort (18 patients); HBV/non-HBV heterogeneity
- Antibody-based PTM enrichment introduces bias
- Functional validation limited to NCL
- HCC-specific findings may not generalize
