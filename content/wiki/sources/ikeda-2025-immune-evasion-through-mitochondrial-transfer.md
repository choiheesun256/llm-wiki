# Immune Evasion via Mitochondrial Transfer

## Summary

Cancer cells transfer mtDNA-mutated mitochondria to TILs through tunneling nanotubes and extracellular vesicles. Transferred mitochondria evade mitophagy and achieve homoplasmic replacement, causing metabolic abnormalities, senescence, and loss of effector/memory function in T cells. Tumor tissue mtDNA mutation presence is a poor prognostic factor for ICI response in melanoma and NSCLC patients.

## Key points

- 5/12 patients share identical mtDNA mutations between TILs and cancer cells
- Transfer via TNTs and small EVs; mitophagy-inhibitory molecules accompany transfer
- Homoplasmic replacement of T cell mitochondria impairs antitumor immunity
- mtDNA mutation status as a potential ICI response biomarker

## Project relevance

- Novel ICI resistance mechanism independent of PD-L1/TMB
- Blocking mitochondrial transfer (TNT/EV inhibitors) or restoring mitophagy as adjunct immunotherapy strategies
- mtDNA mutation as a non-genomic biomarker for treatment response

## Caveats

- Small initial cohort (12 patients); ICI prognostic cohort size not specified
- Unknown whether specific mtDNA mutations are required or any mutation suffices
- TNT/EV inhibitor safety and in vivo efficacy unvalidated
