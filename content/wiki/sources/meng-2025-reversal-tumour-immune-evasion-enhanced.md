# Dual-Functional RNA System for MHC-I Restoration

## Summary

Dual-functional RNA LNP system combining PCSK9-targeting siRNA and tumor antigen mRNA restores MHC-I antigen presentation in HCC. PCSK9 knockdown increases MHC-I surface expression ~6-fold. The system reprograms the TME with increased CD8+ T and NK cell infiltration, reduced M2 macrophages, and anti-tumor efficacy in orthotopic HCC and melanoma models.

## Key points

- PCSK9 as key MHC-I degradation mediator in HCC
- Dual siRNA+mRNA LNP system for simultaneous targeting
- TME reprogramming toward anti-tumor state
- Effective in multiple tumor models

## Project relevance

- Immune evasion through antigen presentation loss
- RNA therapeutics for cancer immunotherapy
- TME reprogramming strategies

## Caveats

- Mouse models; human immune differences
- LNP liver-tropic; limited to hepatic targets
- No clinical trial data
