# 229 Korean NSCLC Proteogenomics (Song 2024)

## Summary

Large-scale proteogenomic study of 229 Korean NSCLC patients integrating WGS, RNA-seq, TMT proteomics, and phosphoproteomics. Identifies five expression-based molecular subtypes (metabolic, alveolar-like, proliferative, hypoxic, immunogenic) via consensus clustering, each with distinct treatment response profiles. High EGFR mutation prevalence reflects East Asian patient demographics.

## Key points

- 229-patient Korean NSCLC cohort with WGS + RNA-seq + TMT proteomics + phosphoproteomics
- Five molecular subtypes identified: metabolic, alveolar-like, proliferative, hypoxic, immunogenic
- Subtype-specific differences in treatment response confirmed
- High EGFR mutation frequency characteristic of Korean/East Asian population

## Project relevance

- Multi-omics subtyping framework applicable to other cancer types
- Comparison reference for CPTAC-based studies in non-East-Asian populations
- Treatment response biomarkers linked to proteogenomic subtypes

## Caveats

- Single-ethnicity cohort; generalizability to other populations uncertain
- Retrospective design
- Some subtypes are small in size, limiting statistical robustness
