# C5aR1 Targeting Reverses PARPi Resistance

## Summary

C5aR1 complement receptor maintains pro-tumoral TAM states that mediate PARP inhibitor resistance in breast cancer. scRNA-seq identifies TAM_C3 (C5aR1-high) cluster enriched in PARPi-resistant tumors. C5aR1 inhibition reprograms TAMs and restores PARPi sensitivity in mouse syngeneic models. High C5aR1 in human breast cancer correlates with poor immunotherapy response.

## Key points

- TAM_C3 (C5aR1-high) mediates PARPi resistance
- C5aR1 inhibition reprograms TAMs and restores drug sensitivity
- PARPi resistance transferable through TME interaction
- C5aR1 as combination therapy target with PARPi

## Project relevance

- TME-mediated drug resistance mechanism
- TAM subtype identification via scRNA-seq
- Combination therapy rationale (C5aR1 + PARPi)

## Caveats

- Mouse syngeneic models; human TME differences
- No clinical C5aR1 inhibitor available
- Complement pathway complexity not fully addressed
- BRCA1/2-independent models; broader applicability unclear
