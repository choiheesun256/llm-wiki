# Procoagulant Platelets in TNBC Immune Evasion

## Summary

Discovers that procoagulant platelets deliver immune checkpoint molecules to TNBC tumors, misguiding immune cell responses. Tumor-released nucleic acids attract platelets via eTLRs to the aberrant tumor microvasculature. Blocking platelet-leukocyte interactions suppresses tumor progression nearly as effectively as systemic IC inhibition, suggesting a novel immunotherapeutic strategy without ICI side effects.

## Key points

- Procoagulant platelets deliver IC molecules to tumors
- Tumor nucleic acids recruit platelets via eTLRs
- Platelet-leukocyte blocking ≈ systemic ICI efficacy
- Novel immune evasion mechanism in TNBC

## Project relevance

- New immune evasion mechanism beyond canonical pathways
- Potential therapeutic target (platelet-tumor interactions)
- TNBC treatment strategy development

## Caveats

- Mouse model (4T1); human validation needed
- TNBC only; other cancer types unexplored
- Specific IC molecule contributions not fully dissected
