# ESCC Proteogenomics: Lipid Droplet and Progression

## Summary

Comprehensive proteogenomic characterization of 293 ESCC patients integrating WES, RNA-seq, proteomics, and phosphoproteomics. Reveals that lipid droplet formation (perilipin-2, apolipoprotein E) promotes ESCC tumorigenesis and progression by enhancing stromalization and suppressing T cell activation, leading to immune evasion. Chromosome 12q13.13 gain drives keratin upregulation associated with poor prognosis.

## Key points

- 293-patient ESCC multi-omics characterization
- APOBEC mutagenesis dominant in ESCC
- Lipid droplet formation promotes tumor progression
- LD-mediated T cell suppression drives immune evasion
- Chr12q13.13 gain → keratin → poor prognosis

## Project relevance

- Large-scale proteogenomics study design
- Lipid metabolism-cancer progression link
- Immune evasion mechanism discovery

## Caveats

- Chinese cohort; population-specific features
- Retrospective design
- ESCC-specific; other esophageal subtypes not included
