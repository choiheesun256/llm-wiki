# Macrophage-Mediated PARPi Resistance in BRCA1 TNBC

## Summary

Macrophages are the predominant immune cell in BRCA1-associated TNBC and mediate PARPi resistance through SREBF1-driven metabolic reprogramming. PARPi enhances both anti- and pro-tumor macrophage features. Combining PARPi with anti-CSF1R antibodies reprograms macrophages, enhances innate immunity and CD8+ T cell response, and extends survival in BRCA1-deficient mice.

## Key points

- Macrophage-mediated immune suppression as PARPi resistance liability
- SREBF1 pathway drives macrophage metabolic reprogramming
- PARPi + anti-CSF1R combination overcomes resistance
- CyCIF single-cell imaging of ~200,000 cells per specimen

## Project relevance

- TME-mediated drug resistance mechanism
- Macrophage targeting as combination strategy
- Metabolic reprogramming in immune cells

## Caveats

- BRCA1-specific; applicability to BRCA2/HRD uncertain
- Mouse model; human TME differences possible
- CSF1R inhibitors have shown limited clinical efficacy
