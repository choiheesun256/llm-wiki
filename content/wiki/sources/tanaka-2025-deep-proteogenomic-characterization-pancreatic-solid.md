# Pancreatic SPN Proteogenomics (Tanaka 2025)

## Summary

The first comprehensive proteogenomic characterization of pancreatic solid pseudopapillary neoplasm (SPN, n=13) compared with PDAC (n=11) and PanNET (n=10), using FFPE tissue MS proteomics (label-free, TopN DDA) and WES (MSK-IMPACT). SPN has a unique proteome enriched for lysosomal proteins and high MITF/TFE3 expression — more specific than currently used IHC markers. Metabolically, SPN favors fatty acid oxidation over glycolysis (opposite to PDAC). MHC class I expression is low. PDGFRA and ERBB2 are overexpressed, nominating RTK-targeted therapies as candidates. Findings are validated in a 285-sample IHC cohort.

## Key points

- SPN proteome is clearly distinct from PDAC and PanNET; lysosomal enrichment and MITF overexpression are defining features
- MITF is a more specific diagnostic marker for SPN than currently used IHC antibodies
- High fatty acid oxidation, low glycolysis — metabolic profile opposite to PDAC
- Immune-cold: low MHC class I expression, suggesting immune evasion
- PDGFRA and ERBB2 are overexpressed, providing actionable RTK targets for a tumor with no approved systemic therapy

## Project relevance

- FFPE-based proteomics methodology is applicable to archival tissue cohorts with limited sample availability
- RTK overexpression surfaced by proteomics in a genomically quiet rare tumor — illustrates added value over sequencing alone
- Lysosomal/MITF biology may intersect with autophagy-related drug resistance mechanisms relevant to the drug repurposing project

## Caveats

- Small cohort (13 SPN); statistical power is limited
- FFPE reduces proteome depth compared to fresh-frozen tissue
- No functional validation of PDGFRA/ERBB2 as therapeutic targets performed
