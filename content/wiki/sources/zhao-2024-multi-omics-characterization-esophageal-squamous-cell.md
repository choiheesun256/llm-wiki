# ESCC Multi-Omics: Molecular Subtypes and Therapeutic Targets

## Summary
This JCI Insight (2024) study performs comprehensive transcriptomic, proteomic, and phosphoproteomic analysis of 60 paired treatment-naive ESCC tumor and adjacent nontumor samples with 7-year follow-up. Unsupervised clustering of proteomics data identifies three molecular subtypes (S-I, S-II, S-III), with subtype III having the worst prognosis and enrichment in glycolysis and DNA repair proteins. Kinase-substrate network analysis from the phosphoproteome nominates CSNK2A1 as a therapeutic kinase target, validated in vitro and in vivo.

## Key points
- TMT-based isobaric quantification of proteome and phosphoproteome (LC-MS/MS): 9,261 proteins, 18,944 phosphosites
- RNA-seq: 19,615 mRNAs quantified; median mRNA-protein correlation 0.30 (tumor) vs 0.11 (adjacent normal)
- Three proteomic subtypes: S-I (best prognosis), S-II (intermediate), S-III (worst prognosis; enriched in glycolysis/DNA repair)
- TIMMDC1 (translocase of inner mitochondrial membrane) validated as independent prognostic biomarker
- Phosphoproteome-based kinase-substrate network identifies CSNK2A1 as a therapeutic target; confirmed by in vitro growth inhibition and in vivo mouse model
- mRNA-protein discordance enriched in cytochrome P450, pyruvate metabolism, glutathione metabolism, and glycolysis pathways

## Project relevance
- Kinase-substrate network-based drug target discovery methodology directly applicable to drug repurposing project
- CSNK2A1 validation workflow (phosphoproteome → kinase network → in vitro/in vivo) is a template for multi-omics-driven candidate drug target confirmation
- Molecular subtype stratification with long-term survival data provides a model for outcome-linked omics integration

## Caveats
- Single-institution Chinese cohort (n=60); geographic bias possible, external validation cohort needed
- Adjacent normal tissue may not represent true normal tissue
- Global phosphoproteome captures only a subset of all phosphorylation events
