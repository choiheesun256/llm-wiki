# Coordinated ecDNA Inheritance in Cancer

## Summary

Multiple ecDNA species encoding distinct oncogenes co-occur in cancer cells with correlated copy numbers. These species are coordinately inherited through mitotic co-segregation facilitated by intermolecular proximity and active transcription at mitosis onset. Computational modeling quantifies co-segregation and co-selection dynamics. TCGA analysis confirms widespread ecDNA co-occurrence (25%+ of ecDNA-positive tumors harbor multiple species).

## Key points

- ecDNA species on different chromosomes show correlated copy numbers in single cells (R=0.26-0.46)
- Transcription inhibition reduces co-segregation of distinct ecDNA species
- 37% of 41 clinical tumors show ecDNA signatures; 17% have co-amplified oncogene pairs
- Coordinated inheritance stabilizes oncogene cooperation and novel regulatory circuits

## Project relevance

- ecDNA cooperation drives multi-oncogene amplification relevant to drug resistance evolution
- Therapeutic strategy: jointly deplete cooperating ecDNA oncogenes
- Computational ecDNA dynamics model applicable to cancer evolution simulations

## Caveats

- ecDNA detection from WGS may underestimate true diversity
- Transcription-independent co-segregation mechanisms not fully explored
- Therapeutic ecDNA depletion strategies remain experimentally unproven
