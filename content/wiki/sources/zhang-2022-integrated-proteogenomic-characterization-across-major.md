# PitNET Proteogenomics (Cell Research 2022)

## Summary
The largest integrative multi-omics characterization of pituitary neuroendocrine tumors (PitNET) to date, covering 200 patients with WES, RNA-seq, proteomics, and phosphoproteomics. Proteomics-based clustering identified 7 molecular subtypes, including an invasive EMT-high subgroup. JAK-STAT pathway alterations were linked to immune exhaustion and infiltration, and potential therapeutic targets (CDK6, TWIST1, EGFR, VEGFR2) were proposed per subtype.

## Key points
- 200 PitNET tumors + 7 anterior pituitary gland (APG) controls; multi-omics integrated
- 10,011 proteins and 29,219 phosphosites identified
- GNAS copy number gain established as diagnostic marker for PIT1 lineage hyperproliferation
- Proteomics-based classification yields 7 clusters; EMT-high cluster = invasive subtype
- JAK1-STAT1-PDL1 axis changes associate with immune exhaustion; JAK3-STAT6-FOS/JUN with immune infiltration
- Findings validated in independent cohort of 750 PitNET patients

## Project relevance
- Multi-omics integration pipeline design applicable to drug repurposing projects
- Cluster-specific therapeutic target identification strategy is directly transferable
- Validation in an independent cohort provides a model for robust biomarker discovery
- Immune subtyping approach relevant to TME characterization in other cancer types

## Caveats
- Fresh-frozen tissue only; limits direct FFPE-based clinical application
- Pituitary-specific tumor type; cross-cancer generalization requires caution
- Therapeutic targets proposed without functional in vitro/in vivo validation
