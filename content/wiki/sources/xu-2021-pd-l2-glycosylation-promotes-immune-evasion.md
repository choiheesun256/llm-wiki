# PD-L2 Glycosylation and Immune Evasion

## Summary
JITC 2021 study demonstrating that N-glycosylation of PD-L2 promotes immune evasion in HNSCC by stabilizing the protein and enhancing PD-1 binding. Glycosylated PD-L2 resists degradation, maintains surface expression, and contributes to anti-PD-1 resistance. Targeting PD-L2 glycosylation restores T cell-mediated killing.

## Key points
- PD-L2 N-glycosylation stabilizes protein and enhances PD-1 binding
- Contributes to anti-PD-1 resistance beyond PD-L1
- HNSCC model demonstrates glycosylation-immune evasion link
- Glycosylation targeting restores T cell cytotoxicity

## Project relevance
- Glycosylation in immune checkpoint regulation
- Resistance mechanism for PD-1 blockade immunotherapy
- Combination therapy rationale (anti-PD-1 + glycosylation inhibitor)

## Caveats
- HNSCC-specific findings may not generalize
- In vitro and xenograft validation only
- Clinical translation needs further investigation
