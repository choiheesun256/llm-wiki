# ANGPTL4 as CRC Prevention Target

## Summary
JNCI 2025 study using cis-Mendelian randomization, colocalization, and observational analyses to evaluate lipid-perturbing drug targets (ANGPTL3, ANGPTL4, APOC3, CETP, PCSK9) in cancer risk. Genetically proxied ANGPTL4 inhibition reduces CRC risk (OR 0.76). Validated with pre-diagnostic protein measures in EPIC cohort and UK Biobank. ANGPTL4 E40K loss-of-function downregulates proliferation and EMT pathways in colon tissue.

## Key points
- ANGPTL4 inhibition reduces CRC risk (OR 0.76, MR + observational concordance)
- Lipid-perturbing drug targets systematically evaluated for cancer prevention
- ANGPTL4 E40K affects proliferation and EMT pathways in colon tissue
- Multi-method triangulation (MR, colocalization, transcriptomics) strengthens causality

## Project relevance
- Drug repurposing for cancer prevention methodology
- Proteogenomic MR framework for target identification
- Lipid-cancer metabolism link for network medicine

## Caveats
- CRC prevention focus (not treatment of established disease)
- European ancestry predominantly
- Observational and genetic evidence only (no RCT confirmation)
