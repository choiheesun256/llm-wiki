# PTM in Gastric Cancer (Review, Song 2025)

## Summary

Comprehensive review of the roles of protein post-translational modifications (PTMs) in gastric cancer. Covers ubiquitination, phosphorylation, acetylation, SUMOylation, and glycosylation, detailing how each PTM type contributes to tumor initiation, progression, and drug resistance. Also discusses PTMs as potential therapeutic targets.

## Key points

- Ubiquitination regulates degradation of tumor suppressors and oncoproteins
- Phosphorylation activates RTK/MAPK/PI3K signaling pathways
- Acetylation modulates both histone and non-histone proteins
- SUMOylation and glycosylation are linked to EMT and metastasis
- PTMs highlighted as druggable targets in gastric cancer

## Project relevance

- Overview of PTM landscape in gastric cancer for context-setting
- Drug resistance mechanisms mediated by PTMs relevant to drug repurposing projects
- Identifies PTM enzymes as potential therapeutic targets

## Caveats

- Review article; no new experimental data
- Gastric cancer-specific focus; limited applicability to pan-cancer PTM analysis
- No computational or systems-level analysis of PTM networks
