# Pediatric Brain Cancer Proteogenomics

## Summary

First large-scale proteogenomics analysis of 218 pediatric brain tumors across 7 histological types (CPTAC/Children's Brain Tumor Network). Proteomic clusters reveal actionable biological features spanning histological boundaries. Proteomics uncovers downstream effects of somatic mutations and CNVs not evident in transcriptomics. Kinase activity analysis identifies druggable targets across tumor types.

## Key points

- 218 tumors across 7 pediatric brain cancer histologies
- Proteomic clusters span histological boundaries
- Proteomics reveals functional effects invisible to transcriptomics
- Kinase activity profiling identifies druggable targets
- Diverse immune microenvironments characterized

## Project relevance

- CPTAC large-scale proteogenomics pipeline
- Cross-histology cancer characterization approach
- Kinase activity-based target discovery

## Caveats

- Pediatric-specific; may differ from adult brain cancers
- Bulk analysis; no single-cell resolution
- Treatment heterogeneity in cohort
