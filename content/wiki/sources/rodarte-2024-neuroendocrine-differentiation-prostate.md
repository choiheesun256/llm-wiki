# ASCL1 in Neuroendocrine Prostate Cancer Transition

## Summary

Demonstrates that ASCL1 is essential for neuroendocrine prostate cancer (NEPC) transition using RPM (Rb1/Trp53/Myc) GEM models. ASCL1 conditional knockout completely blocks NE differentiation, keeping tumors in an adenocarcinoma state. Identifies ASCL1 as a master regulator of lineage plasticity in prostate cancer.

## Key points

- ASCL1 essential for NE differentiation in prostate cancer
- Loss of ASCL1 completely blocks NE transition
- RPM GEM model with conditional knockout
- ASCL1 acts early in lineage plasticity program

## Project relevance

- Lineage plasticity mechanism in treatment resistance
- Potential therapeutic target for NEPC prevention
- Complementary to Romero-2024 (Nature Cancer)

## Caveats

- Mouse model findings; human validation limited
- Single TF focus; NEUROD1/POU2F3 subtypes not explored
- No therapeutic intervention tested
