# ESR1 Mutations in ctDNA During Breast Cancer Therapy

## Summary

Analysis of ESR1 mutations in ctDNA from 171 advanced ER+ breast cancer patients using ultrasensitive multiplex digital PCR. ESR1 mutations detected in 10.5% of patients, exclusively in ER-positive cases with prior aromatase inhibitor exposure. ESR1 mutations predict substantially shorter PFS on subsequent AI therapy (HR=3.1). Demonstrates clonal selection of ESR1 mutations during metastatic AI treatment.

## Key points

- ESR1 ctDNA prevalence 10.5% in advanced ER+ breast cancer
- 97% concordance between ctDNA and tumor biopsy
- ESR1 mutations predict AI resistance (HR=3.1)
- Metastatic AI exposure selects for ESR1 mutations (36.4% vs 5.8%)

## Project relevance

- Liquid biopsy for resistance monitoring
- ESR1 as predictive biomarker for treatment decisions
- Clonal evolution under therapeutic pressure

## Caveats

- 2015; more sensitive NGS methods since developed
- Single center (UK cohort)
- PFS only; OS data limited
