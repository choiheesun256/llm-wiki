# Vascular Normalization for Cancer Immunotherapy

## Summary

Review proposing low-dose anti-angiogenic therapy as a strategy to normalize abnormal tumor vasculature, thereby converting the immunosuppressive TME into an immunosupportive one. Abnormal vessels create hypoxia that polarizes TAMs to M2, recruits Tregs, and excludes effector T cells. Vascular normalization improves perfusion, reduces hypoxia, and enhances immune cell infiltration, with synergistic effects when combined with immunotherapy in preclinical models.

## Key points

- VEGF promotes local and systemic immunosuppression (DC maturation block, Treg expansion, MDSC accumulation)
- Low-dose anti-VEGF creates a "normalization window" with improved vessel function
- 10+ preclinical models show synergy between anti-angiogenic agents and immunotherapies
- Dose matters: high-dose anti-angiogenic causes excessive vessel pruning and worsens hypoxia

## Project relevance

- Conceptual framework for understanding anti-VEGF drug repurposing in combination with immunotherapy
- TME vascular phenotype as a consideration in multi-omics analysis of treatment response
- Normalization window concept relevant to drug dosing optimization

## Caveats

- 2013 review; substantial clinical data (IMbrave150, KEYNOTE-426) has since emerged
- Primarily preclinical evidence at time of publication
- Optimal dosing and timing of normalization window remains clinically challenging
