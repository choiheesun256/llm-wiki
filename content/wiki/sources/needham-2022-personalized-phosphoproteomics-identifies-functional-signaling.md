# Personalized Phosphoproteomics

## Summary

Introduces personalized phosphoproteomics framework that leverages inter-individual variance to identify functionally linked phosphosites. Applied to exercise and insulin signaling in human skeletal muscle, identifies >100 uncharacterized functional phosphosites and discovers cooperative mTOR-AMPK relationship at S377. Establishes a general approach for linking phosphorylation to biological function.

## Key points

- Individual phenotypic variance links phosphosites to function
- >100 previously uncharacterized functional phosphosites identified
- mTOR-AMPK cooperative regulation at S377 discovered
- General framework for any phosphoproteomics perturbation study

## Project relevance

- Novel phosphoproteomics analytical methodology
- Functional phosphosite identification approach
- Applicable to cancer signaling studies

## Caveats

- Only 5 subjects (small cohort)
- Skeletal muscle; not directly cancer tissue
- Exercise/insulin stimuli; cancer-specific perturbations needed
