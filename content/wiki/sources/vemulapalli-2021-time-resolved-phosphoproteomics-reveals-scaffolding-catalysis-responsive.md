# Time-resolved phosphoproteomics reveals scaffolding and catalysis-responsive patterns of SHP2-dependent signaling

## Summary

A time-resolved tyrosine phosphoproteomics study from Dana-Farber/Harvard (eLife 2021) characterizing SHP2 (PTPN11)-dependent signaling in EGFR-amplified breast cancer cells (MDA-MB-468). Using the allosteric SHP2 inhibitor SHP099 combined with EGF stimulation across multiple time points, the study monitored over 400 tyrosine residues and classified responses into six distinct temporal signatures. Key findings include identification of novel SHP2 substrates (occludin, ARHGAP35, PLCγ2) and a mechanism whereby SH2 domain scaffolding physically shields phosphosites from dephosphorylation, independent of catalytic activity.

## Key points

- Time-resolved TMT-based quantitative phosphoproteomics tracks >400 tyrosine residues at multiple time points post-EGF stimulation with or without SHP099 pre-treatment
- Six response clusters distinguish SHP2 catalysis-dependent, scaffolding-dependent, and mixed phosphorylation signatures
- Novel SHP2 substrates identified: occludin (tight junction protein), ARHGAP35 (Rho GTPase activating protein), PLCγ2
- SH2 domain scaffolding protects specific phosphosites from phosphatase access — a catalysis-independent signaling maintenance mechanism
- Some phosphosites increase upon SHP2 inhibition, revealing the complexity of SHP2 as both phosphatase and signal scaffold

## Project relevance

Provides methodological framework for time-resolved phosphoproteomics studies — applicable when studying dynamic signaling responses to perturbations in cancer cells. The scaffolding vs. catalytic function dissection approach is broadly useful for any dual-function signaling protein analysis. The novel substrate identification strategy (combining TMT-phosphoproteomics with allosteric inhibitors) is a reference for phosphoproteomics-based target discovery in drug repurposing contexts.

## Caveats

- Single cell line (MDA-MB-468) limits generalizability across cancer types and molecular contexts
- SHP099 is an allosteric inhibitor; complete separation of catalytic ablation from scaffolding preservation cannot be fully guaranteed
- Analysis restricted to tyrosine phosphoproteomics — serine/threonine signaling changes are not captured
- In vitro EGF stimulation conditions do not fully recapitulate the complex signaling environment of in vivo tumors
