# TKI Drug Mechanisms and Acquired Resistance Review

## Summary

Comprehensive review of protein tyrosine kinase (PTK) roles in cancer, TKI drug mechanisms, and acquired resistance. Covers RTKs (EGFR, VEGFR, PDGFR, ALK) and NRTKs (Abl, Src). Catalogues 20+ FDA-approved TKIs (2001-2017) with targets and indications. Summarizes resistance mechanisms: target mutations (e.g., EGFR T790M), bypass signaling, downstream pathway activation, and phenotype switching (EMT).

## Key points

- 20+ FDA-approved TKIs catalogued with targets and indications
- Common resistance: gatekeeper mutations, bypass pathway activation, EMT
- PI3K/AKT/mTOR and RAS/RAF/MEK/ERK as key downstream pathways
- Combination strategies proposed to overcome resistance

## Project relevance

- Reference for TKI resistance mechanisms across kinase families
- Drug-disease mapping from Table 1 (TKI targets and indications)
- Framework for understanding resistance patterns in drug repurposing context

## Caveats

- 2018 review; newer TKIs and resistance data not included
- Broad overview rather than in-depth mechanistic analysis
- Limited clinical trial evidence for resistance-overcoming strategies
