# DSRCT Multi-Layered Molecular Profiling

## Summary

Multi-layered molecular profiling of 30 refractory DSRCT patients within the MASTER precision oncology program using WGS/WES, RNA-seq, DNA methylation, proteomics, and phosphoproteomics. Corrects diagnosis in 27% of cases, provides treatment recommendations for 93%, and identifies T-DXd as effective therapy based on ERBB2 overexpression, with durable responses lasting 24+ months.

## Key points

- 30 DSRCT patients with comprehensive molecular profiling
- 27% diagnostic correction through molecular characterization
- 93% received molecular-based treatment recommendations
- ERBB2 overexpression enables T-DXd therapy
- Durable T-DXd responses (24+ months)

## Project relevance

- Precision oncology for ultra-rare cancers
- Multi-omics clinical utility demonstration
- Drug repurposing (T-DXd) for rare cancer

## Caveats

- Small cohort (30 patients; ultra-rare cancer)
- Proteomics only in 9/30 patients
- Retrospective; prospective trial needed
