# KRAS G12C-EGFR Inhibitor Resistance in CRC

## Summary
Cancer Discovery 2023 study characterizing acquired resistance to KRAS G12C + EGFR inhibitor combination in CRC. Most resistance alterations are subclonal and prevent ERK inhibition (KRAS amplification, BRAF/MEK mutations, RTK activation). KRAS G12C amplification is the dominant clonal mechanism. Upon drug withdrawal, resistant cells undergo oncogene-induced senescence, creating a senolytic therapeutic vulnerability.

## Key points
- KRAS G12C amplification is the recurrent clonal resistance mechanism
- Most alterations are subclonal, detected at low VAF via ctDNA
- Drug withdrawal induces senescence vulnerability in resistant cells
- ctDNA dynamics enable real-time tracking of resistance evolution

## Project relevance
- KRAS-targeted therapy resistance landscape for drug repurposing
- Senolytic combination strategy as novel therapeutic approach
- Liquid biopsy methodology for resistance monitoring

## Caveats
- CRC-specific findings (may differ from NSCLC)
- Small patient cohort for ctDNA analysis
- Senolytic approach remains preclinical
