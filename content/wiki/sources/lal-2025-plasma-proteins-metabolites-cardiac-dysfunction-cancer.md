# Plasma Biomarkers for Cancer Therapy Cardiotoxicity

## Summary

Multi-omics study of 52 pan-cancer patients identifying plasma protein (Olink, 92 proteins) and metabolite biomarkers for cancer therapy-related left ventricular dysfunction (LVD). Identifies 13 proteins (ST2, TNFRSF14, OPN, MMP-2) and 14 metabolites associated with LVD. ML classification models predict cardiac dysfunction from combined proteomic-metabolomic features.

## Key points

- 13 plasma proteins and 14 metabolites associated with LVD in cancer patients
- Inflammation/chemotaxis markers (ST2, OPN, MMP-2) predominant
- ML models predict LVD from protein + metabolite features
- Sex-specific associations identified

## Project relevance

- Multi-omics + ML integration for clinical biomarker discovery
- Non-invasive prediction of cancer therapy toxicity
- Olink targeted proteomics platform application

## Caveats

- Very small sample (52 patients, 12 LVD cases)
- Cross-sectional design; no longitudinal monitoring
- Limited to 92-protein Olink panel
- Single-center study
