# SAHA PTM Crosstalk in Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma

## Summary

Multi-omics profiling (proteome, phosphoproteome, acetylome) of SAHA/vorinostat-treated NPC 5-8F cells reveals extensive crosstalk between acetylation and phosphorylation. Quantifies 6,491 proteins, 2,456 phosphoproteins, and 228 acetylated proteins, identifying 46 dual-modified proteins. SAHA reprograms glycolysis, EGFR, and Myc signaling pathways through coordinated PTM changes.

## Key points

- HDAC inhibition alters phosphorylation broadly, not just acetylation
- 46 dual acetylated/phosphorylated proteins (WSTF, LMNA) as crosstalk nodes
- TMT-based integrated proteome+phospho+acetylome workflow
- Glycolysis and EGFR signaling among key SAHA-responsive pathways

## Project relevance

- Demonstrates PTM crosstalk as a drug mechanism beyond primary target
- TMT multi-layer PTM workflow applicable to other drug perturbation studies
- HDAC inhibitor mechanism understanding for drug resistance/combination strategies

## Caveats

- Single cell line (5-8F); no in vivo validation
- NPC-specific with EBV biology
- Relatively small acetylome coverage (228 proteins)
