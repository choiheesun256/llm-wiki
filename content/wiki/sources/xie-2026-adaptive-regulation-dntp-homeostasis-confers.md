# dNTP Homeostasis and Osimertinib Resistance

## Summary
Cancer Research 2026 study revealing that adaptive dNTP pool regulation confers osimertinib resistance in EGFR-mutant NSCLC. Resistant cells upregulate RRM2 (ribonucleotide reductase) and downregulate SAMHD1 (dNTP hydrolase), expanding dNTP pools to support proliferation under EGFR inhibition. Targeting dNTP metabolism re-sensitizes resistant tumors.

## Key points
- dNTP homeostasis adaptation drives osimertinib resistance
- RRM2 upregulation and SAMHD1 downregulation expand dNTP pools in resistant cells
- Metabolic bypass of EGFR inhibition rather than pathway reactivation
- dNTP metabolism as actionable therapeutic target for combination therapy

## Project relevance
- Novel metabolic resistance mechanism for EGFR-TKI
- EGFR-TKI resistance biology informing drug repurposing strategies
- Combination therapy rationale (osimertinib + RRM2 inhibitor)

## Caveats
- Single resistance mechanism focus
- Preclinical stage (cell lines and xenografts)
- dNTP targeting selectivity concerns for normal proliferating cells
