# Early-Onset Diffuse GC Proteogenomics

## Summary

Proteogenomic analysis of diffuse-type gastric cancers in young patients integrating exome-seq, RNA-seq, proteomics, phosphoproteomics, and N-glycoproteomics. Identifies four molecular subtypes (proliferation, immune response, metabolism, invasion) where phosphorylation and N-glycosylation data are essential for subtype discrimination beyond genomic/transcriptomic analysis.

## Key points

- Five-layer omics integration including N-glycoproteomics
- Four subtypes distinguishable mainly by proteomic data
- Mutation-phosphorylation correlations link genotype to signaling
- mRNA-protein abundance correlations identify prognostic genes

## Project relevance

- Multi-PTM proteogenomics approach
- Gastric cancer molecular subtyping
- N-glycosylation in cancer classification

## Caveats

- Diffuse-type only; intestinal-type not included
- Young patient cohort; may differ from elderly
- Korean cohort; ethnic validation needed
