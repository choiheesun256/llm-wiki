## Summary

Cancer Cell 2024 study using integrative proteomic, transcriptomic, and epigenomic analysis to identify cell surface immunotherapeutic targets in neuroblastoma. MS-based surfaceome profiling of 9 cell lines and 12 xenografts identified 7,172 and 6,831 proteins respectively. A multi-omic prioritization pipeline yielded 60 high-confidence surface protein candidates. DLK1 (Delta-like canonical Notch ligand 1) was prioritized based on super-enhancer-driven expression and high RNA-protein correlation (R=0.97). DLK1 silencing induced cellular differentiation. ADCT-701, a DLK1-targeting ADC, showed potent and specific anti-tumor activity in xenograft models. DLK1 expression was also confirmed in SCLC and ACC.

## Key points

- Multi-omic surfaceome pipeline (proteomics + transcriptomics + H3K27ac ChIP-seq) is a scalable framework for immunotherapy target discovery
- Super-enhancer-driven expression of DLK1 implies tumor-selective and stable expression — favorable for therapeutic targeting
- RNA-protein correlation of R=0.97 for DLK1 means transcriptomic datasets can reliably proxy protein-level expression
- DLK1 silencing reverses malignant phenotype (differentiation induction), suggesting functional oncogenic dependency
- ADCT-701 efficacy in xenografts provides preclinical proof-of-concept for DLK1 as an ADC target
- Cross-cancer expression in SCLC and ACC broadens the potential indication scope

## Project relevance

Highly relevant to drug repurposing and target identification methodology. The multi-omic prioritization pipeline — combining surface proteomics, transcriptomics, and super-enhancer mapping — is a transferable framework for identifying druggable targets in other cancer types. DLK1 as a Notch ligand also connects to developmental signaling pathways relevant to network medicine approaches. The ADC strategy and target-selection criteria are directly informative for preclinical target validation workflows.

## Caveats

- Surfaceome data derived from cell lines and xenografts; primary patient tumor tissue not directly profiled
- ADC therapeutic window and toxicity profile require clinical-stage validation
- Stability of super-enhancer-driven DLK1 expression under treatment pressure or in tumor subpopulations is unclear
- DLK1 expression in SCLC and ACC is reported but not functionally validated in those contexts
- Cut-off parameters in the multi-omic prioritization pipeline need further benchmarking for generalizability
