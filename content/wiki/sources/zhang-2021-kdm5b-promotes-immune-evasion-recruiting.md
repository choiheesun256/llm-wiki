# KDM5B Promotes Immune Evasion by Silencing Retroelements

## Summary
Nature 2021 study showing KDM5B (H3K4 demethylase) promotes immune evasion in melanoma by recruiting SETDB1 (H3K9 methyltransferase) to silence endogenous retroelements like MMVL30. KDM5B depletion derepresses retroelements, activating cytosolic RNA/DNA sensing and type I interferon response, leading to tumor rejection and immune memory. KDM5B loss enhances responses to immune checkpoint blockade.

## Key points
- KDM5B silences retroelements via SETDB1 recruitment
- Derepression activates innate immune sensing (RIG-I, cGAS)
- Type I IFN response induces tumor rejection
- Enhances ICB response in melanoma

## Project relevance
- Epigenetic immune evasion mechanism
- Therapeutic targeting of chromatin regulators
- Viral mimicry pathway

## Caveats
- Melanoma mouse models
- KDM5B has broad epigenetic functions
- Translation to human tumors needs validation
