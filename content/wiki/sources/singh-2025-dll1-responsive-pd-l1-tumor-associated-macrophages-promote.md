# DLL1/PD-L1+ TAMs in Endocrine Resistance

## Summary

Identifies DLL1-responsive PD-L1+ M2-like tumor-associated macrophages as critical mediators of endocrine therapy resistance in ER+ breast cancer. DLL1 (Notch ligand) on luminal tumor cells recruits TAMs via CCR3/CCL7, which maintain cancer stem cell activity. Combination of anti-DLL1 and anti-PD-L1 antibodies overcomes resistance in preclinical mouse models and patient-derived explants.

## Key points

- PD-L1+ M2 TAMs enriched in endocrine-resistant tumors
- DLL1 recruits TAMs via CCR3/CCL7 axis
- TAMs maintain cancer stem cell properties
- Anti-DLL1 + anti-PD-L1 overcomes resistance

## Project relevance

- TME-mediated drug resistance mechanism
- Immunotherapy for ER+ breast cancer
- DLL1-Notch signaling in resistance

## Caveats

- Small patient cohort (24 patients)
- Mouse models; incomplete human TME
- ER+ breast cancer only
