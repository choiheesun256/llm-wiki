# SigPath: Multiplexed Quantitative Phosphosite Assay

## Summary

SigPath is an immuno-MRM targeted MS panel quantifying 284 phosphosites across 200+ cancer-relevant proteins. Covers major signaling pathways (RTK, PI3K/AKT/mTOR, MAPK, DNA damage, cell cycle). Demonstrates <20% CV reproducibility, applicability to FFPE tissue, and sensitive detection of drug-induced signaling changes. Platform for kinase inhibitor response profiling and clinical biomarker discovery.

## Key points

- 284 phosphosites across 200+ proteins in single targeted MS run
- CV <20% reproducibility; applicable to FFPE tissue
- Detects drug-induced pathway-level signaling changes
- Covers RTK, PI3K/AKT, MAPK, cell cycle pathways

## Project relevance

- Targeted phosphoproteomics for drug response monitoring
- Cancer signaling pathway quantification methodology
- Clinical biomarker development platform

## Caveats

- Limited to 284 pre-selected phosphosites (no discovery)
- Immunoaffinity enrichment adds cost and complexity
- Relative quantitation only
- Biased toward known cancer signaling pathways
