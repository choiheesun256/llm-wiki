# Spatial Transcriptomics of Primary and Metastatic PDAC

## Summary

Visium FFPE spatial transcriptomics of 30 specimens from 13 PDAC patients (primary + matched liver/lung metastases). Defines spatial ecotypes combining tumor cell states and TME composition. Reveals significant intra-tumoral heterogeneity, distinct TME compositions between primary and metastatic sites, and spatially organized CAF subtypes associated with specific tumor cell states.

## Key points

- Visium FFPE on 30 PDAC specimens (primary + metastatic)
- Spatial ecotypes defined by recurring tumor-TME patterns
- Metastases show altered TME vs. primaries
- Intra-tumoral heterogeneity exceeds inter-tumoral in many cases

## Project relevance

- Spatial transcriptomics methodology for solid tumors
- TME spatial organization in metastatic progression
- Ecotype framework for tumor heterogeneity characterization

## Caveats

- Small cohort (13 patients)
- Visium spot-level resolution requires deconvolution
- FFPE RNA quality limitations
- Variable treatment histories across patients
