# IGF2BP3-COX6B2 Metabolic Reprogramming in EGFR-TKI Resistance

## Summary

IGF2BP3 m6A reader protein stabilizes COX6B2 mRNA to increase oxidative phosphorylation (OXPHOS), driving acquired EGFR-TKI resistance in NSCLC. The metabolic switch from Warburg phenotype to OXPHOS dependence represents a targetable vulnerability. OXPHOS inhibitor IACS-010759 suppresses resistant tumor growth in vitro and in PDX models.

## Key points

- IGF2BP3-COX6B2 axis links epitranscriptomics to metabolic reprogramming
- Warburg-to-OXPHOS switch drives TKI resistance
- OXPHOS inhibition overcomes acquired resistance in PDX
- Nicotinamide metabolism regulated by this axis

## Project relevance

- Drug resistance mechanism through metabolic reprogramming
- m6A-dependent post-transcriptional regulation in cancer
- Therapeutic target identification for overcoming TKI resistance

## Caveats

- Limited to gefitinib/osimertinib; other TKI resistance not tested
- PDX model lacks immune microenvironment
- OXPHOS inhibitor clinical applicability uncertain
