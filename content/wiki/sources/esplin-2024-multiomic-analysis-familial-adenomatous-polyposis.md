# Multiomic FAP Early Tumorigenesis

## Summary

Deep multiomic profiling (transcriptome, proteome, metabolome, lipidome) of 93 colorectal samples from 6 FAP patients reveals molecular pathways activated during the mucosa-to-polyp-to-dysplasia transition. Arachidonic acid pathway activation in early hyperplasia provides mechanistic rationale for NSAID chemoprevention.

## Key points

- Four-omics simultaneous profiling across precancerous stages (normal, benign polyp, dysplastic polyp)
- Different omics layers dominate at different transition stages
- Arachidonic acid pathway is the earliest activated targetable pathway
- HTAN resource with rich open-access multiomics data

## Project relevance

- Model for multi-omic integration in early cancer biology
- Directly relevant to drug repurposing (aspirin/NSAID mechanism for CRC prevention)
- MOFA-based cross-omics analysis methodology applicable to other cancer types

## Caveats

- FAP-specific (hereditary CRC); generalization to sporadic CRC requires caution
- Cross-sectional design, no true temporal tracking
