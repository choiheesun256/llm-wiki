# CTLA4 Overcomes KEAP1/STK11 Resistance

## Summary

Nature 2024 study demonstrating that dual immune checkpoint blockade (anti-CTLA4 + anti-PD-L1) overcomes KEAP1/STK11 mutation-driven resistance to PD-(L)1 inhibitors in NSCLC. Analysis of 871 retrospective patients and the POSEIDON phase III trial (1,013 patients) shows tremelimumab addition doubles OS in the STK11/KEAP1 subgroup. KEAP1 loss identified as the strongest genomic predictor of PD-(L)1 resistance.

## Key points

- KEAP1 mutations: strongest genomic predictor of PD-(L)1i resistance
- Dual ICB restores benefit in STK11/KEAP1 mutant NSCLC
- POSEIDON trial: OS doubled with tremelimumab in this subgroup
- Myeloid reprogramming to iNOS+ tumoricidal phenotype

## Project relevance

- Biomarker-guided immunotherapy strategy
- Overcoming immunotherapy resistance
- KRAS-mutant lung cancer treatment

## Caveats

- Post-hoc subgroup analysis; prospective validation needed
- STK11/KEAP1 mutations in ~20% NSCLC
- Dual ICB increases toxicity
