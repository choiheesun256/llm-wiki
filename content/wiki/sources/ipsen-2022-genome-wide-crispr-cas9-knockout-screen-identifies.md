# CRISPR Screen for PARPi Resistance in Prostate Cancer

## Summary

Genome-wide CRISPR-Cas9 KO screen in CRPC C4 cells identifies PARP1 and six novel olaparib resistance genes. Validation confirms ARH3, YWHAE, and UBR5 as resistance modulators. PARP1/ARH3 knockout causes cross-resistance to other PARPis (not carboplatin) through reduced autophagy. Pharmacological autophagy induction partially reverts resistance. Low ARH3 expression independently predicts recurrence in 126 prostate cancer patients.

## Key points

- Brunello v2 library screen (77,441 gRNAs) under olaparib IC50/IC90 selection
- PARP1, ARH3, YWHAE, UBR5 validated as resistance genes in C4 and LNCaP
- Novel mechanism: decreased autophagy drives PARPi resistance
- ARH3 as a potential biomarker for PARPi sensitivity

## Project relevance

- Autophagy modulators as combination partners with PARPi
- ARH3 expression for patient selection in PARPi treatment
- CRISPR screen + MAGeCK as a reproducible pipeline for drug resistance gene discovery

## Caveats

- CRPC cell lines (C4/LNCaP) are HR-proficient; may differ from BRCA-mutant contexts
- In vitro only; no in vivo validation of resistance phenotype
- ARH3 clinical validation from recurrence, not PARPi-treated cohort
