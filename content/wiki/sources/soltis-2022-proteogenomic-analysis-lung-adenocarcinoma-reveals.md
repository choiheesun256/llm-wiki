# LUAD Proteogenomics (APOLLO)

## Summary

Deep proteogenomic profiling of 87 lung adenocarcinomas from the US-based APOLLO network using WGS, RNA-seq, proteomics (7,614 proteins), and phosphoproteomics (10,093 phosphosites). Identifies three genomic subtypes linked to smoking history. Demonstrates that protein expression is more frequently associated with patient survival than RNA. Characterizes three expression subtypes with divergent mutations and therapeutic vulnerabilities.

## Key points

- 87 LUAD with WGS, RNA-seq, proteomics, phosphoproteomics
- 3 genomic subtypes by mutational signatures (smoking-related)
- Protein expression better predicts survival than RNA
- Phosphoproteomic networks reveal subtype-specific vulnerabilities

## Project relevance

- US smoker cohort complementing CPTAC
- Protein > RNA for clinical outcome prediction
- Subtype-specific therapeutic strategies

## Caveats

- Moderate cohort (87 patients)
- US population; smoking-biased
- No independent validation cohort
