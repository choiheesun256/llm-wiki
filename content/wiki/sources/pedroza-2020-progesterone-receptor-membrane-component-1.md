# PGRMC1 in Breast Cancer Growth

## Summary

Demonstrates that PGRMC1 overexpression promotes breast cancer growth in both ER-positive and TNBC cells by altering the phosphoproteome and augmenting EGFR/PI3K/AKT/mTOR signaling. PGRMC1 inhibition (siRNA or AG-205) reduces proliferation, induces cell cycle arrest and apoptosis, and decreases invasion/migration. Proposes PGRMC1 as a therapeutic target across breast cancer subtypes.

## Key points

- PGRMC1 overexpressed in breast tumors across subtypes
- Inhibition induces cell cycle arrest and apoptosis
- Phosphoproteome altered: PI3K/AKT/mTOR and EGFR pathways
- AG-205 (PGRMC1 inhibitor) as potential therapeutic
- Common target for ER+ and TNBC

## Project relevance

- Phosphoproteome-level drug target characterization
- PI3K/AKT/EGFR pathway crosstalk
- Breast cancer therapeutic target discovery

## Caveats

- In vitro only; no in vivo validation
- Antibody array, not MS-based phosphoproteomics
- Direct vs indirect signaling effects unclear
