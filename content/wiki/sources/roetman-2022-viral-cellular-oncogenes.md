# Viral and Cellular Oncogenes in Immune Evasion

## Summary

Review of how viral (HPV E6/E7, EBV LMP1) and cellular (MYC, KRAS, EGFR, BRAF) oncogenes directly promote immune evasion through MHC-I downregulation, PD-L1 upregulation, cytokine modulation, and immune cell exclusion. Highlights convergent mechanisms between viral and cellular oncogenes and the rationale for combining targeted therapy with immunotherapy.

## Key points

- Oncogenes directly regulate immune evasion programs
- MYC upregulates PD-L1 and CD47
- RAS/MAPK modulates cytokines and excludes immune cells
- Convergent mechanisms between viral and cellular oncogenes

## Project relevance

- Oncogene-immune crosstalk conceptual framework
- Rationale for targeted therapy + immunotherapy combinations
- Drug repurposing context for immune modulation

## Caveats

- Review article; no new experimental data
- Published 2022; rapidly evolving field
