# Citron Kinase in Prostate Cancer

## Summary

Identifies citron kinase (CIT) as a pivotal regulator of prostate cancer growth and treatment resistance. CIT overexpression is controlled by E2F2-Skp2-p27 axis and confers resistance to androgen-targeted therapies. CIT kinase activity drives diverse substrates involved in proliferation and splicing. Multikinase inhibitor OTS-167 inhibits CIT and decreases treatment-resistant prostate cancer growth.

## Key points

- CIT overexpressed in prostate cancer, drives proliferation
- E2F2-Skp2-p27 axis controls CIT expression
- CIT confers androgen-targeted therapy resistance
- CIT substrates span proliferation and splicing
- OTS-167 inhibits treatment-resistant PCa growth

## Project relevance

- Novel druggable kinase in prostate cancer
- Treatment resistance mechanism
- Phosphoproteomics-based substrate discovery

## Caveats

- OTS-167 is a multikinase inhibitor; off-target effects possible
- Xenograft models; no immunocompetent models
- Clinical validation lacking
