# PhosphoSitePlus 2014

## Summary

PhosphoSitePlus (PSP) is a curated knowledgebase of mammalian post-translational modifications containing 330,000+ non-redundant sites (phospho, acetyl, ubiquityl, methyl). The 2014 update introduces PTMVar (25,000+ missense mutations at PTM sites that can rewire signaling), a Regulatory Sites dataset, recalibrated HTP MS data with stricter quality thresholds, and 12,180 kinase-substrate interactions.

## Key points

- 338,948 non-redundant PTM sites across 20,021 human proteins
- PTMVar maps cancer/disease mutations onto PTM sites to identify signaling rewiring events
- 95%+ sites from mass spectrometry; early data recalibrated against 1M+ spectra
- 12,180 kinase-substrate interactions with sequence logos

## Project relevance

- Essential reference for phosphoproteomics analysis in proteogenomics papers
- PTMVar connects somatic mutations to signaling consequences — useful for driver mutation interpretation
- Quality thresholds (P < 0.05, Ascore > 13) serve as benchmarks for phosphoproteomics QC

## Caveats

- 2014 snapshot; current PSP has substantially expanded coverage
- MS-biased toward certain PTM types
- PTMVar functional impact is largely predicted, not experimentally validated
