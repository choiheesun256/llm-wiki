# ITCH-mediated PD-L1 Degradation in MAPKi Resistance

## Summary
Cancer Discovery 2022 study showing ITCH E3 ligase ubiquitinates and degrades tumor-surface PD-L1/L2 in MAPKi-treated melanoma, promoting T cell activation and suppressing acquired resistance. MAPKi induces PD-L1 accumulation driving immune evasion; a small-molecule ITCH activator enhances PD-L1 degradation and prolongs antitumor response in vivo.

## Key points
- ITCH E3 ligase ubiquitinates PD-L1 for proteasomal degradation
- MAPKi-induced PD-L1 accumulation drives immune evasion and resistance
- Small-molecule ITCH activator prolongs MAPKi therapeutic response
- In vivo validation in melanoma and KRAS-mutant models

## Project relevance
- Ubiquitin-mediated immune checkpoint regulation
- MAPKi resistance mechanism with immunological basis
- Small-molecule immune modulation beyond antibodies

## Caveats
- Melanoma-focused (generalizability uncertain)
- ITCH activator is an early-stage compound
- PD-L1 is not the sole resistance mechanism
