# MAP3K1/MAP2K4 Breast Cancer Driver Mutations

## Summary

Establishes MAP3K1 and MAP2K4 as key tumor suppressors in ER+ breast cancer operating via the JNK2-p53-FOSL1 axis. Loss-of-function mutations in these genes are mutually exclusive with TP53 alterations, mechanistically compensating for p53 loss by reducing JNK2 phosphorylation, impairing p53 activation, and upregulating FOSL1/FRA1 to promote proliferation and metastasis.

## Key points

- MAP3K1/MAP2K4 loss-of-function mutations enriched in ER+ breast cancer
- Mutual exclusivity with TP53 alterations confirmed in TCGA and METABRIC
- MKK4DN expression enhances xenograft tumor growth and lung metastasis
- Pan-cancer CPTAC analysis shows compensatory FOSL1 regulation in moderate-TP53-mutation tumors

## Project relevance

- JNK pathway activators or FOSL1 inhibitors as potential therapeutic strategies
- Mutual exclusivity patterns useful for patient stratification and synthetic lethality discovery
- Demonstrates CPTAC phosphoproteomics reuse for pathway-level validation

## Caveats

- Focused on ER+ breast cancer; applicability to other subtypes untested
- No direct FOSL1 inhibitor currently available for clinical use
- In vitro/xenograft validation only; no prospective clinical data
