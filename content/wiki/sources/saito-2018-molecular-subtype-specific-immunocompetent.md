# Bladder Cancer Molecular Subtype GEM Models

## Summary

Development of molecular subtype-specific immunocompetent GEM models for bladder cancer: UPPL (luminal) and BBN (basal). Basal subtype shows higher immune infiltration and responds to anti-PD-1 therapy, while luminal subtype is immune-cold and resistant. Models recapitulate human bladder cancer molecular subtypes and predict immunotherapy response.

## Key points

- UPPL (luminal) vs BBN (basal) subtype-specific models
- Basal: immune-hot, responds to anti-PD-1
- Luminal: immune-cold, resistant to immunotherapy
- Models recapitulate human molecular subtypes

## Project relevance

- Subtype-specific immunotherapy response prediction
- Immunocompetent cancer models for drug testing
- Molecular subtype-immune microenvironment link

## Caveats

- Mouse models; incomplete human recapitulation
- Published 2018; newer combination therapies not tested
- Single-agent immunotherapy only
