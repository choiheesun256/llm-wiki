# CAF-Stemness Classification in HCC Liver Transplant

## Summary

Multi-omic analysis (RNA-seq, proteomics, ATAC-seq) of 122 HCC liver transplant recipients beyond Milan criteria identifies three transcriptomic subgroups with distinct survival outcomes. Cancer stemness signature driven by CAF-derived CXCL12 via CXCR4/XRCC5 axis dominates the high-risk group. Validated in independent 80-patient cohort.

## Key points

- Three molecular subgroups from integrated RNA-seq/proteomics/ATAC-seq
- CAF-CXCL12-CXCR4-XRCC5 axis promotes cancer stemness
- Superior to UCSF and Up-to-7 criteria for LT candidate selection
- ~4,022 proteins quantified by TMT proteomics

## Project relevance

- Multi-omics integration for cancer subtyping
- CAF-tumor crosstalk and stemness biology
- Molecular stratification for clinical decision-making

## Caveats

- HBV-related HCC only; other etiologies not tested
- Chinese cohort; Western validation lacking
- Retrospective study design
