# ACK1/TNK2 Kinase in Cancer (Review)

## Summary

Review of ACK1/TNK2 non-receptor tyrosine kinase signaling in cancer. ACK1 bridges RTKs to intracellular effectors with multi-domain architecture (SAM, kinase, SH3, CRIB, UBA). Functions as epigenetic regulator via KDM3A phosphorylation, regulates androgen receptor in prostate cancer, and shows gene amplification across multiple cancers.

## Key points

- Non-RTK that bridges transmembrane RTKs to effectors
- Epigenetic regulation via KDM3A/JHDM2a tyrosine phosphorylation
- AR regulation in hormone-refractory prostate cancer
- Gene amplification and somatic mutations in multiple cancers

## Project relevance

- Kinase signaling and drug resistance mechanisms
- Epigenetic regulation by kinase activity
- Potential therapeutic target across cancers

## Caveats

- Review article; no original data
- Published in 2015; may miss recent developments
- ACK1 inhibitors still in early clinical development
