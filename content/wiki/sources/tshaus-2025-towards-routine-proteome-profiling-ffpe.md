# Large-Scale Pan-Cancer FFPE Proteomics Resource (Tüshaus 2025)

## Summary

This study presents the first large-scale pan-cancer proteomics resource derived from FFPE tumor specimens, profiling 1,220 samples across six cancer types (GBM, OSCC, DLBCL, PDAC, CRC, and melanoma) using LC-FAIMS-MS/MS on an Orbitrap Exploris 480. A median of over 4,000 proteins per sample were quantified, with approximately 11,000 proteins identified across the cohort. A TIC normalization approach is introduced to address variable sample loading in FFPE material, and stable analytical performance is demonstrated over three years of longitudinal processing.

## Key points

- 1,220 FFPE tumor samples profiled: GBM (246), OSCC (168), DLBCL (265), PDAC (204), CRC (145), MEL (192)
- LC-FAIMS-MS/MS workflow with PROCAL retention time standards and label-free quantification
- TIC normalization reduces inter-cohort variability 2-3 fold compared to standard loading approaches
- Median over 4,000 proteins quantified per sample; approximately 11,000 proteins total across all six cancer types
- Tissue-of-origin protein fingerprints identified for each cancer type
- Stable and reproducible analytical workflow demonstrated across 3 years of longitudinal processing
- panFFPE web resource is publicly available

## Project relevance

- Establishes a reference workflow for clinical proteomics using retrospective FFPE archives, directly applicable to drug repurposing studies lacking fresh-frozen tissue
- TIC normalization strategy is applicable to multi-cohort pan-cancer proteomics integration
- PDAC cohort (n=204) is directly relevant to pancreatic cancer drug repurposing analyses
- Pan-cancer protein expression landscape provides a benchmark for integration with transcriptomic drug repurposing signatures

## Caveats

- FFPE material limits post-translational modification (PTM) analysis, particularly phosphorylation, compared to fresh-frozen tissue
- Label-free quantification has lower precision than multiplexed isobaric labeling approaches such as TMT
- Phosphoproteomics not included in this dataset
- Tissue-of-origin protein fingerprints may confound pan-cancer analyses without careful batch correction
