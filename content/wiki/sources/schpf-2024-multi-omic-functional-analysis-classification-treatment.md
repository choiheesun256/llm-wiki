# FUS/EWSR1-TFCP2 Sarcoma Multi-Omics

## Summary

Multi-omic characterization of 12 patients with FUS-TFCP2 or EWSR1-TFCP2 fusion rhabdomyosarcoma using WES/WGS, RNA-seq, and DNA methylation. All tumors show ALK outlier expression with intragenic deletions producing truncated but oncogenic ALK variants. CDKN2A/MTAP co-deletions provide rationale for PRMT5 inhibitor therapy. Establishes this as a distinct sarcoma entity with genomic instability and HRD features.

## Key points

- All 12 tumors show ALK outlier expression with truncated variants
- CDKN2A/MTAP co-deletion → PRMT5 inhibitor sensitivity
- Genomic instability with HRD features
- Distinct entity from other fusion-driven sarcomas

## Project relevance

- Precision oncology for ultra-rare sarcomas
- ALK and PRMT5 as therapeutic targets
- MASTER program clinical-preclinical integration

## Caveats

- Ultra-rare (12 patients)
- ALK inhibitor response variable
- No proteomics data included
