# Ampullary Adenocarcinoma Proteogenomics (Cell Discovery 2025)

## Summary
Comprehensive proteogenomic analysis of 198 ampullary adenocarcinoma (AMPAC) samples (186 AMPAC + 12 duodenal cancers) from a Chinese cohort. Chr 4q loss drives fatty acid accumulation and cell proliferation via HADH downregulation. Proteomics-based clustering reveals three subtypes (C-FAM, C-AD, C-CC); the most aggressive C-AD is characterized by focal adhesion enrichment. Paradoxically, immune M1/DC-infiltrated clusters associate with poor prognosis through IL-6 secretion.

## Key points
- 198 samples; WES (n=133), RNA-seq (n=67), proteomics (n=198), phosphoproteomics (n=96)
- 15,363 proteins identified; 28,714 phosphosites
- 4q loss → HADH downregulation → fatty acid accumulation → cell proliferation (CNV cis effect)
- 6 cancer-associated genes (ERBB2, EP300, MYH9, MKL1, DACH1, MACF1) affected at all three omics levels
- C-AD subtype (worst prognosis): focal adhesion-enriched, present in both histological subtypes
- M1/DC immune-high cluster associates with poor prognosis via potential IL-6 secretion
- PCNA (intestinal-type) and ANO1 (pancreatobiliary-type) proposed as subtype-specific targets

## Project relevance
- Multi-omics pipeline for rare GI cancer; model for similar rare cancer analyses
- CNV-driven metabolic reprogramming (fatty acid axis) as a drug target concept
- IL-6-mediated immune paradox in TME relevant to immunotherapy response interpretation
- Histological subtype-specific target identification approach

## Caveats
- Single-center Chinese cohort (Fudan); lacks external validation
- RNA-seq available for only ~1/3 of samples (n=67); limits transcriptomic power
- IL-6 immune paradox mechanism not functionally validated
- Rare tumor limits statistical power for subgroup analyses
