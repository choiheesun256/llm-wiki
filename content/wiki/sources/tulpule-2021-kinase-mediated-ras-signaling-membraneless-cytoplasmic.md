# RTK Protein Granules and RAS Signaling (Tulpule 2021)

## Summary

Tulpule et al. (Bivona lab, Cell 2021) demonstrate that RTK fusion oncoproteins such as EML4-ALK and RET fusions form membraneless cytoplasmic protein granules, enabling lipid-membrane-independent RAS activation. These higher-order assemblies locally concentrate GRB2/SOS1 complexes to serve as a novel signaling platform for RAS-MAPK pathway activation. The study establishes biomolecular condensates as a mechanism for oncogenic signaling that is distinct from classical receptor tyrosine kinase signaling at the plasma membrane.

## Key points

- EML4-ALK fusion forms discrete cytoplasmic membraneless protein granules, characterized by super-resolution microscopy (SIM) and FRAP.
- Granules locally concentrate GRB2/SOS1, driving RAS-GTP loading independent of lipid membranes.
- Higher-order assembly is required for oncogenic MAPK signaling; disruption abrogates RAS activation.
- Similar granule formation observed for RET fusion oncoproteins, suggesting a generalizable mechanism across RTK fusions.
- Methods: SIM, FRAP, subcellular fractionation + proteomics, RAS-GTP pulldown, cell line models H3122 (EML4-ALK), Beas2B, and Ba/F3.

## Project relevance

- Provides mechanistic insight into how cytoplasmic condensates rewire canonical signaling networks — relevant conceptual background for perturbation prediction models (annForce) that aim to capture non-linear signaling responses.
- Highlights that oncogenic signaling can occur outside canonical membrane-proximal contexts, which has implications for network medicine drug target identification.

## Caveats

- Study is based on in vitro cell line models; direct observation of protein granules in primary patient tumors was not performed.
- Therapeutic targeting strategies for condensate-based signaling were not presented.
- Generalizability to RTK fusions beyond ALK and RET remains to be established in vivo.
