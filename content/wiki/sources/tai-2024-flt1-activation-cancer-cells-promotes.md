# FLT1/VEGFR1 PARPi Resistance in Breast Cancer (Tai 2024)

## Summary

FLT1 (VEGFR1) activation is identified as a novel driver of PARPi resistance in BRCA1/BARD1-deficient breast cancer. The mechanism is non-angiogenic: FLT1 activates the AKT survival pathway in tumor cells and suppresses cytotoxic immunity (reducing CD8+ T cell infiltration). Genetic or pharmacological FLT1 blockade with axitinib restores PARPi sensitivity in vivo in a T cell-dependent manner. A retrospective patient analysis shows that high FLT1 expression prior to treatment predicts shorter progression-free survival.

## Key points

- PARPi-resistant tumors show upregulation of VEGFR2/PGF and FLT1 activation
- FLT1 promotes tumor cell survival via AKT and reduces CD8+ T cell infiltration — a non-angiogenic, cell-autonomous and immune mechanism
- Axitinib re-sensitizes PARPi-resistant tumors; effect is T cell-dependent
- Pre-treatment FLT1 expression is a candidate predictive biomarker for PARPi response
- Resistance mechanism is independent of canonical BRCA reversion mutations

## Project relevance

- Illustrates a TME-driven, non-cell-autonomous PARPi resistance mechanism
- FLT1/axitinib axis is an actionable combination strategy for BRCA1/BARD1-mutant TNBC
- Supports the concept that immune contexture shapes response to DDR-targeted therapies — relevant to drug repurposing in immune-cold tumors

## Caveats

- Evidence from mouse models; clinical validation requires prospective trials
- Patient analysis is retrospective with a limited sample size
- BRCA2-independent resistance mechanisms were not fully assessed
