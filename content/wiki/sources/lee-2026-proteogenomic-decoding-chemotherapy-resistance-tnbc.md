# Proteogenomic Decoding of Chemotherapy Resistance in TNBC

## Summary

Prospective proteogenomic analysis of 50 TNBC patients treated with neoadjuvant anthracycline-taxane chemotherapy. WES + RNA-seq + TMT proteomics/phosphoproteomics with paired baseline and residual tumor analysis. NMF clustering identifies 5 subtypes with distinct chemotherapy response: immune-enriched (55.6% pCR) vs. EMT/xenobiotic (0% pCR). AURKB pathway activation in residual tumors suggests Aurora kinase inhibition + paclitaxel combination strategy.

## Key points

- 5 proteogenomic TNBC subtypes predict chemotherapy response
- Immune-enriched subtype: highest pCR; EMT: worst response
- AURKB pathway activated in treatment-resistant residual tumors
- Protein-based prediction outperforms RNA-based models

## Project relevance

- Proteogenomic chemotherapy response prediction in TNBC
- Multi-omics subtyping for treatment stratification
- Drug resistance mechanism identification and combination strategy

## Caveats

- Small cohort (50 patients, 7-12 per subtype)
- Single Korean institution
- AURKB synergy validated in vitro only
- Neoadjuvant setting; metastatic application unclear
