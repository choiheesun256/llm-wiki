# TP53 Loss Drives Immune-Cold CRC via mTORC1/Autophagy

## Summary

Multi-omics analysis linking TP53 loss to persistent mTORC1 activation and autophagy suppression in microsatellite-stable (MSS) colorectal cancer, driving immune-cold TME. Integrates TCGA bulk RNA-seq (647 patients), scRNA-seq (11,138 TILs), and CPTAC proteome/phosphoproteome data. TP53-null tumors show 4E-BP/S6K phosphorylation (mTORC1), FOXP3-dominant regulatory features, and loss of p53-autophagy coupling. Proposes mTOR inhibition + ICI combination.

## Key points

- TP53 loss → persistent mTORC1 + autophagy suppression in MSS CRC
- CPTAC phosphoproteomics confirms mTOR substrate phosphorylation
- Treg-dominant immune environment in TP53-mutant tumors
- mTOR inhibitor proposed as immunotherapy sensitizer

## Project relevance

- TP53-mTOR metabolic-immune axis in immunotherapy resistance
- Multi-omics integration (transcriptome + proteome + phosphoproteome)
- Combination therapy rationale for immune-cold CRC

## Caveats

- Computational analysis only; no experimental validation
- Public dataset heterogeneity and confounders
- MSS-specific; not applicable to MSI-H CRC
- No clinical evidence for mTOR + ICI combination
