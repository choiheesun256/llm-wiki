# NUAK1-NADK Axis in Osimertinib Resistance

## Summary

NUAK1 kinase drives osimertinib resistance in NSCLC by phosphorylating NADK at Ser64, increasing NADP+ production and mitigating ROS accumulation. Virtual drug screening identifies T21195 as a NADK-S64 phosphorylation inhibitor that synergizes with osimertinib to reverse resistance.

## Key points

- NUAK1-NADK-S64 phosphorylation axis reduces ROS to promote resistance
- T21195 identified as NADK phosphorylation inhibitor via virtual screening
- Combination of T21195 + osimertinib overcomes resistance
- Clinical validation of NUAK1 overexpression in resistant tissues

## Project relevance

- Kinase-substrate signaling in drug resistance
- ROS-mediated resistance mechanism
- Drug combination strategy for overcoming TKI resistance

## Caveats

- Limited to two cell line pairs (HCC827, PC9)
- T21195 lacks preclinical/clinical validation
- In vivo ROS dynamics not assessed
