# Single-cell Target Discovery in Multiple Myeloma

## Summary
Cancer Research 2023 study using scRNA-seq of 53 bone marrow aspirates from 41 MM patients (146,725 cells) to discover 38 myeloma marker genes encoding surface and intracellular proteins as therapeutic targets. 20 candidate genes highlighted, 11 previously uncharacterized. Cross-validated with bulk RNA-seq, flow cytometry, and proteomics via a high-throughput unbiased pipeline.

## Key points
- 38 MM-specific marker genes identified from scRNA-seq
- 11 novel therapeutic targets previously uncharacterized
- Multi-omic cross-validation (scRNA, bulk RNA, proteomics, flow cytometry)
- 146,725 single cells from 53 bone marrow samples

## Project relevance
- Systematic target discovery pipeline methodology
- Multi-omic validation framework for candidate targets
- Surface protein identification for therapeutic accessibility

## Caveats
- Multiple myeloma (hematologic malignancy, not solid tumor)
- Target validation at preclinical level only
- Functional studies limited in scope
