# Proteogenomic MR: Adiposity-CRC Mediated by GREM1

## Summary

Mendelian randomization + proteogenomic analysis identifying GREM1 (BMP antagonist) as a mediating circulating protein in the adiposity-CRC causal pathway. Using UVMR, MVMR, and colocalization with SomaScan plasma proteomics (≤4,907 aptamers), finds 6,591 adiposity-protein and 33 protein-CRC associations. GREM1 mediates BMI-CRC association, particularly in women.

## Key points

- GREM1 identified as mediator of BMI-CRC relationship
- MR + colocalization provides causal evidence
- 33 circulating proteins associated with CRC risk
- Sex-specific effect (stronger in women)

## Project relevance

- Proteogenomic causal inference methodology (MR + proteomics)
- Cancer risk mechanism through circulating protein mediators
- Integration of GWAS and proteomics for pathway discovery

## Caveats

- European-centric GWAS data
- Single mediator focus; multiple pathways likely
- SomaScan aptamer binding may not reflect protein function
- No experimental validation of GREM1 mechanism
