# Dynamic Neuroendocrine Transition in Prostate Cancer

## Summary

Nature Cancer study demonstrating that the neuroendocrine transition in prostate cancer is dynamic and reversible, with continuous ASCL1 dependency. Removal of ASCL1 reverts NE phenotype back to adenocarcinoma, proving epigenetic plasticity. Uses inducible systems, scRNA-seq, and scATAC-seq for temporal profiling of NE transition.

## Key points

- NE transition is dynamic and reversible
- ASCL1 continuously required to maintain NE state
- Epigenetic changes are plastic, not fixed
- Therapeutic ASCL1 targeting could reverse NEPC

## Project relevance

- Lineage plasticity reversibility as therapeutic opportunity
- Epigenetic mechanisms of treatment resistance
- Complementary to Rodarte-2024 (Cancer Research)

## Caveats

- Mouse model; human reversibility unverified
- ASCL1-subtype only; NEUROD1/POU2F3 not addressed
- No direct ASCL1-targeting drug available
