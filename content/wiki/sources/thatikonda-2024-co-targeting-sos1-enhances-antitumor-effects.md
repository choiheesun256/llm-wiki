# Co-targeting SOS1 to Overcome KRAS G12C Inhibitor Resistance (Thatikonda 2024)

## Summary

This Nature Cancer 2024 study evaluates the combination of the SOS1 inhibitor BI-3406 with the KRAS G12C inhibitor adagrasib across a panel of 14 KRAS G12C-mutant cell lines (NSCLC and CRC), CDX, and PDX models. The combination achieved antitumor efficacy equal to or greater than adagrasib combined with SHP2i or EGFRi, with complete suppression of RAS-MAPK signaling. MRAS upregulation was identified as a novel mechanism of acquired resistance to KRAS G12C inhibition.

## Key points

- High-throughput compound screen tested 179 compounds in combination with adagrasib across 14 KRAS G12C cell lines
- BI-3406 (SOS1i) + adagrasib demonstrated potent antitumor effects superior to adagrasib monotherapy
- Combination fully suppressed RAS-MAPK signaling; SHP2i and EGFRi combinations did not achieve equivalent suppression
- Resistance onset was delayed in combination arms, and efficacy was retained in adagrasib-resistant models
- MRAS upregulation identified as a previously unrecognized resistance mechanism under KRAS G12C inhibitor pressure
- Both SOS1i and SHP2i are capable of suppressing MRAS activity
- In vivo efficacy confirmed in CDX models (NCI-H2122, SW837, F3008, B8032) and PDX models

## Project relevance

- MRAS as a resistance node represents a potential target for network medicine-based drug repurposing and resistance mapping
- Demonstrates that targeting upstream GEF activity (SOS1) blocks feedback reactivation of RAS signaling, a generalizable combination design principle
- RAS-MAPK pathway suppression data can inform perturbation modeling and target prioritization

## Caveats

- All findings are from preclinical models; clinical trials are ongoing but no patient efficacy data are available yet
- Study is restricted to KRAS G12C; other KRAS mutations are not evaluated
- Toxicity profile of the combination in patients has not been established
