# PARP1-DOT1L Axis in PARPi Resistance

## Summary

PARP1 trapping induced by PARPi treatment directly binds to the DOT1L promoter, upregulating DOT1L expression. DOT1L then drives H3K79me2-mediated transcriptional activation of PLCG2 and ABCB1, promoting drug efflux and stemness in ovarian cancer. Combining DOT1L inhibitor SGC0946 with PARPi shows synergistic effects in CDX and PDO models.

## Key points

- PARP1 trapping paradoxically promotes resistance via DOT1L activation
- DOT1L-H3K79me2-PLCG2/ABCB1 axis mediates PARPi resistance
- DOT1L inhibitor + PARPi synergy in preclinical models
- Non-BRCA mutated OC particularly affected

## Project relevance

- Epigenetic mechanisms of drug resistance
- Combination therapy strategies for PARPi resistance
- Histone methylation in transcriptional regulation of resistance genes

## Caveats

- Role in BRCA-mutated OC unclear
- SGC0946 not in clinical development
- Organoid models lack immune microenvironment
