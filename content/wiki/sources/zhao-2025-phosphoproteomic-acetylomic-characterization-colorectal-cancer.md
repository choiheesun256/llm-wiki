# CRC Kinase Inhibitor Response: Phospho- and Acetylomics

## Summary
This ACS Pharmacology & Translational Science (2025) study systematically profiles the molecular response to seven kinase inhibitors (targeting EGFR, MAPK, PI3K-mTOR, and cell cycle pathways) in the HCT116 colorectal cancer cell line at proteomic, phosphoproteomic, and acetylomic levels using TMTpro quantification. The study reveals unique pathway-level perturbations, mitochondrial functional effects, and kinase activity changes for each inhibitor, and discovers PTM crosstalk between lysine acetylation and protein phosphorylation.

## Key points
- Seven inhibitors: lapatinib (ERBB2/EGFR), AT7519 (multi-CDK), PIK-93 (PI4KIIIbeta), AS-605240 (PI3Kgamma), Ro-3306 (CDK1), refametinib (ERK MAPK), ZTSK474 (PI3K-mTOR); 1 µM, 12h, HCT116
- Proteomics: 6,147 proteins quantified; differential expression with FC 1.2 and p < 0.05 cutoff
- Inhibitors targeting the same pathway show distinct biological effects (e.g., AT7519 vs Ro-3306 on metabolism)
- Mitochondrial proteins specifically perturbed: lapatinib (inner membrane), refametinib (matrix), AT7519 (matrix/IMS/MOM)
- PTM crosstalk: AKT2 phosphorylation down (lapatinib) accompanies HDAC6 acetylation up (refametinib)
- GSEA (hallmark + KEGG) and PTM-SEA/KSEA used for pathway and kinase activity inference
- Drug combination strategies explored via correlation coefficient of complementary drug effects

## Project relevance
- Demonstrates that drugs targeting the same pathway have divergent off-target proteomic effects — critical consideration for drug repurposing candidate selection
- PTM crosstalk analysis provides rationale for combining inhibitors that complement phosphorylation and acetylation landscapes
- Methodology (TMTpro multi-condition proteomics + KSEA) is directly applicable to phosphoproteomics-guided drug repurposing

## Caveats
- Single cell line (HCT116); results may not generalize to other CRC lines or patient-derived samples
- Fixed 1 µM concentration may not reflect clinically relevant drug exposures
- Acetylomic coverage is partial; global acetylation landscape not fully captured
