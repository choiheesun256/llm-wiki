# Proteogenomics of Early-Onset Endometrial Carcinoma

## Summary

Proteogenomic analysis of 215 endometrial carcinomas (81 early-onset, <40 years) reveals age-specific driver landscape with enriched CTNNB1/SIGLEC10 mutations in EEEC. SIGLEC10^Q144K induces aberrant protein expression that interacts with ERα to promote progestin resistance. An exposome-related mutational signature links environmental factors to EEEC development. Protein biomarkers for fertility-sparing progestin response are identified.

## Key points

- CTNNB1 and SIGLEC10 hotspot mutations enriched in early-onset vs. late-onset EEC
- SIGLEC10-ERα interaction as a novel progestin resistance mechanism
- 3D-Sig-Explorer quantifies age-specific mutagen contributions
- 17 germline pathogenic variant carriers identified among 108 FIGO IA EEECs

## Project relevance

- SIGLEC10-ERα axis opens new drug targets for endocrine resistance
- Age-specific mutational landscapes inform precision oncology strategies
- Wnt/β-catenin pathway (via CTNNB1) as a druggable target in EEEC

## Caveats

- Single Chinese institution (TJFD); population-specific findings possible
- No phosphoproteomics; FFPE-based proteomics only
- Progestin response biomarkers lack prospective clinical validation
