# Exosomal wtEGFR Transfer and Osimertinib Resistance

## Summary
Identifies a novel osimertinib resistance mechanism through intercellular transfer of exosomal wild-type EGFR from resistant cells to sensitive cells. wtEGFR-containing exosomes activate downstream signaling (ERK/AKT) in recipient cells, conferring resistance without genomic mutation. Blocking exosome secretion restores sensitivity.

## Key points
- Exosomal wtEGFR transfer confers osimertinib resistance
- Non-genetic resistance mechanism via intercellular communication
- ERK/AKT reactivation in recipient cells
- GW4869 (exosome inhibitor) restores drug sensitivity

## Project relevance
- Non-genetic drug resistance mechanism relevant to drug repurposing
- Exosome-mediated intercellular communication in tumor microenvironment
- Therapeutic combination strategy (TKI + exosome inhibitor)

## Caveats
- In vitro and xenograft models only
- Single resistance mechanism focus
- Clinical translation unclear
