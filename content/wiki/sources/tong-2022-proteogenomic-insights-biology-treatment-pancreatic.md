# EUS-TA-Based Preclinical Models for PDAC (Tong 2023)

## Summary

A comprehensive review of preclinical model establishment from endoscopic ultrasound-guided tissue acquisition (EUS-TA) samples in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC). Covers patient-derived cells (PDC), patient-derived xenografts (PDX), and patient-derived organoids (PDO) from EUS-FNA and EUS-FNB biopsies, enabling precision medicine even for unresectable patients (the majority of PDAC cases). Summarizes protocols, success rates, genomic profiling strategies, and drug sensitivity testing approaches.

## Key points

- EUS-TA enables preclinical model generation from patients who cannot undergo surgical resection — applicable to most PDAC patients
- PDOs offer the highest success rates and fastest establishment time among the three model types (PDC, PDX, PDO)
- PDX models best preserve the original tumor heterogeneity
- Drug sensitivity testing on EUS-derived models can guide real-time treatment selection
- EUS-FNB yields larger tissue cores than EUS-FNA, improving model establishment success rates

## Project relevance

- Establishes feasibility of building functional precision medicine models from minimally invasive biopsies in PDAC
- PDO drug sensitivity data can be integrated with molecular profiling pipelines for drug repurposing
- Provides a practical methods reference for sample acquisition and model establishment protocols

## Caveats

- Review paper with no original data; cited study results are heterogeneous across institutions and biopsy techniques
- Model establishment success rates vary widely — not yet a standardized workflow
- Clinical implementation remains limited by logistical barriers: time, cost, and technical expertise
