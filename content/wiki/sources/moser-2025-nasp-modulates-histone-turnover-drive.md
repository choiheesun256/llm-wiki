# NASP Histone Chaperone in PARPi Resistance

## Summary

PARP inhibitors trigger histone release from chromatin in cancer cells, and NASP histone chaperone maintains the stability of released H3-H4 histones. Loss of NASP causes PARPi hypersensitivity in vitro and in vivo, regardless of homologous recombination status. NASP acts with INO80 complex and PARP1 to regulate histone turnover, identifying histone supply pathways as a novel PARPi resistance axis.

## Key points

- PARPi-induced histone eviction is cancer cell-specific
- NASP stabilizes released histones for PARPi survival
- NASP loss → PARPi hypersensitivity (HR-independent)
- NASP-INO80-PARP1 complex for histone turnover

## Project relevance

- Novel chromatin-based drug resistance mechanism
- Histone supply pathway as therapeutic target
- PARPi mechanism of action and resistance

## Caveats

- No NASP inhibitors currently available
- Cancer-specific histone release mechanism not fully elucidated
- Applicability to HR-proficient tumors needs validation
