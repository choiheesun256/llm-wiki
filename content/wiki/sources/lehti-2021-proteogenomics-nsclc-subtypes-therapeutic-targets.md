# NSCLC Proteogenomic Subtypes

## Summary

In-depth proteogenomic analysis of 141 NSCLC tumors (~14,000 proteins via TMT-HiRIEF) identifying 6 proteome subtypes across all major histologies. Reveals STK11 mutation-dependent LAG-3 immune evasion via HNF1A activation and FGL1 expression. Develops DIA-based MS classification method validated in 208 + 84 independent samples for clinical biopsy application.

## Key points

- 6 proteome subtypes across NSCLC histologies (~14,000 proteins)
- STK11 → HNF1A → FGL1 → LAG-3 immune evasion axis
- DIA-based classifier validated in independent cohorts (208 + 84)
- Subtype-specific therapeutic targets and immune features

## Project relevance

- Large-scale proteogenomic subtyping methodology
- Immune evasion mechanism discovery through proteomics
- Clinically deployable DIA-based tumor classification

## Caveats

- Resected early-stage bias; late-stage/biopsy may differ
- TMT batch effect considerations
- Clinical utility of 6-subtype system needs validation
- STK11-LAG3 axis partially validated functionally
