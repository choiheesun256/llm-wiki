# Proteogenomics of CRC Progression

## Summary

Stage-resolved proteogenomic analysis of 435 trace-tumor samples from 148 CRC patients covering normal to advanced cancer. Reveals KRAS/BRAF mutually exclusive mutations with OXPHOS in IEN phase, chr17q loss/chr20q gain at distinct stages, and TP53 mutations with ECM rigidity in advanced disease. Maps progression paths across CMS/CRIS classifications and distinguishes MSI from MSS features.

## Key points

- 435 samples across CRC progression (NT → IEN → IFT → A-CRC)
- KRAS/BRAF exclusive mutations with stage-specific functional effects
- Distinct MSI vs. MSS proteogenomic features
- CMS/CRIS subtype-specific progression trajectories

## Project relevance

- CRC carcinogenesis molecular trajectory analysis
- Stage-specific mutation-proteome correlation
- MSI/MSS distinction at proteome level

## Caveats

- Chinese cohort; regional CRC differences possible
- Micro-dissected trace-tumor samples
- Few T4 advanced stage samples
- AOM/DSS mouse model for validation
