# CIC-MAPK Modulation in RET-Fusion Cancer

## Summary

Demonstrates that CIC (Capicua) loss activates MAPK/ERK signaling to confer resistance to RET inhibitors in RET-fusion sarcoma. CIC functions as a tumor suppressor opposing RET-driven tumorigenesis. Combination of RET inhibitor (selpercatinib) with MEK inhibitor overcomes CIC loss-mediated resistance, providing a rational combination strategy.

## Key points

- CIC loss activates MAPK/ERK bypass signaling
- CIC loss confers RET inhibitor (selpercatinib) resistance
- MEK inhibition restores RET inhibitor sensitivity
- CIC as tumor suppressor in RET-fusion cancers
- Combination RET+MEK strategy proposed

## Project relevance

- Drug resistance mechanism through tumor suppressor loss
- MAPK bypass pathway in targeted therapy resistance
- Combination therapy rationale for RET-fusion cancers

## Caveats

- Sarcoma-specific; validation needed in other RET-fusion cancers (NSCLC)
- Limited clinical/patient data
- Single resistance mechanism studied
