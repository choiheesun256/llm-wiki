# UDCA for Antitumor Immunity via TGF-β Degradation

## Summary

Demonstrates that ursodeoxycholic acid (UDCA), an approved bile acid drug, enhances antitumor immunity by inducing CHIP-mediated ubiquitination and autophagic degradation of TGF-β. UDCA reduces Treg cell differentiation and synergizes with anti-PD-1 therapy in syngeneic mouse models. Retrospective clinical analysis supports the combination benefit in tumor patients.

## Key points

- UDCA induces TGF-β degradation via CHIP ubiquitination
- Treg reduction and CD8+ T cell activation
- Synergy with anti-PD-1 therapy
- Retrospective clinical data supports benefit

## Project relevance

- Drug repurposing of approved compound for cancer
- TGF-β post-translational regulation mechanism
- Combination immunotherapy strategy

## Caveats

- Mouse models; prospective human trial needed
- Retrospective clinical data subject to bias
- Optimal UDCA dosing not established
