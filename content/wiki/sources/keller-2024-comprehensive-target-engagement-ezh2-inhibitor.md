# BI 836880 VEGF/Ang-2 Inhibitor Dose Finding

> **Note**: PDF filename says "ezh2-inhibitor" but content is about BI 836880 VEGF/Ang-2 bispecific nanobody.

## Summary

Phase I dose-escalation study of BI 836880, a humanized bispecific nanobody blocking VEGF-A and Ang-2, in advanced solid tumors. Comprehensive biomarker approach (free/total VEGF-A, Ang-2, sVEGFR3, sTie2, PlGF) combined with DCE-MRI and PopPK/PD modeling supports 720 mg q3w as the biologically active monotherapy dose. Free Ang-2 >90% inhibition achieved at ≥360 mg q3w.

## Key points

- BI 836880: bispecific nanobody dual-targeting VEGF-A and Ang-2
- Free VEGF-A depleted at all doses; Ang-2 >90% inhibition at ≥360 mg q3w
- DCE-MRI confirms tumor vascular target engagement
- PopPK/PD modeling supports 720 mg q3w dose

## Project relevance

- PK/PD modeling methodology for biologic dose optimization
- Anti-angiogenic dual targeting approach (VEGF + Ang-2)
- Biomarker-guided dose finding framework for clinical trials

## Caveats

- Phase I; small, heterogeneous patient population
- Monotherapy dose finding only; combination doses not determined
- DCE-MRI performed in limited patients
- Dose finding, not efficacy demonstration
