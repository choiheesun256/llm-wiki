# Pan-Cancer Proteogenomics: Therapeutic Targets

## Summary

Cell 2024 study integrating CPTAC proteogenomics data from 1,043 tumors across 10 cancer types to identify therapeutic targets. Quantifies 2,863 druggable proteins classified into 5 tiers. Identifies protein overexpression/hyperactivation-driven dependencies, synthetic lethality targets, and putative neoantigens through integration with genetic screen data. Published by Bing Zhang lab.

## Key points

- 1,043 tumors, 10 cancer types, 2,863 druggable proteins
- 5-tier target classification system
- mRNA-protein correlation median 0.48; protein-level targets missed by genomics
- Synthetic lethality identification for tumor suppressor loss
- KRAS mutant peptides as public neoantigens

## Project relevance

- Comprehensive druggable proteome landscape
- Drug repurposing opportunities from protein-level data
- Key reference for CPTAC pan-cancer proteogenomics

## Caveats

- Treatment-naïve tumors only
- 10 cancer types; more needed
- Computational predictions require experimental validation
