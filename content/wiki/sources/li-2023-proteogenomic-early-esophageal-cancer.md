# Proteogenomics of Early Esophageal Cancer

## Summary

Large-scale proteogenomic analysis of 786 trace-tumor samples from 154 ESCC patients across 9 histopathological stages. WES + label-free proteomics + phosphoproteomics reveals stage-specific carcinogenesis events. Identifies 6 major molecular tracks, TP53-driven DNA replication enhancement, and PGK1 S203 hyperphosphorylation as a drug target. Validated in independent cohort of 256 samples.

## Key points

- 786 samples across 9 ESCC carcinogenesis stages
- 6 major molecular tracks in ESCC progression
- PGK1 S203 phosphorylation as drug target
- TP53 mutation enhances DNA replication early

## Project relevance

- Largest stage-resolved ESCC proteogenomic study
- Phosphoproteomic drug target identification
- Carcinogenesis trajectory analysis methodology

## Caveats

- Chinese population-specific (ESCC epidemiology differs regionally)
- FFPE micro-dissection quality dependent
- PGK1 target lacks clinical validation
- Alcohol-related findings need confounder control
