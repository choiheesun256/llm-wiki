# MTC Proteogenomic Characterization

## Summary

Comprehensive multi-omics landscape of 102 medullary thyroid carcinomas from five Chinese centers using WES, RNA-seq, methylation, proteomics, and phosphoproteomics. Identifies BRAF and NFT as novel driver genes beyond RET and RAS. Proteome-based stratification reveals three molecularly heterogeneous subtypes (Metabolic, Basal, Mesenchymal) with distinct therapeutic targets. TNC/TNXB identified as potential prognostic biomarkers.

## Key points

- 102 MTC patients with comprehensive multi-omics
- BRAF and NFT as novel drivers beyond RET/RAS
- 3 proteomic subtypes with distinct biology
- TNC/TNXB as prognostic biomarkers

## Project relevance

- Rare cancer proteogenomics
- Novel driver discovery through multi-omics integration
- Proteomic subtyping distinct from transcriptomic

## Caveats

- Chinese cohort; population-specific features
- No treatment outcome data
- Rare cancer limits external validation
