# Proteogenomic Analysis of Cervical Cancer

## Summary
Nat Comms 2024 comprehensive proteogenomic analysis of 139 Chinese cervical cancer tumors using WES, RNA-seq, proteomics (TMT + DIA), phosphoproteomics, and acetylproteomics. Links HPV-associated multi-omic perturbations to pathogenesis. EP300 acetylates FOSL2-K222 accelerating proliferation. Three proteomic subgroups with distinct biology identified. PRKCB identified as radioresponse biomarker.

## Key points
- 139 CC tumors with 5 omic layers including acetylproteomics
- HPV-driven multi-omic landscape characterized
- EP300/FOSL2-K222 acetylation promotes proliferation
- PRKCB as radioresponse biomarker

## Project relevance
- Comprehensive cancer proteogenomics reference
- PTM (acetylation + phospho) integration methodology
- Therapeutic target identification pipeline

## Caveats
- Chinese cohort only
- Cervical cancer specific
- Some subgroups have small sample sizes
