# Proteogenomics of Bladder Cancer Chemoresistance

## Summary

Comprehensive proteogenomic characterization (WGS, RNA-seq, proteomics, phosphoproteomics) of 46 pre- and 14 post-treatment muscle-invasive bladder cancer tumors. Identifies ATAD1 short isoform and RAF family protein abundance as chemosensitivity biomarkers. Phosphoproteomics reveals GSK3B-S9/Wnt signaling and JAK/STAT pathway as potential targets to overcome cisplatin-based chemoresistance.

## Key points

- Multi-omics clustering recapitulates molecular subtypes and reveals chemosensitivity-associated groups
- RAF protein abundance marks chemotherapy-sensitive tumors
- GSK3B and STAT3 inhibition as actionable targets against chemoresistance
- PD-L1 correlates with TROP-2/NECTIN-4, supporting combination immunotherapy + ADC strategies

## Project relevance

- GSK3B/JAK-STAT inhibitors are druggable and relevant for drug repurposing
- Isoform-level protein analysis adds resolution beyond standard bulk proteomics
- Paired pre/post-treatment design captures NAC-induced molecular changes

## Caveats

- Small cohort (46 pre-treatment, 14 post-treatment) from a single institution
- Cisplatin-based NAC-specific; may not generalize to immunotherapy or other regimens
- No external validation cohort
