# CAMK2D as Shared Regulator of TPD52 Family

## Summary

Integrative analysis of 3,825 phosphoproteomic datasets identifies phosphosites on TPD52 family members (TPD52, TPD52L1, TPD52L2) and uses network-based kinase prediction to identify CAMK2D as a shared regulatory kinase hub. Constructs kinase-substrate interaction network linking calcium/calmodulin signaling to TPD52 family phosphorylation in cancer.

## Key points

- Meta-analysis of 3,825 phosphoproteomic datasets
- CAMK2D identified as shared kinase for all TPD52 family members
- Network-based kinase-substrate relationship inference
- TPD52 family phosphorylation linked to cancer progression

## Project relevance

- Large-scale phosphoproteomic data integration methodology
- Kinase-substrate network inference for cancer signaling
- Computational kinase activity prediction framework

## Caveats

- Computational predictions lack in vitro kinase assay validation
- Heterogeneous datasets (mixed cell types and conditions)
- No functional validation of phosphosite mutations
- Pan-cancer analysis limits cancer-type-specific conclusions
