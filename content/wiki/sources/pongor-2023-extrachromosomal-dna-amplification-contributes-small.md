# ecDNA in Small Cell Lung Cancer

## Summary

First comprehensive map of extrachromosomal DNA (ecDNA) in SCLC using WGS, optical mapping, single-cell DNA sequencing, and FISH. ecDNA amplifications are a primary source of MYC family oncogene amplification, creating transcription-amplifying units that drive tumor heterogeneity. Complex ecDNA is associated with significantly worse survival (HR=2.8). Cell-free nucleosome profiling can noninvasively detect ecDNA.

## Key points

- 18.8% of SCLC tumors carry ecDNA amplicons
- MYC paralogs predominantly encoded on ecDNA
- ecDNA-enabled transcriptional flexibility drives heterogeneity
- Complex ecDNA associated with worse survival (HR=2.8)
- cfDNA nucleosome profiling enables noninvasive detection

## Project relevance

- ecDNA as oncogene amplification mechanism
- Tumor heterogeneity and treatment resistance link
- Liquid biopsy biomarker potential

## Caveats

- SCLC-specific; generalization needed
- Retrospective; prospective validation needed
- cfDNA detection sensitivity/specificity needs optimization
