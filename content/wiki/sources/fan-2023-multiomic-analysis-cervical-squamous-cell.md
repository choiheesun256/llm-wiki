# Multiomic CSCC Cellular Ecosystems

## Summary

Integrative scRNA-seq, spatial transcriptomics, and spatial proteomics analysis of cervical squamous cell carcinoma identifies 8 tumor meta-programs via NMF. The epithelial-cytokeratin program (MP6) drives immune exclusion through TGF-β/FABP5/CAF crosstalk, while the epithelial-immune program (MP7) predicts response to neoadjuvant chemo + checkpoint blockade.

## Key points

- 163,880-cell atlas with 50 cell subtypes across 14 CSCC tumors
- NMF-based meta-program discovery reveals distinct tumor-TME interaction modes
- FABP5-mediated TGF-β signaling between MP6 tumor cells and CAFs creates immune exclusion
- MP7 score correlates with pathological complete remission after NACT+ICB

## Project relevance

- NMF meta-program framework transferable to other cancers
- TGF-β/CAF axis is a drug-targetable immune exclusion mechanism
- Demonstrates multimodal spatial validation workflow

## Caveats

- Small clinical validation cohort (n=21)
- CSCC-specific findings may not generalize to other squamous carcinomas
