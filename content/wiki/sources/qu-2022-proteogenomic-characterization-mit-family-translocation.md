# MiT Family tRCC Proteogenomics

## Summary

Comprehensive proteogenomic analysis of 86 MiT family translocation renal cell carcinoma (tRCC) samples using WES, RNA-seq, proteomics, and phosphoproteomics. Identifies 15 TFE3 fusion partners, reveals DNA repair deficiency as important in carcinogenesis, marked metabolic dysregulation, and mTOR signaling as a potential therapeutic target. Defines molecular subtypes with distinct prognoses.

## Key points

- 86-sample tRCC proteogenomics (rare cancer)
- 15 TFE3 fusion partners identified
- DNA repair deficiency important in tRCC
- mTOR signaling as potential therapeutic target
- Molecular subtypes with prognostic significance

## Project relevance

- Rare cancer proteogenomics methodology
- Fusion-driven cancer characterization
- mTOR targeting in kidney cancer

## Caveats

- Chinese cohort; population-specific fusion prevalence
- Rare cancer limits external validation
- Small phosphoproteomics cohort (28 tumors)
