# NENA NSLA Proteogenomic Characterization

## Summary

Proteogenomic characterization of 141 never-smoker lung adenocarcinoma (NSLA) tissues without EGFR or ALK alterations (NENA). Multi-omics integration (WGS, RNA-seq, methylation EPIC, proteomics, phosphoproteomics) reveals estrogen signaling as the key activated pathway in NENA, driven by DNA hypomethylation and copy number alterations. Proposes saracatinib (Src inhibitor) as potential therapeutic, validated in vitro.

## Key points

- 141 NENA NSLA proteogenomic characterization
- Estrogen signaling identified as key activated pathway
- DNA hypomethylation drives estrogen pathway activation
- Saracatinib proposed and validated as potential drug
- 22 prognostic proteins identified

## Project relevance

- Proteogenomics of driver-negative lung cancer
- Methylation-proteomics integration methodology
- Drug target discovery from multi-omics

## Caveats

- Korean single-center cohort
- In vitro drug validation only; no clinical trial
- NENA is a heterogeneous patient group
