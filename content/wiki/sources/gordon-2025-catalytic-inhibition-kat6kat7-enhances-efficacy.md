# KAT6/KAT7 Inhibition Overcomes Menin Inhibitor Resistance

## Summary

KAT7 interacts with the menin-MLL complex and co-regulates oncogenic transcription in MLL leukemia. Combined catalytic inhibition of KAT6/KAT7 (PF-9363) with menin inhibitors rapidly evicts MLL-FP from chromatin, overcomes both primary and acquired resistance, and induces differentiation and loss of leukemia-initiating capacity.

## Key points

- KAT7 is colocalized with menin-MLL complex at chromatin and represents a novel targetable dependency
- Combination therapy overcomes both genetic (MEN1 mutation) and nongenetic resistance to menin inhibitors
- PF-9363 (Pfizer) is in clinical development, enabling rapid translation
- In vivo PDX models confirm synergistic anti-leukemia activity

## Project relevance

- Paradigm for epigenetic drug combination to overcome resistance
- Framework applicable to other epigenetic therapy resistance contexts

## Caveats

- MLL-specific; applicability to non-MLL leukemia or solid tumors unexplored
- Long-term safety of KAT7 inhibition in normal hematopoiesis needs evaluation
