# Genetic Scores for Multi-omic Traits

## Summary
Nature 2023 study developing genetic scores for 17,227 molecular traits across proteomics (SomaScan, Olink), metabolomics (Metabolon, Nightingale), and RNA-seq from the INTERVAL cohort (n=50,000). 10,521 traits reach Bonferroni significance. Validated across European, Asian, and African American ancestries, generating synthetic multi-omic datasets from UK Biobank genetics for phenome-wide disease association scans.

## Key points
- Genetic prediction of 17,227 multi-omic traits using Bayesian ridge regression across 5 platforms
- 10,521 traits achieve Bonferroni-significant prediction
- Cross-ancestry validation (European, Asian, African American)
- Synthetic multi-omic data generation from genetics alone enables disease association discovery

## Project relevance
- Multi-omic prediction framework applicable to drug repurposing studies
- Cross-platform integration strategy for heterogeneous omics data
- Disease-trait association methodology for target identification

## Caveats
- UK blood donors introduce population bias
- Blood-based traits only (no tissue-specific coverage)
- Platform-specific genetic architectures limit generalizability
