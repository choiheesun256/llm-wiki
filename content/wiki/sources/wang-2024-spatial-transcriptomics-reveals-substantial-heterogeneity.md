## Summary

Nature Communications 2024. Spatial transcriptomics applied to 92 TNBC patients, defining 9 spatial archetypes linked to clinical outcome. Detailed molecular characterization of tertiary lymphoid structures (TLS) yields a 30-gene TLS signature that predicts ICB response across multiple cancer types. Tumor and stroma compartments contribute independently to TNBC molecular subtypes (BL, IM, M, MSL, LAR). Integrates histomorphological analysis with ST. Brussels/KTH collaboration.

## Key points

- 9 spatial archetypes defined in an unsupervised manner, each with distinct prognostic associations
- 30-gene TLS signature derived from spatial TLS characterization — pan-cancer ICB predictive value
- Tumor vs. stroma compartment separation reveals independent contributions to molecular subtyping
- CAF subtypes spatially mapped to specific archetypes
- Histomorphology (H&E) + ST co-analysis pipeline

## Project relevance

The spatial archetype framework is directly relevant to any cancer spatial heterogeneity study. The 30-gene TLS signature is immediately applicable as an ICB response predictor in other datasets. The compartment-separated molecular subtyping approach could be applied to PDAC or other solid tumors where stroma confounds bulk analysis.

## Caveats

- Visium resolution (~55 µm/spot) cannot resolve individual cells
- 92-patient cohort; prospective clinical validation of archetypes needed
- Pan-cancer generalizability of TLS signature requires broader validation
- No longitudinal/treatment time-course data — spatial dynamics under therapy not captured
