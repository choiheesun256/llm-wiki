# Osimertinib Resistance EGFR Mutations (Starrett 2020)

## Summary

Systematic characterization of drug sensitivity and allele specificity for EGFR resistance mutations (C797S, L718Q/V) arising after first-line osimertinib treatment in NSCLC. Uses Ba/F3 cell-based assays and genetically engineered mouse (GEM) models to map the therapeutic vulnerabilities of each resistance mutation, informing rational sequential treatment strategies.

## Key points

- C797S sensitivity differs depending on cis vs trans configuration relative to the activating mutation
- L718Q/V mutations restore sensitivity to first-generation EGFR TKIs
- Resistance spectrum varies by allele context (exon 19 deletion vs L858R)
- Sequential TKI therapy strategies supported by allele-specific data

## Project relevance

- Classification of osimertinib resistance mechanisms by allele specificity
- Drug sensitivity profiles applicable to drug repurposing in EGFR-mutant NSCLC
- GEM model validation provides in vivo context for resistance mutations

## Caveats

- Findings based on in vitro and mouse models; limited clinical validation
- Compound resistance mechanisms (co-occurring alterations) not fully addressed
- Clinical outcome data with sequential TKI strategies are limited
