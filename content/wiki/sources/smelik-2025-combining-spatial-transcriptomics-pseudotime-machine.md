# ST + Pseudotime for Prostate Cancer Biomarkers

## Summary

Combines spatial transcriptomics from HEST-1k dataset, pseudotime analysis, and machine learning to discover prostate cancer biomarkers. Pseudotime correlates with Gleason grade and models malignant transformation. Identifies 45 pseudotime-correlated genes and develops urine protein-based prediction models achieving AUC of 0.92, substantially outperforming PSA. Validated in >2,000 patients.

## Key points

- Pseudotime correlates with Gleason grade in spatial data
- 45 genes associated with malignant transformation
- Urine biomarker model AUC 0.92 (vs PSA 0.69)
- Validated across sera, tissue, and urine in >2,000 patients

## Project relevance

- Novel ST + pseudotime methodology
- Non-invasive biomarker development
- ML for clinical diagnostics

## Caveats

- Public ST data; no original data generated
- Prostate cancer only
- Prospective clinical validation needed
