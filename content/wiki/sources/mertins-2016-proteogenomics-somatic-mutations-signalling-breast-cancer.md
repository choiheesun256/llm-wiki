# CPTAC Breast Cancer Proteogenomics

## Summary

CPTAC proteogenomic analysis of 105 TCGA breast cancers quantifying 12,553 proteins and 33,239 phosphosites by iTRAQ. Connects somatic mutations to downstream signaling changes, identifies 5q deletion trans-effects (CETN3/SKP1 loss → EGFR/SRC activation), and reveals GPCR signaling cluster detectable only at protein level. Identifies amplicon-associated kinases including CDK12, PAK1, PTK2.

## Key points

- Landmark CPTAC breast cancer proteogenomics study
- Somatic mutations connected to phosphoproteomic signaling
- 5q trans-effects link chromosomal loss to kinase activation
- Protein-level signaling clusters invisible at mRNA level

## Project relevance

- CPTAC reference study for breast cancer proteogenomics
- Mutation-to-signaling framework
- Kinase activity inference from phosphoproteomics

## Caveats

- iTRAQ quantification less accurate than TMT
- 105 samples; relatively small by current standards
- Published 2016; methods have since improved
