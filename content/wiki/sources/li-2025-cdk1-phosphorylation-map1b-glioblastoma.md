# CDK1-MAP1B Phosphorylation in GBM

## Summary

CDK1 kinase drives GBM progression through phosphorylation of MAP1B at multiple sites (Ser832, 1260, 1899, 1939, 2209, 2271), causing microtubule destabilization. Label-free phosphoproteomics identifies 2,836 CDK1-regulated phosphosites from 15,156 total. MAP1B phosphorylation validated as prognostic biomarker using CPTAC GBM data.

## Key points

- CDK1 drives 2,836 phosphosite changes in GBM cells
- MAP1B hyperphosphorylation promotes microtubule destabilization
- MAP1B phosphorylation as prognostic biomarker (CPTAC validated)
- CDK1 as potential therapeutic target in GBM

## Project relevance

- Kinase-substrate phosphorylation network mapping
- Phosphoproteomics for drug target identification
- Microtubule biology in cancer progression

## Caveats

- Single cell line (U251); GBM heterogeneity not captured
- In vitro only; no in vivo validation
- CDK family member redundancy possible
- Small tissue cohort (5 patients)
