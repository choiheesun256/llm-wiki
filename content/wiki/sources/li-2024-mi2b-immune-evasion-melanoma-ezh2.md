# Mi-2β/EZH2 Immune Evasion in Melanoma

## Summary

Mi-2β chromatin remodeling enzyme promotes immune evasion in melanoma by binding EZH2, activating K510 methylation, and inducing H3K27me3 at IFN-γ-stimulated gene loci. This suppresses ISG transcription and T cell-mediated killing. Mi-2β-targeted inhibitor Z36-MP5 restores ISG expression and induces anti-PD-1 response in cold melanoma models.

## Key points

- Mi-2β → EZH2 K510 methylation → H3K27me3 → ISG silencing
- Mi-2β silencing rescues anti-PD-1 response in cold melanoma
- Z36-MP5 inhibitor converts cold to hot melanoma
- TCGA: Mi-2β anti-correlated with CD8 infiltration

## Project relevance

- Epigenetic mechanism of immunotherapy resistance
- Novel drug target (Mi-2β/Z36-MP5) for cold tumors
- Chromatin remodeling in immune evasion

## Caveats

- B16F10 mouse model; limited human melanoma diversity
- Z36-MP5 preclinical only; pharmacokinetics unknown
- Melanoma-specific findings
- Relationship to existing EZH2 inhibitors unclear
