# Osteoclast-Cancer Metabolic Crosstalk and PARPi Resistance

## Summary

Osteoclasts produce glutamine that cancer cells take up to enhance GPX4 expression and neutralize oxidative stress, conferring resistance to PARP inhibitors in bone metastatic breast cancer. Combination of PARPi with zoledronate (osteoclast inhibitor) shows synergistic anti-tumor effects.

## Key points

- TME-mediated (not tumor-intrinsic) PARPi resistance through metabolic crosstalk
- GPX4 upregulation via ATF4-dependent stress response is the key resistance mechanism
- PARPi + zoledronate combination — both FDA-approved — shows synergy in vivo
- Links PARPi resistance to ferroptosis escape via GPX4

## Project relevance

- Drug repurposing example: zoledronate as PARPi sensitizer in bone metastasis
- Highlights importance of TME metabolism in drug resistance modeling

## Caveats

- Mouse bone metastasis models may not fully recapitulate human bone marrow microenvironment
- BRCA/HR deficiency status of cell lines not clearly specified
