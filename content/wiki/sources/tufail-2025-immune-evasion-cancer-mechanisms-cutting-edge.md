# Immune Evasion in Cancer (Review, Tufail 2025)

## Summary

This comprehensive review covers the mechanisms by which tumors evade immune destruction and the cutting-edge therapeutic strategies targeting these pathways. It synthesizes tumor-induced immunosuppression, immune checkpoint regulation, TME remodeling, and metabolic reprogramming into a unified landscape. Checkpoint pathways including PD-1/PD-L1, CTLA-4, LAG-3, and TIGIT are reviewed alongside emerging therapeutic modalities such as bispecific antibodies, oncolytic viruses, and nanotechnology-based immunotherapy.

## Key points

- Four pillars of immune evasion: immunosuppression, checkpoint regulation, TME transformation, and antigen presentation suppression.
- Metabolic axis: lactate accumulation, hypoxia, and arginase activity suppress T cell function in the TME.
- Novel checkpoints reviewed: LAG-3, TIGIT, and VISTA as emerging therapeutic targets beyond PD-1/CTLA-4.
- Emerging therapeutics: bispecific antibodies (e.g., tebotelimab), oncolytic viruses, and nano-immunotherapy platforms.
- Review is comprehensive but deliberately broad, covering the full immune evasion landscape as of 2025.

## Project relevance

- Background reference for understanding why network medicine-based drug repurposing must account for immune evasion when selecting candidate drugs.
- Metabolic reprogramming section is relevant for drug candidates targeting tumor metabolism in the drug repurposing project.
- Checkpoint and TME sections provide context for interpreting immune cell composition in multi-omic cancer datasets.

## Caveats

- Review paper; no original experimental data.
- Breadth comes at the cost of mechanistic depth in any single area.
- Rapidly evolving field; specific therapeutic claims may be superseded by newer trial data.
