# PDTC/ATC Thyroid Cancer Proteogenomics

## Summary

Integrative proteogenomic characterization of 348 poorly differentiated (PDTC) and anaplastic (ATC) thyroid cancer samples — the most aggressive thyroid cancer subtypes with limited treatment options. Multi-omics integration (WES/WGS, RNA-seq, proteomics, phosphoproteomics) defines molecular subtypes, identifies therapeutic vulnerabilities and druggable kinase targets, and proposes prognostic biomarkers.

## Key points

- 348-sample PDTC/ATC proteogenomics (large for rare cancer)
- Molecular subtypes with distinct biology defined
- Therapeutic vulnerabilities and druggable targets identified
- Distinct features vs differentiated thyroid cancer
- Prognostic biomarkers proposed

## Project relevance

- Large-scale proteogenomics of aggressive/rare cancer
- Therapeutic vulnerability discovery methodology
- Druggable target identification from multi-omics

## Caveats

- Retrospective cohort; prospective validation needed
- Treatment heterogeneity in cohort
- Rare cancer limits external validation
