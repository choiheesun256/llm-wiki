# Spatial Transcriptomics Technologies and Cancer Applications Review

## Summary

Comprehensive review of spatial transcriptomics technologies covering three categories: LCM-based, in situ imaging (smFISH, MERFISH, ISS), and spatial indexing (Visium, Slide-seq, STEREO-seq). Compares 20+ methods across resolution, throughput, and gene coverage. Summarizes cancer applications including TME mapping, tumor heterogeneity, cell-cell interactions, and drug response spatial patterns.

## Key points

- 20+ spatial transcriptomics methods compared (Table 1)
- Three categories: LCM-based, in situ imaging, spatial indexing
- Trade-offs: resolution vs. throughput vs. gene coverage
- Cancer applications: TME, heterogeneity, therapy resistance spatial patterns

## Project relevance

- Reference for selecting appropriate spatial transcriptomics technology
- Overview of cancer-specific spatial analysis applications
- Framework for integrating spatial data into multi-omics cancer studies

## Caveats

- Review without original experimental data
- Rapidly evolving field; newer technologies may not be covered
- Computational analysis methods not deeply compared
