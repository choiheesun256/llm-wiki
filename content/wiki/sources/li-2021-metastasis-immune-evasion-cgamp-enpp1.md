# ENPP1-Mediated cGAMP Hydrolysis in Immune Evasion

## Summary

ENPP1 ectonucleotidase hydrolyzes extracellular cGAMP (from cGAS-STING activation in CIN tumors) to generate immunosuppressive adenosine, promoting metastasis and immune evasion. ENPP1 loss suppresses metastasis, restores immune infiltration, and potentiates anti-PD-1 response. In human cancers, ENPP1 expression correlates with reduced immune infiltration and immunotherapy resistance.

## Key points

- ENPP1 hydrolyzes cGAMP → adenosine, suppressing anti-tumor immunity
- CIN tumors use ENPP1 to evade cGAS-STING-mediated immunity
- ENPP1 loss restores immune infiltration and anti-PD-1 response
- Potential therapeutic target for immunotherapy sensitization

## Project relevance

- Immune evasion mechanism via metabolite conversion
- Drug target identification (ENPP1 inhibitor)
- CIN-immunity axis in cancer progression

## Caveats

- Mouse models primarily; limited clinical validation
- ENPP1 has multiple substrates beyond cGAMP
- Adenosine pathway redundancy (CD73)
- ENPP1 inhibitors in early development
