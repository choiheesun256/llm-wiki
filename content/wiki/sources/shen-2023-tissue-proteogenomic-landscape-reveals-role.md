# SEL1L3 in Lung Adenocarcinoma

## Summary

Identifies SEL1L3, a previously uncharacterized protein from chromosome 4, as overexpressed in 90% of lung adenocarcinoma tumors through Taiwan Cancer Moonshot proteogenomics data. High SEL1L3 associates with better overall survival (HR=0.54), reduced recurrence, and favorable immune profiles. Functional studies show SEL1L3 regulates cell viability via ER stress pathways.

## Key points

- SEL1L3 overexpressed in 90% of lung adenocarcinoma
- High SEL1L3 → better OS (HR=0.54)
- ER stress pathway activation upon SEL1L3 knockdown
- Favorable immune profiles with higher expression

## Project relevance

- Proteogenomics-driven novel gene discovery
- Human Proteome Project (C-HPP) contribution
- Biomarker for immunotherapy response

## Caveats

- Single cancer type (lung adenocarcinoma)
- SEL1L3 function not fully characterized
- Small validation cohort (98 patients)
