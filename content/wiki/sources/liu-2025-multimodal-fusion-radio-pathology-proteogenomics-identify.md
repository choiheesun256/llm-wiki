# Multimodal Fusion Subtyping of IDH-Wildtype Glioma

## Summary

Multimodal fusion subtyping (MOFS) framework integrating MRI radiomics, WSI pathomics, WES, RNA-seq, and LC-MS proteomics from 1,194 IDH-wildtype adult glioma patients. Identifies three subtypes: MOFS1 (proneural, best prognosis), MOFS2 (proliferative, worst prognosis, STRAP as target), MOFS3 (TME-rich, anti-PD-1 sensitive). DNN classifier enables non-invasive MRI-based subtype prediction.

## Key points

- 11 fusion algorithms + COCA for robust 3-subtype classification
- STRAP identified as therapeutic target for proliferative MOFS2
- DNN enables non-invasive MRI-based subtype prediction
- 122 patients with full 5-modality data; 1,194 total

## Project relevance

- Multimodal data integration for cancer subtyping
- Proteogenomics in glioma classification
- AI-based non-invasive molecular subtyping

## Caveats

- Chinese cohort only; cross-ethnic validation needed
- Retrospective design
- Limited full multimodal cohort (122 patients)
