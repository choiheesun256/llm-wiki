# Short-chain Acyl PTMs in Cancer

## Summary
Comprehensive review of short-chain acyl PTMs (lactylation, succinylation, crotonylation, butyrylation, β-hydroxybutyrylation, glutarylation, malonylation, propionylation) in cancer. Covers metabolic origins, writer/eraser/reader enzymes, functional roles in gene regulation and signaling, and therapeutic targeting strategies. Emphasizes the metabolic reprogramming-PTM axis.

## Key points
- Eight types of short-chain acyl PTMs systematically reviewed
- Metabolic reprogramming drives the PTM landscape in cancer
- Lactylation emerging as an oncogenic mark linked to Warburg effect
- Therapeutic targeting of writers/erasers as drug development strategy

## Project relevance
- PTM biology in cancer providing new drug targets
- Metabolism-epigenetics crosstalk relevant to drug repurposing
- Drug target identification through PTM enzyme inhibition

## Caveats
- Review article (no original data)
- Rapidly evolving field with frequent new discoveries
- Many PTMs lack validated antibodies for reliable detection
