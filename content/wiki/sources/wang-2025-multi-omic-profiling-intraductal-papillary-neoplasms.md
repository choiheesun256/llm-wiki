## Summary

Cancer Cell 2025. Large-scale proteomics and glycoproteomics of 64 IPMNs, 5 IOPNs, 76 normal ducts, 104 PDACs, 43 NATs, and 55 cyst fluid samples. Identifies IPMN grade- and subtype-associated proteins in both tissue and cyst fluid. ECM protein alterations are linked to IPMN-to-PDAC progression. Proposes potential early-detection biomarkers. Validated by IHC and laser microdissection (LMD). From Johns Hopkins (Hui Zhang & Ralph Hruban).

## Key points

- Grade-stratified protein signatures (low-grade vs. high-grade IPMN vs. PDAC) across a large multi-class cohort
- Cyst fluid proteomics as a minimally invasive biomarker source for IPMN risk stratification
- Glycoproteomics reveals glycosylation changes accompanying malignant progression
- ECM remodeling proteins track with IPMN→PDAC progression steps
- LMD-based cell-type-specific protein profiling improves signal specificity

## Project relevance

Directly relevant to pancreatic cancer early detection and IPMN management. The ECM protein alterations identified here complement spatial transcriptomics findings (e.g., CLDN4 paper). The cyst fluid biomarker panel has immediate translational value. The proteomics methodology serves as a reference for integrating protein-level data with genomic/transcriptomic analyses.

## Caveats

- Retrospective cohort — prospective biomarker validation needed
- Cyst fluid collection requires EUS-FNA, limiting routine clinical use
- Relative (not absolute) quantification in proteomics
- Clinical cut-offs for progression prediction not yet established
- No multi-institutional validation reported
