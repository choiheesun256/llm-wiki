# DNA Methylation Loss Promotes Immune Evasion

## Summary

Pan-cancer TCGA analysis shows global DNA methylation loss promotes immune evasion independently of TMB and aneuploidy. Immunomodulatory genes in late-replicating domains are silenced by CGI promoter hypermethylation in demethylated tumors. Methylation loss is a stronger predictor of ICI resistance than mutation burden, validated in lung cancer (n=60) and melanoma (n=40) cohorts. Genomic hypomethylation correlates with immune escape signatures of aneuploid tumors.

## Key points

- Global methylation loss predicts ICI resistance better than TMB
- Immunomodulatory genes concentrated in late-replicating domains, repressed by CGI hypermethylation
- Independent of mutation burden and aneuploidy
- Consistent across lung cancer, melanoma, and 21 TCGA cancer types

## Project relevance

- Methylation-based biomarker for immunotherapy response prediction
- Epigenetic modulation as immunotherapy combination strategy
- Links aneuploidy, methylation, and immune evasion mechanistically

## Caveats

- 450K methylation arrays (limited coverage vs. WGBS)
- Small ICI-treated cohorts (n=60, n=40); prospective validation needed
- Correlative; direct causal mechanism not fully established
