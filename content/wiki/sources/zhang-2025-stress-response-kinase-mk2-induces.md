# MK2-RSK-EphA2 Axis in EML4-ALK Lung Cancer (Biol. Pharm. Bull. 2025)

## Summary
In EML4-ALK lung cancer cells, stress kinase MK2 (not p38) regulates the RSK-EphA2 axis to induce non-canonical Ser-897 phosphorylation of EphA2 in an ERK-activated context. MK2 inhibition suppresses both p-RSK and pS-EphA2, and importantly blocks the migration of crizotinib-resistant (CR) cells. This establishes MK2 as a potential therapeutic strategy to overcome ALK-TKI resistance via the non-canonical EphA2 axis.

## Key points
- EML4-ALK variants v1, v3a, v5a all show constitutive pS-EphA2 (Ser-897); blocked by ALK-TKIs
- MK2 inhibitors (MK2 IN-3, MK2 IN-1) suppress p-RSK and pS-EphA2; p38 inhibitors do not
- MK2 overexpression with EML4-ALK v5a further enhances RSK phosphorylation
- Crizotinib-resistant (CR) cells maintain constitutive pS-EphA2; suppressed by RSK/MK2 inhibitors
- CR cell migration blocked by EphA2 inhibitor (ALW-II 41-27) + RSK/MK2 inhibitors
- Clinical relevance: IHC showed pS-EphA2 high expression correlates with poor prognosis in lung adenocarcinoma (cited prior work)

## Project relevance
- Non-canonical RTK signaling axis (MK2-RSK-EphA2) as an ALK-TKI resistance mechanism
- MK2 as a therapeutic target for drug-resistant lung cancer; relevant to drug repurposing for ALK+ NSCLC
- Kinase inhibitor combination strategy to overcome acquired resistance
- EphA2 Ser-897 phosphorylation as a pharmacodynamic biomarker

## Caveats
- Cell line study only; no in vivo or clinical sample validation
- Downstream molecular consequences of EphA2 Ser-897 phosphorylation not fully characterized
- Potential off-target toxicity of MK2 inhibition not evaluated
- Mechanistic focus on migration; other malignant phenotypes not fully explored
