# Negative Selection in Tumor Genome Evolution

## Summary
Genome Biology 2018 pan-cancer analysis of 7,546 exomes across 26 tumor types revealing negative selection on essential cellular functions (protein synthesis, glucose metabolism, molecular transport) and the immunopeptidome. Develops somatic substitution bias-corrected dN/dS measure. Immune-mediated negative selection acts on MHC-exposed regions, correlating with tumor-type-specific immune activity.

## Key points
- Negative selection on cancer-essential genes and immunopeptidome
- SSB-corrected dN/dS measure for 26 tumor types
- Immune surveillance shapes tumor genome evolution
- ~34% of negative selection underestimated by standard methods

## Project relevance
- Tumor evolution framework
- Immune editing concepts
- Pan-cancer genomic analysis methodology

## Caveats
- TCGA data (2018 vintage)
- Computational analysis only
- Controversial topic in cancer genomics
