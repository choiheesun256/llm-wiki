# IDH-wt GBM Pharmaco-Proteogenomics

## Summary

Integrated pharmaco-proteogenomic analysis of IDH-wildtype glioblastoma defines two molecular subtypes (mitotic vs invasive) with distinct drug sensitivities. Combines WES, RNA-seq, proteomics, phosphoproteomics, and high-throughput drug screening to identify subtype-specific therapeutic vulnerabilities and kinase activity-based actionable targets for personalized GBM treatment.

## Key points

- Two proteomics-based IDH-wt GBM subtypes: mitotic and invasive
- Subtype-specific drug sensitivities identified
- Kinase activity profiling reveals actionable signaling nodes
- Phosphoproteomics-drug response integration
- Personalized treatment selection framework

## Project relevance

- Pharmaco-proteogenomics integration methodology
- Drug sensitivity prediction from proteomics
- Cancer subtype-specific treatment strategy

## Caveats

- IDH-wt only; excludes IDH-mutant GBM
- In vitro drug screen; limited in vivo validation
- Single-center Korean cohort
