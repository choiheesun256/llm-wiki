# Integrated Xenium + Visium + scFFPE-seq for Breast Cancer TME

## Summary

10x Genomics integrates three platforms — Xenium (in situ, 313 genes, subcellular resolution), Visium (spatial, whole-transcriptome), and scFFPE-seq (single-cell from FFPE) — on serial sections of breast cancer FFPE tissue. Cross-platform cell type annotations show high concordance. Demonstrates complementary multi-scale TME analysis from archival clinical specimens.

## Key points

- Xenium provides subcellular spatial resolution for precise cell typing
- Visium enables unbiased whole-transcriptome spatial discovery
- scFFPE-seq achieves single-cell resolution with full transcriptome from FFPE
- High cross-platform concordance validates integrated approach

## Project relevance

- Spatial transcriptomics platform comparison for TME analysis
- FFPE compatibility enables retrospective clinical cohort studies
- Multi-scale spatial analysis methodology for tumor-immune interactions

## Caveats

- Platform developer's showcase study (potential bias)
- Single patient/tissue; generalizability not demonstrated
- Xenium panel limited to 313 genes; whole-transcriptome requires Visium
- No standardized computational pipeline for three-platform integration
