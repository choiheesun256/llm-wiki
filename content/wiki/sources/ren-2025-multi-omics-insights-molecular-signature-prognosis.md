# HPSCC Multi-Omics Characterization

## Summary

First comprehensive multi-omics characterization of 103 hypopharyngeal squamous cell carcinoma (HPSCC) patients using WES, RNA-seq, proteomics, and phosphoproteomics. Identifies 4 gene expression subtypes, estrogen response pathway activation, aberrant PKC/CDK kinase regulation, and HPV-associated prognostic markers. Develops and cross-site validates a 6-gene prognostic model.

## Key points

- 103 HPSCC patients; first comprehensive multi-omics
- 4 expression subtypes identified
- Estrogen response pathway upregulated
- PKC/CDK kinases aberrantly regulated
- 6-gene prognostic model validated cross-site

## Project relevance

- Multi-omics of rare HNSCC subtype
- Phosphoproteomics-based kinase analysis
- Prognostic biomarker development

## Caveats

- Chinese cohort; population-specific features
- Limited phosphoproteomics depth (652 proteins)
- Rare cancer limits external validation
