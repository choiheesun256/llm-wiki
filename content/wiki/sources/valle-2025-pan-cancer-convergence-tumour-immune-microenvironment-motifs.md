# CyTOF/IMC Pan-Cancer TME Review (Vallée 2025)

## Summary

Vallée et al. conduct a PRISMA-guided scoping review of 61 CyTOF and imaging mass cytometry (IMC) studies across 17 cancer types to identify pan-cancer convergent motifs in the tumor-immune microenvironment (TME). Five recurring immunobiological motifs are identified: CD8+ T cell bifurcation, CD38+ TAM barrier, TLS maturity states, CTLA-4+ NK cells, and metabolically defined niches. The review also summarizes bioinformatics pipelines used across these studies (FlowSOM, PhenoGraph, CITRUS) and notes increasing adoption of machine learning and spatial modeling.

## Key points

- Scoping review of 61 studies: CyTOF (46), IMC (12), both (3); 17 cancer types; median panel size 33.5 markers (CyTOF) and 33 markers (IMC).
- Five pan-cancer TME motifs: (1) CD8+ T cell bifurcation into effector vs exhausted states, (2) CD38+ TAM barrier suppressing cytotoxicity, (3) TLS maturity gradients, (4) CTLA-4+ NK cell subset, (5) metabolically defined immune niches.
- CyTOF studies frequently identify exhausted CD8+ T cell subsets; IMC studies highlight TLS architecture and macrophage-T cell exclusion zones.
- Machine learning integration and spatial modeling are increasingly used across recent studies.
- Standard pipelines: FlowSOM, PhenoGraph, CITRUS for clustering and cell type annotation.

## Project relevance

- Provides a systematic pan-cancer view of TME immune composition that is directly relevant to interpreting immune features in drug repurposing target landscapes.
- The five convergent motifs offer a framework for categorizing immune phenotypes in multi-cancer datasets used in the drug repurposing project.
- CyTOF/IMC pipeline summary is useful background for integrating high-dimensional immune profiling data.

## Caveats

- Review paper; no original data generated.
- Substantial heterogeneity in marker panels across studies limits direct cross-study quantitative comparison.
- CyTOF and IMC are not widely accessible technologies, limiting translational generalizability.
