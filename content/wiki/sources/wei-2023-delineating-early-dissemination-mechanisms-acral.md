## Summary

Nature Communications 2023. scRNA-seq and spatial transcriptomics of 12 acral melanoma (AM) patients (6 LN-positive vs. 6 LN-negative). Identifies 13 cell types. MYC+ melanoma cells (MYC+MEL) and FGFBP2+ NKT cells are tightly correlated with lymph node (LN) metastasis. LN metastasis requires a metabolic shift to fatty acid oxidation (FAO) driven by MITF in MYC+MEL cells. The FAO inhibitor etomoxir suppresses MITF-mediated LN metastasis in vivo. Fudan University.

## Key points

- MYC+MEL subpopulation enriched in LN-metastatic samples — defined by scRNA-seq
- FGFBP2+ NKT cells co-correlate with LN metastasis — immune cell perspective
- MITF drives FAO metabolic reprogramming as a prerequisite for LN dissemination
- Etomoxir (FAO inhibitor) blocks MITF-dependent metastasis in a murine AM model
- scRNA-seq + ST integration maps spatial invasion routes of MYC+MEL cells

## Project relevance

Demonstrates the power of integrating scRNA-seq and ST to mechanistically dissect metastasis at single-cell resolution. The metabolic reprogramming (MITF-FAO) axis is a potential drug target relevant to drug repurposing. The LN metastasis comparison design (LN+ vs. LN-) is a useful study design template for other cancer metastasis work.

## Caveats

- Small cohort (n=12) — larger validation needed
- Acral melanoma is a rare subtype — generalizability to other melanomas or cancers limited
- Precise molecular linkage between MITF and FAO activation requires further mechanistic work
- Etomoxir clinical applicability (toxicity profile) not addressed
- Single time-point snapshot; no longitudinal metastatic dynamics
