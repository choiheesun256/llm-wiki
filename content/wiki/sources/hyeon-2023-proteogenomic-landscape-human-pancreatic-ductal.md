# Proteogenomics of PDAC Asian Cohort

## Summary

Proteogenomic analysis of 196 PDAC patients (WES, RNA-seq, proteomics, phosphoproteomics) identifies seven significantly mutated genes and reveals mutation-phosphorylation correlations linking KRAS/SMAD4 mutations to RhoA/actin signaling. Integrated clustering identifies six molecular subtypes — classical progenitor, three squamous variants, immunogenic progenitor, and exocrine-like — with distinct immune compositions and therapeutic implications.

## Key points

- KRAS mutation frequency reaches 94.9% with targeted deep sequencing (1M× depth)
- Mutation-phosphorylation mapping directly links somatic mutations to kinase pathway changes
- Protein and phospho data subdivide squamous subtype into three clinically distinct groups
- TS4 (invasive-proliferative) enriched in pro-tumorigenic immune cells that suppress T cell proliferation

## Project relevance

- Mutation-to-phosphorylation analysis framework transferable to other cancer types
- Six-subtype classification informs subtype-specific drug selection strategies
- Demonstrates value of Asian-specific cohorts complementing Western TCGA/CPTAC data

## Caveats

- Korean single-population cohort; may differ from Western patient profiles
- Only 150/196 samples passed cellularity threshold for proteomics
- No KRAS inhibitor response data available
