# Multi-Omic Analysis for Pituitary Neuroendocrine Tumors

## Summary

Review of multi-omic analysis approaches for pituitary neuroendocrine tumors (PitNETs), covering genomics, transcriptomics, epigenomics, proteomics, and metabolomics. Discusses subtype-specific mutations (GNAS, USP8, SF3B1), WHO 2022 transcription factor-based classification, epigenomic alterations, and the potential of multi-omics integration for improved classification, prognostication, and therapeutic strategies.

## Key points

- Subtype-specific key mutations (GNAS, USP8, SF3B1, HMGA2)
- WHO 2022 TF-based classification validated by transcriptomics
- Epigenomic changes more prevalent than somatic mutations
- Multi-omics integration improves classification and prognosis
- Comprehensive overview of PitNET molecular landscape

## Project relevance

- Multi-omics integration methodology for rare tumors
- Biomarker discovery approaches
- Tumor classification framework

## Caveats

- Review article; no original data
- Rare tumor; large cohorts scarce
- Heterogeneous study designs across reviewed papers
