# TFE3-CCND1-PDL1 in Xp11.2 tRCC

## Summary

TFE3 fusion proteins in Xp11.2 translocation RCC upregulate CCND1/Cyclin D1 through direct transcription, NR1D1-mediated regulation, and AKT/mTOR-mediated protein stabilization. High Cyclin D1-CDK4 activity degrades PDL1 protein via ubiquitin-proteasome system and autophagy, providing rationale for sequential CDK4 inhibitor and anti-PDL1 therapy.

## Key points

- Three mechanisms converge on CCND1 upregulation in Xp11.2 tRCC
- Cyclin D1-CDK4 mediates PDL1 protein degradation
- Sequential CDK4i + anti-PDL1 treatment strategy proposed
- Post-translational PDL1 regulation mechanism

## Project relevance

- Immune evasion through PDL1 protein stability regulation
- CDK inhibitor combination strategies in rare cancers
- Fusion protein downstream signaling

## Caveats

- Very rare cancer type (Xp11.2 tRCC)
- In vivo combination validation lacking
- Applicability to other cancers uncertain
