# Skull-Base Chordoma Proteogenomics (Nature Communications 2024)

## Summary
Integrative genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and phosphoproteomics of 187 skull-base chordoma (SBC) tumors from 162 patients. Chromosome instability (CIN) is identified as a prognostic predictor; RPRD1B is a putative target for radiotherapy-resistant patients. Chr 1q gain associates with upregulated mitochondrial function and poor outcomes; Chr 9p/10q loss links to immune cold subtype and immune evasion. Proteomics-based classification yields subtypes P-I, P-II, P-III.

## Key points
- 187 samples (121 primary + 66 recurrent); WES (n=95), RNA-seq (n=180), proteomics (n=187), phosphoproteomics (n=163)
- CIN score is an independent prognostic factor (OS log-rank p=0.0024, PFS p=0.0013)
- Chr 1q gain → upregulated mitochondrial function → poor prognosis
- Chr 9p/10q loss → immune cold subtype → immune evasion
- P-II subtype: high CIN + immune cold + increased invasiveness
- RPRD1B proposed as therapeutic target for radiotherapy-resistant patients
- Longitudinal design (primary + recurrent) enables molecular evolution analysis

## Project relevance
- CIN scoring as a prognostic biomarker methodology applicable to other rare cancers
- Immune cold subtype characterization relevant to immunotherapy response prediction
- Radiotherapy resistance target identification (RPRD1B) is a drug repurposing reference case
- Paired primary/recurrent samples provide insight into treatment-induced molecular changes

## Caveats
- Rare tumor type; direct cross-cancer generalization requires caution
- Radiotherapy effects may confound CIN interpretation in recurrent tumors
- RPRD1B proposed as target without comprehensive functional validation
- Surgical outcome variable (GTR vs IR) may confound prognostic analyses
