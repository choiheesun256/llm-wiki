## Summary

Edwards et al. (2008) demonstrate that BRCA2-deficient cancer cells acquire resistance to PARP inhibitors through secondary intragenic deletions in BRCA2 that remove the original c.6174delT frameshift mutation and restore the open reading frame. Using the CAPAN1 pancreatic cancer cell line (which is BRCA2-null due to the c.6174delT founder mutation) and 12 independently derived PARP-inhibitor-resistant (PIR) clones, they characterize four classes of intragenic BRCA2 deletions (458 bp to ~58 kb) that produce near-full-length BRCA2 isoforms retaining functional homologous recombination (HR). These ORF-restoring mutations explain resistance to both PARP inhibitors and the platinum crosslinker cisplatin, but not to docetaxel — consistent with an HR-dependent mechanism. Critically, analogous ORF-restoring secondary deletions are identified in carboplatin-resistant ovarian tumours from two patients with germline BRCA2 c.6174delT mutations, translating the cell-line findings to clinical relevance. Most deletions are flanked by short regions of microhomology, implicating error-prone repair pathways (MMEJ/SSA) — ironically driven by the very HR deficiency that defines BRCA2-mutant tumours — as the source of resistance-conferring mutations.

## Key Contributions

1. **Mechanism of PARP inhibitor resistance via ORF restoration**: Secondary intragenic deletions in BRCA2 eliminate the c.6174delT frameshift and restore HR competence, providing a novel and clinically relevant resistance mechanism distinct from epigenetic silencing or drug efflux.

2. **Four structural classes of resistance mutations**: BRCA2ΔA (58,545 bp, n=5 clones), BRCA2ΔB (58,614 bp, n=1), BRCA2ΔC (39,898 bp, n=5), BRCA2ΔD (458 bp, n=1). All remove c.6174delT; the smallest (ΔD/PIR12) deletes only 153 amino acids, yielding a near-complete protein.

3. **Cross-resistance to platinum but not taxanes**: PIR clones resist cisplatin (also an HR-repaired lesion inducer) but remain sensitive to docetaxel, demonstrating selectivity consistent with restored HR rather than broad multidrug resistance.

4. **Clinical validation in patient tumours**: ORF-restoring BRCA2 deletions found in carboplatin-resistant ovarian tumours (UK1999: 137-bp deletion; UK2223: 8-bp deletion) from two BRCA2 c.6174delT heterozygous patients, establishing the in vivo relevance.

5. **Truncated BRCA2 isoforms are HR-competent despite domain loss**: New isoforms lacking BRC repeats 6–8 and portions of the DNA-binding domain still rescue HR, revealing unexpected functional redundancy and redefining the minimal functional unit of BRCA2 for HR.

6. **Ironical mutagenesis**: The deletions likely arise from error-prone repair processes (SSA, MMEJ) that are upregulated in HR-deficient cells — meaning BRCA2 deficiency itself generates the mutations that overcome BRCA2 deficiency.

## Methodology and Architecture

**Experimental system**
- CAPAN1 (BRCA2 c.6174delT homozygous pancreatic cancer) as the BRCA2-null model; BxPC3 (BRCA2 wild-type) as control.
- Twelve PIR subclones generated by two parallel protocols: stepwise KU0058948 dose escalation (PIR1–6) or continuous 100 nM treatment (PIR7–12) over 15 days per passage.

**Drug sensitivity**
- SRB colorimetric assay over 15 days; SF50 (50% surviving fraction) calculated by four-parameter logistic regression.
- Three drugs tested: KU0058948 (PARP inhibitor), cisplatin (platinum crosslinker), docetaxel (microtubule stabilizer).

**HR functional assays**
- RAD51 foci: cells exposed to 10 Gy X-rays or 10 µM KU0058948 (24 h); immunofluorescence for RAD51; >5 foci threshold; >100 cells scored per condition.
- I-SceI DR-GFP reporter: VC8-DR-GFP BRCA2-deficient hamster cells; BRCA2 constructs transfected, ISceI-expressing vector co-transfected; GFP+ fraction measured by flow cytometry at 72 h.
- gamma-H2AX foci: to confirm equivalent DSB induction across cell lines, decoupling damage from repair.

**Chromosomal instability**
- MMC (0.05 µg/ml, 16 h) treatment; 50 DAPI-stained metaphase spreads per cell line scored for gaps, breaks, triradials, quadriradials, dicentrics, tricentrics (blinded to cell line identity).

**BRCA2 characterization**
- Western blot/IP with N-terminal (Ab-1, residues 1651–1821) and C-terminal (Ab-2, residues 3245–3418) antibodies to map new isoform termini.
- Subcellular fractionation (ProteoExtract kit) to assess nuclear localization.
- PCR of cDNA and gDNA spanning BRCA2 exons 11–27; Sanger sequencing to map deletion boundaries; coordinates referenced to GenBank AY436640.
- CGH + single-colour BRCA2 FISH (BAC RP11-37E23) to determine copy number.

**siRNA and reconstitution**
- BRCA2 siRNA (Dharmacon) knockdown in PIR clones; phenotype (KU0058948 sensitivity, RAD51 foci) assessed 48 h post-transfection.
- WT, p6174delT, pPIR1, pPIR12 constructs in pcDNA3.1 transfected into CAPAN1; G418 selection; tested in 15-day survival assay and DR-GFP assay.

**Patient samples**
- Frozen ovarian tumour tissue from UK1999 (stage IIIC, carboplatin-taxol resistant) and UK2223 (stage IIIB, paclitaxel-carboplatin resistant); laser capture microdissected; BRCA2 exon 11 amplified and sequenced (primers B2Ex11F4/R1, B2Ex11F3/B2Ex24R2, B2Ex11F4/B2Ex11R1).

## Results

|---------|--------|---------|-------|
| SF50 KU0058948 | 5.75 nM | 7.35 µM (1,370-fold) | 7.35 µM |
| RAD51 foci (X-ray) | Deficient | Restored | Partial |
| MMC chromosomal aberrations/cell | 2.44 ± 1.88 | 1.08 ± 1.25 (P < 0.0001) | — |
| BRCA2 ORF | Frameshift (c.6174delT) | Restored (ΔA/B/C) | Restored (ΔD) |
| HR (DR-GFP, vs. pcDNA3.1) | — | ~4-fold (pPIR1) | ~4-fold (pPIR12) |
| Cisplatin resistance | Sensitive | Resistant | Resistant |
| Docetaxel resistance | Sensitive | Sensitive | Sensitive |

**Deletion characteristics**

All four deletion classes share these properties: (1) removal of c.6174delT frameshift, (2) in-frame ORF restoration, (3) retention of N-terminal BRCA2 epitopes (present in c.6174delT product) and C-terminal epitopes (absent from c.6174delT product), (4) nuclear translocation, (5) RAD51 interaction. Deletions are flanked by 1–6 bp microhomologies in nearly all cases.

**Protein domain consequences**: BRCA2ΔA and ΔB remove BRC repeats 1–8, most of the proposed DBD, TR2, and NLS — yet these isoforms retain HR activity, suggesting the retained N-terminal region suffices for RAD51 loading in this context. BRCA2ΔC removes BRC repeats 6–8. BRCA2ΔD (PIR12) removes only 153 aa, yielding a near-full-length protein with two BRC repeats lost.

**Patient tumours**: UK1999 BRCA2 ORF restored by a 137-bp deletion flanked by two 6-bp direct repeats (exon 11); protein loses 46 aa. UK2223 restored by an 8-bp deletion flanked by a 6-bp direct repeat; protein loses 3 aa. Both tumours had expressed near-full-length BRCA2 with TR2/NLS at time of carboplatin resistance.

- **Frakkila et al. (2021)** — Heterogeneity of clonal evolution in acquired PARP inhibitor resistance (later clinical corroboration of reversion mutations).
