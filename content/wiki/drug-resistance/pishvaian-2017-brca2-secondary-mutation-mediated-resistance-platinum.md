## Summary

This short communication reports the first clinical case of a BRCA2 reversion (secondary somatic) mutation conferring resistance to PARP inhibitor-based therapy in a pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDA) patient. A 63-year-old woman with a germline BRCA2 frameshift mutation (c.6486_6489delACAA, p.K2162fs*5) initially had a near-complete response to a combination of veliparib, 5-FU, and oxaliplatin. Upon disease recurrence, next-generation sequencing of the recurrent tumor revealed a secondary somatic BRCA2 deletion (c.6448_6473del, p.K2150fs*17) located 13 bases upstream of the germline deletion. This 26-bp deletion restored the BRCA2 reading frame, likely recovering homologous recombination (HR) repair function and thereby enabling the tumor to escape PARP inhibitor-mediated synthetic lethality. The resistant clone appeared as an eccentric outgrowth from the original pancreatic mass, consistent with clonal selection under therapeutic pressure.

## Key Contributions

- **First PDA case of BRCA2 reversion resistance:** Established that the secondary mutation-driven PARP inhibitor resistance mechanism, previously documented in ovarian and breast cancer, also operates in pancreatic cancer.
- **Mechanism confirmation:** A 26-bp somatic intragenic deletion upstream of the germline frameshift restored the BRCA2 open reading frame, providing a molecular explanation for loss of PARP inhibitor sensitivity.
- **Clinical relevance of clonal dynamics:** The secondary mutation was detected at only ~8% MAF in the resected specimen, while the germline allele frequency was ~52%, indicating resistance arose from a minor sub-clone that underwent positive selection.
- **Rationale for serial biopsy and broad NGS:** Demonstrates that panel-based NGS of pre- and post-treatment biopsies can identify reversion mutations and that whole-genome sequencing may be required to detect all such events.

## Methodology and Architecture

**Patient and clinical course:**
- Germline BRCA2 mutation confirmed by Myriad Genetics (buccal swab, 2010).
- Enrolled in Phase I/II trial: veliparib (200 mg orally twice daily) + modified FOLFOX6 (oxaliplatin 85 mg/m² Day 1; 5-FU 2400 mg/m² continuous infusion Days 1–3) every 2 weeks (NCT01489865).
- Near-complete response by March 2015 (15 months progression-free survival).
- Recurrence detected May 2015; surgery September 2015; post-operative maintenance with olaparib (400 → 200 mg twice daily).
- Disease progressed to peritoneal carcinomatosis by January 2016.

**Molecular profiling (Foundation ONE NGS panel):**
- Pre-treatment liver lesion: germline BRCA2 (c.6486_6489delACAA, MAF ~61%), KRAS G12V, TP53 P295fs*50.
- Post-treatment pancreatic resection: same primary mutations + new secondary BRCA2 somatic deletion (c.6448_6473del, MAF ~8%), germline BRCA2 MAF reduced to ~52%.

**Mechanism (Figure 2):**
- Germline deletion: 4-bp deletion in exon 11 → premature STOP codon (p.K2162fs*5).
- Secondary 26-bp somatic deletion located 13 bp upstream → when present together with the germline deletion, restores the reading frame, allowing read-through and functional BRCA2 protein production.

## Results

| Parameter | Outcome |
|-----------|---------|
| Initial response to veliparib + FOLFOX | Near-complete radiographic response (liver lesion disappeared; pancreatic mass barely visible by March 2015) |
| Progression-free survival on trial | ~15 months |
| Recurrence pattern | Eccentric outgrowth from original pancreatic mass from May 2015; consistent with independent resistant clone |
| Secondary BRCA2 mutation MAF | ~8% in resection specimen |
| Germline BRCA2 MAF in resection | ~52% (reduced from ~61% pre-treatment) |
| Functional consequence of secondary mutation | Restored BRCA2 reading frame → likely restored HR repair → PARP inhibitor resistance |
| Clinical outcome | Peritoneal carcinomatosis Jan 2016; enrolled immunotherapy trial; death 24 May 2016 |

The secondary mutation's low MAF indicates it was present in a small but positively selected resistant sub-clone. The eccentric morphology of the recurrent mass on imaging was retrospectively interpreted as evidence of clonal outgrowth of a pre-existing resistant clone from the tumor periphery.
