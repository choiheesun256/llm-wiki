## Summary

This 2008 Nature Letter by Sakai et al. establishes that **secondary intragenic mutations in BRCA2** — frameshift-correcting mutations arising within an already-mutated BRCA2 allele — are a major mechanism by which BRCA2-mutated cancers acquire resistance to cisplatin (and cross-resistance to PARP inhibitors). The authors demonstrate this mechanism using a cisplatin-resistant breast cancer cell line (HCC1428), in vitro cisplatin selection of pancreatic cancer cells (Capan-1), and genetic analysis of recurrent platinum-resistant ovarian tumors from patients. The findings are clinically significant because tumors harboring secondary BRCA2 mutations become resistant to both platinum agents and PARP inhibitors simultaneously, limiting therapeutic options. The mechanism is analogous to BCR-ABL secondary mutations that confer imatinib resistance in leukemia.

## Key Contributions

1. **Secondary BRCA2 mutations restore wild-type reading frame**: In cisplatin-resistant BRCA2-mutated cancer cells, intragenic secondary mutations (small indels near the original frameshift site) correct the frameshift and restore near-full-length, functional BRCA2 protein.
2. **In vitro selection recapitulates clinical resistance**: Cisplatin selection of Capan-1 (BRCA2.6174delT) yielded 14 resistant clones; 7 had secondary BRCA2 mutations with restored BRCA2 expression. Five distinct secondary mutation types were identified, showing convergent evolution toward frame restoration.
3. **Restored BRCA2 restores HR and confers cross-resistance to PARP inhibitors**: BRCA2-restored clones show improved RAD51 foci formation, functional HR (DR-GFP assay), and are resistant to PARP inhibitor AG14361 — unlike clones that acquired cisplatin resistance through other mechanisms.
4. **In vivo evidence from patient tumors**: One patient's recurrent platinum-resistant ovarian carcinoma (UW3548) showed genetic reversion of BRCA2.6174delT in the tumor, confirming secondary mutations operate clinically.
5. **Mechanistic analogy to imatinib resistance**: Just as BCR-ABL mutations restore kinase activity to circumvent imatinib, secondary BRCA2 mutations restore HR to circumvent platinum/PARP inhibitor sensitivity.

## Methodology

### Experimental Models
- **HCC1428**: Cisplatin-resistant breast cancer cell line with BRCA2 6174delT primary mutation; harbors a spontaneous 2,135-bp homozygous deletion activating cryptic splice sites in exon 11, generating two in-frame BRCA2 transcripts encoding truncated but partially functional BRCA2 proteins.
- **Capan-1**: Pancreatic cancer cell line, homozygous BRCA2 6174delT; used for in vitro cisplatin selection (12 million cells, 2 µM cisplatin, 5–6 weeks) to generate 14 resistant clones.
- Five patients' recurrent BRCA2-mutated ovarian carcinoma specimens analyzed post-platinum treatment.

### Key Assays
| Assay | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Sanger sequencing (genomic DNA + cDNA) | Identify primary and secondary BRCA2 mutations |
| Western blotting (Ab-1, Ab-2) | Detect BRCA2 protein isoforms (middle vs. C-terminus) |
| FISH | BRCA2 copy number in Capan-1 |
| RAD51 foci (post-IR) | Measure HR competency in resistant clones |
| DR-GFP HR reporter assay | Quantify HR efficiency of novel BRCA2 constructs |
| XTT / crystal violet cytotoxicity | Cisplatin and PARP inhibitor (AG14361) sensitivity |
| siRNA knockdown (BRCA2, FANCA) | Confirm causal role of BRCA2 restoration |
| SNP/LOH analysis | Detect genetic reversion in patient tumor DNA |

## Results

### HCC1428 — A Naturally Cisplatin-Resistant BRCA2-Mutated Cell Line
- A 2,135-bp deletion in HCC1428 activates two cryptic exon 11 splice donor sites, producing two BRCA2 isoforms that lack internal BRC repeats but retain the C-terminal ssDNA-binding domain and NLSs.
- These truncated BRCA2 proteins restore sufficient HR function for cisplatin resistance; siRNA depletion of BRCA2 or FANCA re-sensitizes cells to cisplatin.

### Capan-1 In Vitro Selection — Five Types of Secondary Mutations
- 7/14 cisplatin-resistant Capan-1 clones restored near-full-length BRCA2 protein via secondary intragenic mutations in exon 11 near the 6174delT site.
- Secondary mutations included small deletions, insertions, and complex indels — each individually correcting the 6174delT frameshift.
- Secondary mutations occurred on only one copy of the duplicated mutant BRCA2 allele.
- All 7 BRCA2-restored clones showed improved RAD51 foci formation (HR restoration) and resistance to PARP inhibitor.
- 7 clones lacking secondary BRCA2 mutations retained PARP inhibitor sensitivity, indicating they used alternative resistance mechanisms.

### Functional Restoration of Homologous Recombination
- DR-GFP assay: all tested secondary-mutation constructs produced GFP frequencies ≥ wild-type BRCA2 construct, confirming functional HR restoration.
- Ionizing radiation-induced RAD51 foci recovery was statistically significant in 6/7 BRCA2-restored clones (p < 0.05 vs. parental Capan-1).

### Clinical Specimen — UW3548 Ovarian Carcinoma
- Constitutional DNA: heterozygous 6174delT + two SNPs (IVS21-66C/T, IVS25-299G/C).
- Recurrent tumor: SNPs showed LOH (hemizygous), but both 6174delT and wild-type BRCA2 sequences were detected at the mutation locus — evidence of genetic reversion after duplication of the mutant allele.
- Interpretation: duplication of the 6174delT allele followed by intragenic reversion of one copy restored wild-type BRCA2.

### Cross-Resistance Implications
- Because secondary BRCA2 mutations restore HR, tumors acquiring this resistance mechanism become refractory to both platinum chemotherapy and PARP inhibitors simultaneously.
- Platinum-resistant clones without secondary BRCA2 mutations remain PARP inhibitor-sensitive, suggesting PARP inhibitors may still be effective in this subset.
