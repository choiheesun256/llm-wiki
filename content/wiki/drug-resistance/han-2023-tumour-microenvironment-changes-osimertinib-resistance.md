## Summary

Han et al. (2023) investigate how the tumour microenvironment (TME) is remodelled when NSCLC patients develop acquired resistance to osimertinib (third-generation EGFR-TKI). Using RNA-seq-based immune infiltration analysis from clinical biopsies combined with in vitro co-culture experiments on an osimertinib-resistant cell line (H1975OR), the study demonstrates that resistance is accompanied by a shift toward an immunosuppressive TME: decreased CD8+ T cell infiltration, increased M0/M2 macrophage prevalence, and T cell exhaustion. Critically, the study shows that this immunosuppressive remodelling is mediated by tumour-derived exosomes (TDEs) rather than soluble conditioned medium, with H1975OR exosomes carrying miR-1258-3p and miR-17-5p driving M0 macrophage polarisation toward the pro-tumorigenic M2 phenotype. M2 macrophages in turn attenuate the inhibitory effect on tumour cell proliferation. The work positions TDEs as key intercellular communication vehicles in osimertinib resistance and the TME as a tractable target for overcoming resistance.

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive characterisation of TME immune landscape at osimertinib resistance using both clinical RNA-seq (TIMER 2.0 / CIBERSORT / xCell) and in vitro functional experiments.
- Quantitative demonstration that immune score, microenvironment score, and CD8+ T cell infiltration all decrease significantly at osimertinib resistance, while M0 macrophage infiltration increases.
- Establishment of the H1975OR cell line (IC50 = 1686 nM, 116-fold increase over parental H1975) as a model for acquired osimertinib resistance with unknown genomic mechanisms.
- Mechanistic finding that TDEs from resistant cells — not conditioned medium — drive M0-to-M2 macrophage polarisation, which then reduces anti-tumour macrophage function.
- Identification of miR-1258-3p and miR-17-5p as key upregulated miRNAs in H1975OR exosomes, alongside proteomics showing upregulation of ECM/TGF-beta/PI3K-Akt-related proteins (COL6A1, ACLY, TLN1).
- Demonstration that H1975OR TDEs inhibit late-activation of CD4+ T effector cells and increase CD4+ TEFF exhaustion, but do not suppress T-cell-mediated tumour killing capacity per se.
- Evidence that pre-activated PBMCs kill osimertinib-resistant tumour cells more potently, suggesting immunotherapy activation strategies may overcome resistance-associated immune evasion.

## Methodology and Architecture

### Clinical Data
- Paired biopsy specimens: 4 baseline (BL, pre-osimertinib) + 5 progressive disease (PD, at resistance) from Shanghai Pulmonary Hospital, plus public dataset of 12 samples (3 BL + 9 PD).
- Immune infiltration estimated via TIMER 2.0 using xCell (immune/microenvironment score) and CIBERSORT (22 immune cell fractions by LM22 signature matrix).

### In Vitro Resistance Model
- H1975OR generated by stepwise osimertinib dose escalation to 1 µM; confirmed by CCK8 (IC50), apoptosis (flow cytometry, Annexin V/PI), migration (Transwell), and Western blot (EGFR/pEGFR).
- Exosomes isolated by serial ultracentrifugation (110,000 g); characterised by TEM, NTA, and TSG001/Alix/HSP70 markers.

### Immune Cell Co-culture Experiments
- THP-1-derived M0 macrophages stimulated with S-EXO, R-EXO, S-CM, or R-CM; polarisation assessed by flow cytometry (CD45/CD11b/CD86/CD206/CD68) and morphology.
- Macrophage conditioned medium (CM) applied to tumour cells; proliferation monitored in real time by RTCA (xCELLigence).
- PBMCs from 3 donors co-cultured with tumour cells or CMs/exosomes; T cell phenotyping by multi-colour flow cytometry (CD62L/CD45RA/CD69/CD25/HLA-DR/PD1 markers).
- PBMC tumour-killing assay: IL-2/anti-CD3 pre-activation; RTCA readout at E:T ratios of 10:1 and 20:1.

### Exosome Multi-omics
- miRNA-seq (Illumina Hiseq2000, 1×50bp) with GO/KEGG enrichment and TargetScan target prediction.
- Label-free proteomics (Q Exactive Plus); GO/KEGG/PPI network analysis.

## Results

| Finding | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Immune score at resistance | Significantly decreased (P < 0.001) |
| Microenvironment score | Significantly decreased (P < 0.001) |
| CD8+ T cells (CIBERSORT) | Significantly reduced at PD (P < 0.05) |
| M0 macrophages | Significantly increased at PD (P < 0.01) |
| M2 macrophage trend | Increasing (not significant) |
| H1975OR IC50 | 1686 nM (116-fold vs parental H1975) |
| M0-to-M2 driver | R-EXO (not R-CM) — exosome-specific effect |
| M1 macrophage CM | Strongest tumour proliferation inhibition |
| M2 macrophage CM | Attenuated tumour inhibitory effect |
| CD4+ TEFF late-activation | Significantly reduced by R-EXO (P < 0.05 vs control; P < 0.001 vs R-CM) |
| CD4+ exhausted TEFF | Increased by R-EXO and tumour co-culture |
| T-cell tumour killing | Not suppressed by TDEs; pre-activated PBMCs more potent on H1975OR |
| miRNAs upregulated in R-EXO | miR-1258-3p and miR-17-5p (P < 0.01) |
| Proteins upregulated in R-EXO | COL6A1, ACLY, TLN1 |
| Proteins downregulated in R-EXO | LGALS1, CLIC1, ITGA3 |
| Enriched pathways in R-EXO proteins | ECM-receptor interaction, PI3K-Akt, TGF-beta, Wnt, SCLC pathway |
